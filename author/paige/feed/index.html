Lacey and Brandon tied the knot with an undeniably chic, southern wedding at The Bridge Building.

This sweet pair first met by chance at the gym they both frequent. Their first date on the rooftop of The George Jones marked the start of forever. Fast forward, and they found themselves back on that same rooftop as Brandon popped the question on a rainy afternoon.

For their wedding, the overall experience of the day was most important to this couple. Plus a little sunshine! Above all, they wanted their family and friends to have fun and enjoy some good, southern food.

They opted for black and white with metallic accents of gold and silver. Since they aren't big on flowers, stunning, oversized magnolias were used throughout the day. From the bouquets to the captivating floral ring suspended above the center of the space.

Similarly, candles in modern glass cylinders of varying heights were placed down the aisle, at the altar and around the reception for ambiance and romance.

A sweet ceremony in the Cumberland space was followed by cocktail hour on the riverfront with refreshing drinks at the ready for sipping.

Shortly after, dinner was served buffet-style. Entree selections included hickory-smoked pulled pork and honey balsamic chicken, served with four cheese macaroni, roasted brussels sprouts, and roasted corn and charred tomato risotto.

After dinner, guests made their way to the Observatory for dancing on the rooftop.

And after a bundtini or two for dessert, guests sent the couple off through a neon glow stick tunnel in celebration of their union.

Congratulations to Lacey and Brandon!

With a bright white palette and plenty of candles, white bridesmaids dresses and dinner on the riverfront, Neysha and Joel's modern Southern wedding reception at The Bridge Building was reflective of their vision.

This sweet couple's biggest priority was for their guests to experience Music City in a chic, urban environment on their special day.

Strikingly contemporary and beautifully captured by Parker Young, the day was ultra-stylish from start to finish with a little Southern flair.

After a heartwarming ceremony at Scarritt Bennett, guests made their way to The Bridge Building to continue the celebration.

Self-proclaimed foodies, this pair went for Infinity's Southern favorites for a delicious Southern-inspired menu.

During a sun-soaked cocktail hour on the riverfront patio, guests noshed on candied bacon and french fry shooters with blackberry ketchup. They also had custom cocktail napkins foil-printed with fun facts about the couple! Such a fun idea.

We loved that Neysha and Joel chose to host their wedding outside on the patio! A chic farm table overlooking the river was the perfect sweetheart table for the newlyweds.

Dinner began with grilled peach salads and biscuits with honey butter. Mouthwatering entrees included hot chicken and house hickory-smoked pulled pork with garlic mustard greens and four-cheese macaroni.

Neysha and Joel are such a fun couple and madly in love. It was a joy working with them and seeing them so happy having a great time dancing and celebrating with their family and friends.

We helped Mary Keith and Austin kick off their wedding festivities with a sweet, blue summer rehearsal dinner at The Bell Tower.

The couple brought the summer sky inside The Bell Tower by pairing its classic and historic lines with a fun and inviting color palette of Carolina blue and white. A fresh, mini baguette was placed at each setting accompanied by whimsical hand calligraphed name cards by White Ink Calligraphy.

For dinner, guests were treated to chef-carved slow roasted prime rib with grilled squash, zucchini wedges, and rosemary roasted new potatoes. After dinner guests enjoyed cheesecake with fresh strawberries for dessert. And the party didn't stop there!

Mary Keith and Austin were joined by a hundred more of their guests as their after party began. Guests snacked on a late night bite of delicious beef sliders and danced the night away to celebrate this wonderful couple and their upcoming nuptials.

Tracey and Eddie said "I do" under an arch of fresh greenery at The Bell Tower. From ribbon wands to table linens, their southern wedding was navy and blush from head to toe. This is one of our favorite color palettes. The contrasting shades provide that perfect balance of masculine and feminine.

This couple really embraced the south, encouraging their guests to "take a taste of Tennessee." From southern bites of fried mac and cheese, hot chicken sliders, and candied bacon deviled eggs to GooGoo Clusters and MoonPies as parting gifts. This was a southern wedding at its finest.

Following the ceremony and cocktail hour, everyone was treated to a plate dinner. Guests had the option of house smoked brisket with grilled asparagus and cheddar and chive smashed potatoes or maple mustard glazed chicken with southern style green beans and rosemary roasted new potatoes.
