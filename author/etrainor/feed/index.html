Kristen and Vincent had a classic ceremony and reception filled with modern boho florals and neutral, white, and gold details.

After meeting at UT Knoxville as freshmen in college, these two later moved to Charlotte, NC. While driving back to visit family and friends in Nashville, they planned a full night in Knoxville to break up the drive. Little did Kristen know, Vincent had plans of his own. He proposed at a beautiful rooftop hotel, surrounded by roses with their sweet pup Henry by their side. Once they arrived in Nashville the next morning, Kristen was surprised with an engagement party full of their closest friends and family. So sweet!

When it came to choosing their venue, Kristen and Vincent loved the traditional wedding feel The Bell Tower has, as well as how close it is to Broadway.

On her big day, Kristen stunned in a modern, long sleeve mermaid gown with a deep V-neckline. Her hair was styled in a classic low bun with curls to frame her face, and she held a beautiful freeform style bouquet of white florals and greenery. And to top it off, she wore a chapel-length veil adorned with pearls.

Kristen's bridesmaids wore custom matching gowns while carrying posy versions of her bridal bouquet, while Vincent and his dapper groomsmen sported matching black tuxedos with white boutonnieres.

When it came to the design, Kristen wanted a mix of classic, modern, and boho. We have to say, we think we nailed it with the help of The Florista. The ceremony was set with mahogany Chiavari chairs and large floral arrangements lining the aisle, full of white blooms, pampas grass, and abundant greenery. A beautiful hanging floral installation was suspended above the altar, creating a stunning focal point, while The Bell Tower’s staircase was dressed in the dreamiest greenery to tie the room together.

On the patio, Kristen and Vincent achieved the perfect mix of classic, modern, and boho by setting the space with a classic white bar, a large floral arrangement, a custom neon sign, and a modern lounge area with an Insta-worthy white floral backdrop. 

The reception was filled with neutral, white, and gold details. The king's table was lined with greenery garlands, dreamy taper candles in gold candlesticks, and centerpieces that repurposed the bridesmaids’ bouquets to add more depth and texture. The rest of the room was filled with round tables dressed in white linens that had a mix of classic floral centerpieces in varying heights, some with modern gold stands, and pillar candles at the center. They also chose a beautiful white facade and abundant white floral arrangements to decorate the live band’s stage.

For their first dance, Kristen and Justin chose “All Yours” by Tyler Childers. “We have both always loved Tyler and would listen to all his vinyls on our record players,” Kristen told us.

They also chose to share a final dance at the end of the night, giving them a moment to take in the venue and “look all around and reflect on the amazing night that just happened with all our favorite people.”

The happy newlyweds finished the night with a fun exit as guests lined the sidewalk and waved streamers. Scroll on to see all the beautiful details!

Meg and Will said their “I dos” with a stunning ceremony between the grand arched windows at The Bell Tower and a reception decorated with gorgeous drapery and chandeliers. 

The couple shares a modern day love story of meeting on a dating app, hitting it off from the beginning, and knowing they had something special!

On their wedding day, Meg donned a Pronovias dress with a gorgeous cathedral train. She wore a beaded hair piece, elegant updo, and held a classic, long stem, white rose bouquet. Her bridesmaids wore matching  velvet gowns in a beautiful deep red. Will looked dashing in his military uniform, and their pup, who was a special part of their day, sported a classic black tux!

The main space at The Bell Tower was decorated with soft white drapery set between the windows, several glamorous chandeliers hanging from above, and dark chiavari chairs facing the windows, creating the most romantic ambiance.  The grand staircase was adorned with more white drapery and floral arrangements that matched Meg’s bouquet.

The space was later flipped for the reception and filled with round tables and a classic king’s table for the wedding party under the grand windows. The color palette of champagne and blush was incorporated into every aspect of their setup with the beautiful and elevated floral arrangements set on white linens with champagne-colored napkins and candles to create a warm glow.  As a special touch, Meg and Will’s  names shined in white lights against the wall with a custom gobo from Nashville Lighting and Production.

With it being a December wedding, the couple incorporated special holiday details to bring in the magic of the season. They asked guests to write their well wishes on red ornaments that were then hung on a Christmas tree— a cute alternative option to the traditional guest book!  

Meg and Will then shared their first dance to “Something About the Way You Look Tonight” by Elton John—a special song, as Elton John was their first concert together. Guests then gathered to dance the night away, and that they surely did!

Anna Caroline and Matthew celebrated their nuptials with a dreamy rooftop ceremony and trendy reception at the Bridge Building, full of disco balls, baby’s breath arrangements, and celestial vibes with a retro style. 

These lovebirds grew up together from preschool to high school and only started dating once they got to college in 2014, and the rest is history! Later on, Matthew got down on one knee and proposed in April 2020 on the Pedestrian Bridge. 

Since the Bridge Building is connected to the bridge, their venue choice seemed almost a given. Anna Caroline later told us, “I toured about 4 to 5 venues before coming to the Bridge Building and knew immediately it was the one. The view and convenience of being downtown was too perfect for us!” 

It was important to the pair that their guests ate, drank, and danced the night away! What more could you ask for?

On the big day, the rooftop was set with white drapery, free-standing baby’s breath arrangements at the altar, and hanging disco balls. Anna walked the aisle in a fitted mermaid gown and perfect bridal nails detailed with pearls, while her bridesmaids dressed in a mix of rust-colored and beige dresses in different styles.

The pair shared a sweet first look before the ceremony, but that didn’t stop the tears from flowing during the “I dos.” Afterwards, guests made their way to the riverfront to sip on some Pinot Grigio as the dinner stations were set in the main space. Stations included Mexican street corn, gourmet french fries, a mix of salads, and all the fixings. 

Tables were decorated with silky linens, black taper candles, pink roses, and bundles of baby’s breath with silver stars intertwined for a celestial look to complete their on-trend and stylish design. And the rooftop turned into a retro vibe with disco balls and colorful lighting to set the mood. 

Guests signed vinyl records and snagged a photo in front of a wall of silver streamers, disco balls, and a custom LED sign of Anna Caroline and Matthew’s newly-shared last name as they made their way to the dance floor. Custom cakes, one to look like a 6-pack of Miller Lite beer and one adorned in pearls, created by Crazi Cakes were ready to be served.

Before the night came to an end, Anna Caroline donned a silver cowboy hat and tulle gloves embroidered with the phrases “to have and to hold" on one hand and “to love and to cherish” on the other. She then danced the night way with her new husband Matthew and all their loved ones!
