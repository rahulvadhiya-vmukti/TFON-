Baylor University’s president Linda A. Livingstone hosted the “Give Light” fundraising campaign at The Bell Tower with an industrial style, greenery decor, and loads of personal touches. 

In the main space, a stage was set for live performances and stories by Baylor alumni, rising songwriter Trannie S. Anderson, CMA Chairman and songwriter Jim Beavers, and Grammy Award-winning songwriter and producer Brett James. Rustic, industrial Renaissance chandeliers hung from above, illuminating the dark wood farmhouse tables below. They were decorated with a mix of greenery runners and small vases with dainty arrangements of white flowers and more greenery, as well as votive candles for a soft glow.

For that personalized touch, the venue’s beloved arch windows were adorned with faux stained glass made by AMP, and the Baylor University logo was projected in the covered patio space by Nashville Lighting and Production.

Lounge areas were also set on the patio with chic cowhide rugs, modern leather sofas, and simple centerpieces of white florals and greenery in gold vases. Smaller versions of the Renaissance chandeliers hung from above, wrapped in magnolia leaves to create a cohesive look.

There were drink stations placed on the patio and the in-house chefs served passed apps of mini chicken and waffles, arancini, mini beef wellingtons, and individual pre-set charcuteries! 

Dinner stations were later displayed with slider selections consisting of beef tenderloin, caprese, crab cakes, and Southern comforts. But that’s not all! Later, desserts of miniature pecan pies, chocolate mousse pies, and cherry pies were set out for guests.

Catie and Brendan’s wedding celebration at The Bell Tower was filled with fresh blue accents, gold details, and a mix of classic and modern decor. 

The couple met at the University of Kentucky in Lexington through mutual friends at a football tailgate, bonding over their love for Kentucky basketball. Catie agreed to a group date with their friends— and the rest is history. 

Brendan proposed in Frankfurt at the Lovers Leap winery, surrounded by family and friends. To celebrate, the couple shared their first bottle of wine together! 

When it came to the big day, Catie and Brendan knew they wanted to incorporate their alma mater’s colors of blue and white. We think it turned out beautifully! 

The wedding began with a beautiful ceremony in the main space, which was decorated with varying gold geometric pendant lights and greenery. The ceremony arch was a modern gold rectangle with greenery and a touch of white flowers.

After they were pronounced husband and wife, the celebration continued to our patio for cocktail hour. The space was decorated with light blue linens accented with white that tied into a green boxwood photo wall decorated with a white balloon arch and custom neon sign. 

While guests mingled over cocktails and hors d'oeuvres, Infinity staff flipped the main space and mezzanine for the reception. Tables were set  with crisp white linens, beautiful blue floral napkins, candles, and gold-framed table numbers. Some featured arrangements of greenery and white flowers in tall gold stands, while others were set with greenery surrounding the candles.

At the end of the night, they played “Dixieland Delight” as the couple went around to say goodbye to the crowd. It was a very fun and memorable evening for all.

Cassidy and Darius celebrated their nuptials with a dreamy rooftop ceremony and reception full of romantic ambiance at The Bridge Building. 

These Nashville natives met the modern way, through an app called Tinder, and the rest was history! Later on, Darius privately reserved the gardens behind the Cheekwood mansion and got down on one knee. 

It was important to these two that everyone had fun on the big day, and being from Nashville, Cassidy knew she wanted to step away from any “country” style and focus more on a modern vibe.

“I wanted a modern, simple, romantic venue with lots of natural light, and I knew it was The Bridge Building the second I stepped into the space,” she explained. 

Opting out of a first look, this pair first locked eyes as Cassidy walked down the aisle to her now husband, Darius, who was standing in front of a floral arch full of white blooms and greenery.

“We had eyes only for each other and felt the whole ceremony was authentically us,” Cassidy shared. Not to mention, an unbeatable view of downtown Nashville just across the river! 

Afterwards, guests made their way to the riverfront to snack on passed appetizers of roasted fig and goat cheese flatbread, hot chicken slider, candied bacon, pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, crispy herbed risotto balls, and fried green tomatoes. 

Once they found their seats in the main space, decorated with loads of greenery and romantic candle lighting, a dinner service of plated maple mustard glazed chicken with grilled asparagus, roasted corn, and charred tomato risotto, began. 

Before the night came to an end with a fun bubble exit and custom cookies, Cassidy and Darius shared a first dance to “The Very Thought Of You” by Michael Buble.

Wanting to show off their new city, it was important that the venue be close to downtown, and The Bridge Building was the perfect place.
