Tala and Vagif said their “I dos” at the Estate at Cherokee Dock with a stunning outdoor ceremony in the Serenity Gardens followed by a beautiful seated dinner in the Lakeview Event Center. 

These two first met during their college years and eventually fell in love. Vagif proposed at Nashville’s Love Circle, a romantic spot that overlooks the entire city. After surprising Tala with a sweet picnic, she said yes!

When it came to planning their wedding, it was important for the couple that their day flowed flawlessly so their only focus could be on sitting back, relaxing, and taking it all in. Accomplishment achieved, if we say so ourselves! Their day went off without a hitch.

Tala stunned in a sweetheart mermaid gown with draped off-the-shoulder sleeves and embroidered lace details. She accessorized with a jeweled hair piece, dainty tennis necklace, and a waltz length veil. Her bridesmaids donned champagne floor-length gowns in different styles, and all the ladies carried matching long-stem bouquets of classic white florals and greenery. Vagif and his groomsmen looked dapper in classic black tuxedos with coordinating white floral boutonnieres.

Following intimate vows in the Serenity Gardens, guests gathered on the patio of the Lakeview Event Center for a cocktail hour featuring an array of hors d’oeuvres prepared by the in-house chefs. Dishes included  ahi tuna in wonton baskets, avocado toast, butternut goat cheese stuffed mushrooms, classic tomato bruschetta, mini beef wellington, and braised short ribs. 

When the reception began, Tala and Vagif were introduced with a special cultural performance by the Al Fursan Dabka and Zaffah Group as guests prepared for a plated dinner service of chicken piccata with grilled asparagus and parmesan risotto and herb salmon with broccolini. 

The space was set with the most beautiful classic design featuring a mix of neutral white and champagne-colored linens, round and rectangle tables, and light wood crossback chairs. The tables were decorated with beautiful floral arrangements full of white blooms and greenery, white taper candles in gold candlesticks, and some elevated flowers to create dimension across the room. String lights were also hung above, cascading to the center of the room to create a warm glow. 

After a sweet first dance to Elvis Presley’s “I Can’t Stop Falling In Love,” Tala and Vagif cut their classic white three-tier cake and opened up the dance floor!

Here’s a look at Mindy and Gary’s romantic and rose-filled winter wedding day at The Bridge Building. A day filled with a lot of love and sweet, personalized details! 

This couple met through a church choir and dated for six years. When it came time to pop the question, Gary found the perfect opportunity to get down on one knee while walking the shore of the Waikiki beach in Hawaii. 

When they started planning their wedding, Mindy and Gary wanted to focus on good food and lots of florals. And with the help of our Instagram page and a thorough Google search, they decided The Bridge Building was the perfect spot to tie the knot. 

Mindy looked stunning in her sweetheart neckline, mermaid gown with a classic full updo. Her bridesmaids wore mix-and-match burgundy red dresses, while Gary and his men wore coordinating brown houndstooth suits with burgundy bow ties and pocket squares.

For the ceremony, the space was set with a greenery arch of roses at the end of the aisle, a glamorous chandelier above, and a beautiful view of downtown in the background. While saying their “I dos,” Mindy and Gary shared sweet personalized vows and included a unity sand pouring.

The space was then transformed for the reception, using their ceremony arch as the backdrop to their sweetheart table! The tables were decorated with white linens, burgundy napkins, and romantic floral centerpieces of greenery with red roses.

When guests found their seats, they enjoyed a plated dinner prepared by the in-house chefs of maple mustard glazed chicken with whipped potatoes and broccolini, rosemary maple pork chops with creamed kale and parmesan polenta fries, and a Creole shrimp and grits. 

After dinner, Mindy and Gary shared their first dance to “Everything” by Ella Mai and continued celebrating with their family and friends! Mindy told us her most memorable moment of the night was when her groom serenaded her with a song. How sweet!

Abigail and Jacob said their “I dos” with a stunning outdoor ceremony on the Lakeside Lawn at the Estate at Cherokee Dock, followed by a beautiful seated dinner in the Lakeview Event Center.

The couple first met through a mutual friend in 2020. On their first date, they slow-danced in an empty parking lot in the rain. Talk about romance! Abigail and Jacob both knew within a week they had something special and were engaged the following year.

Abigail stunned in a sweetheart mermaid gown with a large, timeless bow on the back. She accessorized with coordinating satin fingerless gloves, gold flower earrings with a pearl drop, and a cathedral-length veil. She wore her hair in an elegant, sleek low bun. A vision of a classic bride! Her bridesmaids donned champagne ballerina-length gowns in different styles, and all the ladies carried matching long-stem bouquets of white florals and greenery. Jacob looked dapper in a white and black tuxedo, while his groomsmen wore matching classic black tuxedos with coordinating white floral boutonnieres.

The couple exchanged their vows on the beautiful Lakeside Lawn, standing in front of a ceremony arch created with white florals and greenery, which served as a stunning backdrop with sweeping views of the lake behind them.

Following the ceremony, guests gathered in the Lakeview Event Center, which was decorated in all whites for Abigail and Jacob’s classic reception. The tables were set with wooden chairs, crisp white linens, stunning elevated white flower arrangements of roses and greenery, with white drapery cascading across the ceiling. The large covered patio was set with a round white bar and mixed seating, round white and cocktail tables also decorated in white, providing guests with the perfect setting to enjoy the evening.

After dinner, everyone enjoyed a night of dancing and entertainment alongside the beautiful lakeside views!

As their dreamy wedding day came to an end, Abigail and Jacob shared a memorable celebratory jump into the pool with their wedding party. They then drove away in a luxurious Bentley as fireworks went off down the scenic drive. A perfect ending to a classic fairytale of an evening!

Madding and Brooks celebrated their marriage with an elegant wedding day starting at the First Presbyterian Church in Nashville and ending at The Bell Tower, which was filled with refined style, lots of florals, and New Year’s celebrations!

Both Madding and Brooks went to the University of Auburn but didn’t meet until attending a mutual friend's wedding after graduation in 2017. Although they ultimately had a connection, they didn’t start dating until that following fall when they ran into each other again at an Auburn football game. When you know, you know!

Brooks later surprised Madding while she was working a photoshoot of her pastor. He got down on one knee and asked her to marry him! 

When it came to planning the big day, Madding knew she wanted a refined classic look with lots of flowers, and we think, with the help of our team, they hit it on the nail! She also said it was most important to find just the right venue, have a great band, and capture the day perfectly. 

They started the weekend with a rehearsal dinner at the Bridge Building. The space was set with a head table in navy linens with three round tables in white, all with gold chiavari chairs and tall centerpieces full of greenery, white flowers, and pops of red blooms. Our in-house chefs prepared a custom tapas stations with a lamb loin with mushroom risotto and broccolini, smoked gouda grits with shrimp, and beef tenderloin with potato gratin and brussel sprouts. Guests then celebrated with a cake by Dessert Designs.

On her wedding day, Madding stunned in a classic off-the-shoulder ball gown with a cascading white bouquet of orchids and peonies. Her bridesmaids had matching green velvet dresses and bouquets of greenery with champagne ribbons tied around the stems. As for Brooks and his groomsmen, they rocked classic black tuxedos with white boutonnieres.

After saying “I do” at First Presbyterian Church, the wedding party took a trolley over to The Bell Tower and enjoyed a pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta bite and a gimlet named after the couple’s dog Teddy before finding their seats in the main space. 

The main space was transformed into an elegant and romantic dream, full of cascading drapery, glamorous chandeliers above, and abundant candles on every table to create a warm ambiance. The grand floral arrangements filled the room with whimsical greenery and white blooms with pops of pastel blue, purple, and pink. The flowers served as the table centerpieces but also lined the white stage facade, decorated the grand staircase, and draped across their white six-tiered wedding cake. 

While Madding and Brooks shared a private meal together, their guests dined on a stationed dinner with different kinds of risotto, salad, roasted vegetables, and mashed potatoes, and later had a late night snack of gourmet french fries and quesadillas.

Before the clock struck midnight, Madding and Brooks cut their cake, shared a sweet first dance, and passed out their party hats! Guests counted down to the new year and everyone celebrated with silver streamers, beads, and all the fun as a live band played to celebrate the newlyweds.
