Anne and Michael's lush spring wedding at The Bridge Building Event Spaces was reminiscent of a garden party.

Hues of pink, taupe, coral, and ivory brought the industrial space to life. While fresh spring florals from Enchanted Florist brought the outside in.

The day began with a breezy rooftop ceremony followed by cocktail hour on the riverfront. Guests delighted in key west crab cakes, bacon wrapped shrimp, fried mac and cheese and more.

After mixing and mingling, dinner was served. Entrees included bourbon glazed salmon and filet with red wine demi-glace, grilled asparagus and rosemary roasted new potatoes.

With satisfied stomachs, guests headed back to the rooftop for dancing.

As their parting gift, family and friends were encouraged to take a cookie or two in celebration of Anne and Michael.

Chelsey and Parker's minimal boho wedding at The Bridge Building was a modern take on classic bohemian romance.

Inspired by minimalism and geometric shapes, the couple utilized a color palette of charcoal and ivory, with rustic greens, and a hint of gold for added glamour.

Their stylish reception was adorned with perfectly overgrown leafy runners, desert cacti, whimsical air plants, and sweet succulents by Kelly Jean Florals.

Following a sunset rooftop ceremony, guests proceeded downstairs for dinner. Guests were served a choice of maple mustard glazed chicken or bourbon glazed salmon with southern style green beans, parmesan risotto, and garlic parmesan mashed potatoes. Yum!

Alongside a stunning three-tiered naked cake, cupcakes resembling succulents and other desert flora were also up for grabs. All desserts were vegan and utterly delicious - courtesy of Leeuw Bake Shop!

Meghan and Kevin's modern burgundy wedding at The Bridge Building was organic with a contemporary twist.

Inspired by geometric shapes and a variety of textures, Meghan and Kevin transformed The Bridge Building's industrial space into a modern candlelit oasis.

Utilizing the couple's romantic color palette of ivory, beige and gold with pops of burgundy, PS Designs created breathtaking arrangements filled with eucalyptus, burgundy peonies, blush garden roses, and sweet succulents.

Furthermore, wooden food stations in varying heights created subtle drama, and guests dined on gourmet grilled cheese sandwiches, sliders, mashed potatoes and a selection of fresh salads.

After dinner, guests mingled and enjoyed the evening views of the Pedestrian Bridge and downtown Nashville.

Later for dessert, they indulged in sweet potato, strawberry and sweet lemonade cupcakes from Cupcake Collection. It was an enchanted evening for all within The Bridge Building's very own secret garden!

]]> RACHEL + NICK’S ITALIAN-INSPIRED WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/rachel-nick-italian-inspired-wedding/ Wed, 08 Feb 2017 18:26:47 +0000 http://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=217 A wink to a romantic night in Tuscany with Rachel and Nick at The Bridge Building.

The post RACHEL + NICK’S ITALIAN-INSPIRED WEDDING appeared first on Infinity Hospitality.

We were thrilled to help Rachel and Nick celebrate their marriage with a lavish Italian-inspired wedding reception at The Bridge Building.

While on a trip to Italy, Nick proposed in Italian (swoon!) over dinner. Soon after, Rachel decided on an all-Italian theme for their wedding, which happened to fit perfectly with her last name, Tuskan.

With wine red as the primary color and plenty of olive leaf greenery, their day perfectly embodied a hopelessly romantic Italian affair. Endless candlelight and antique gold accents added a romantic opulence, while burgundy touches added drama and style.

Bright, leafy arrangements filled with vegetables by Forget Me Not Designs gave a nod to Rachel's dietitian profession. To incorporate Nick's professional wrestling career, a fun grooms cake was made by Dulce Desserts to emulate a championship wrestling belt.

The wedding party made a memorable entrance, giving a second nod to Nick's love of all things wrestling. Introductions were complete with championship belts and skits, and guests were also treated to a hilariously choreographed dance by Rachel and her father.

For dinner, guests were served chicken saltimbocca with garlic parmesan mashed potatoes and balsamic marinated vegetables.

From the Tuscan inspired surroundings and cuisine to a magical send-off with tossed olive leaves, it was truly an enchanting evening for all. Congratulazioni!
