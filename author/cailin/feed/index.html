Here’s a look back at Samantha and Sam’s modern garden wedding at The Bridge Building that we haven’t stopped dreaming about. 

Samantha and Sam, both originally from Philadelphia, began their sweet relationship back in high school. After spending many years together and making a big move to Music City, Sam surprised Samantha with a proposal over a nice dinner at Gulch restaurant Kayne Prime. 

When it came time to plan their big day, the couple decided that they wanted a wedding that was easygoing and overflowing with happiness.

Samantha fell in love with the industrial look of The Bridge Building but wanted to add loads of greenery for an indoor garden feel. 

On their October wedding day, Samantha’s bridesmaids were dressed in champagne-colored dresses to match their ivory bouquets, and Sam’s groomsmen were all suited up in sleek black-tie attire. 

To set the scene, Nashville Lighting & Production hung an array of Edison bulbs to pair with Forget Me Not’s simple and timeless arrangements. Greenery lined the aisles and draped over the venue’s steel beams, complimenting the lush round ceremony arch at the altar and creating an oh-so-romantic style. 

Samantha stunned in a delicate floral lace gown, while Sam chose a classic and timeless black tux.

After their vows, guests mingled for cocktail hour and enjoyed pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta and hot chicken bites. 

Once the reception tables were set, everyone sat down for a plated dinner service of ginger soy salmon, smoked brisket, or lemon and herb baked chicken, and a few sweet toasts were shared. 

Samantha and Sam later shared their first dance to “When I Say I Do” by Mathew West, followed by a father-daughter dance to “My Girl” by The Temptations. 

After a celebratory dance party, the night ended with a sparkler exit under the scenic Pedestrian Bridge! Keep scrolling for more details on an indoor garden vibe with modern style at The Bridge Building.

Although it wasn’t the original day they had planned, Savannah and Jack’s whimsical summer micro wedding was filled with so much love and pops of color, and we loved being a part of it! 

Their love story started back in college, where they met at a philanthropy event for a local children’s hospital—the University of Illinois Dance Marathon! After three years, their friendship blossomed into a relationship. 

Jack decided to propose to Savannah in Chicago, her favorite city, with an elaborately planned day that included some of her friends and family. 

When it was time to pick the reception venue, Savannah told us that it was love at first sight with The Bell Tower and our planning team.

"We could tell right off the bat that their level of professionalism was unparalleled. They worked with us to meet all of our needs for COVID-19 when our plans changed dramatically. I would recommend them to EVERYONE,” she said. We love a happy bride!

Their wedding day began with a sweet ceremony at Christ the King Catholic Church, and then they were showered with dried flower petals as they made their way to an intimate reception at The Bell Tower. 

We couldn't help but fall for the sweet personal touches they incorporated into their big day. Savannah created a “love collage” for the reception, her grandmother called in to read a love poem that her family traditionally reads at weddings, and her mom made their table settings. 

In the main space at The Bell Tower, guests sat down at a wooden farm table decorated with colorful wildflowers and white taper candles to enjoy a meal that started with plated appetizers.

Since the guest list was so small, everyone had the opportunity to share kind words about the bride and groom and make a toast. As it turns out, this was one of the most memorable moments of their wedding day!

After the meal, Savannah and Jack shared a sweet first dance to “When I Get My Hands On You” by The New Basement Tapes. They chose this song because they “love Bob Dylan, and the song [uses] original Bob Dylan lyrics that Marcus Mumford and other musicians then put music to.” Plus, “it's romantic and sweet, and we love dancing in our kitchen to it,” Savannah said. And isn’t that the whole point? We think so!

The rest of the day featured lots of laughter, quality family time, and dancing. Scroll on to see all of the details from Savannah and Jack’s whimsical summer micro wedding!

Brennan and Kyle said "I do" surrounded by a small group of family and friends last fall at The Bridge Building. 

Brennan, a Nashvillian, met Mississippi native Kyle in Music City during a casual volleyball game. The pair started dating, and then Kyle popped the question in their home with a bundle of blooms and their beloved dogs. 

When the pair started planning their big day, they decided they wanted a relaxed micro wedding filled with good food, and, above all, they wanted everyone to have a good time. 

Their October wedding day began with a sweet rooftop ceremony under white drapery and glowing string lights. They wanted to keep it intimate, so they decided to forego wedding parties in order to focus on enjoying time with each of their guests. 

Florals were a very important part of their decor. The couple exchanged vows in front of a wooden arch adorned with a mix of greenery and pink and burgundy blooms that was perfectly coordinated to match Brennan’s bridal bouquet of dahlias, ranunculus, and champagne roses. 

After the ceremony, guests enjoyed a few hors d'oeuvres and relaxed during the cocktail hour until it was time for the seated dinner. The tables were set with white linens, and the tent was lit with soft purple hues. 

Guests filled their stomachs with Creole shrimp and grits, mustard-glazed chicken with carrots and whipped potatoes, and a yummy ribeye with grilled asparagus and Swiss potato gratin. 

Following dinner, the DJ  set the tone for the rest of the night, and guests danced the night away!
