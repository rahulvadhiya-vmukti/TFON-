When Courtney and Mike met through work, they didn’t expect to fall in love, but a connection was formed. After a sweet romance, Mike proposed to Courtney on the rooftop of her high-rise apartment building. 

Like many wedding days in 2020, Courtney and Mike’s went differently than they originally planned. When the couple decided to postpone their big celebration, they decided to mirror their proposal with a more intimate elopement on the rooftop of The Bridge Building. 

So on the morning of June 27, 2020, Courtney and Mike exchanged their vows in front of their immediate family with the Music City skyline behind them. 

Courtney stunned in a column-fitted dress with a beaded top, and Mike rocked a navy blue suit with a blue tie. A hexagonal wooden wedding arch draped in simple greenery served as a modern focal point of their small ceremony. 

After they said “I do,” the newlyweds enjoyed a sweet bite of their simple two-tiered white and gold cake by Nashville Sweets and then a tasty brunch provided by Infinity. We love the dessert-first approach!

On the menu was a balsamic grape and apple salad, honey butter biscuits with buttermilk chicken tenders, and a plated entree of turkey sausage, scrambled eggs, goat cheese grits, and homefries. Yum!

Courtney and Mike will go on to celebrate with all of their friends and extended family in July 2021. For more of their sweet and simple elopement on The Bridge Building rooftop, keep scrolling!

Today we're jumping back to Jonna and Brian's New Year's Eve wedding! Their black and gold style at The Bridge Building was the perfect way to head into 2020.

A little over six years ago, Jonna was introduced to Brian by her best friend, who at the time was dating Brian's cousin. Once Brian was sure he had found the one, he got down on one knee and popped the question. The rest is history!

When asked why she chose The Bridge Building, Jonna told us they used to talk their dog for a walk along downtown Nashville's Riverfront Park and down the pedestrian bridge, which is attached the venue.

"We walked the stairs of our wedding venue at least 100 times before we toured it. We actually took our very first picture together in Nashville along the bridge. When we found out we could get married somewhere so special to us, the decision was very easy!" she explained.

For the big day, The Bridge Building was transformed with glamorous touches of black and gold. In the Cumberland space hung an array of string lights with Nashville Lighting & Production's Maria chandelier glowing center stage.

Following Jonna and Brian's sweet ceremony, our team quickly flipped the main space for dinner while guests enjoyed the couple's signature cocktail, a Moscow mule, and some savory bites.

Once the reception was set, dinner service began. Guests filled their plates with slow-roasted prime rib, grilled cheese, street tacos, gourmet french fries, and more. Yum!

In lieu of wedding favors, Jonna and Brian chose to make a donation to St. Jude Children's Hospital in honor of their guests—a thoughtful and sustainable choice.

And what better way to end a New Year's Eve wedding? Sparklers along the river after the fireworks, of course!

Stephanie and Ben's moody southern wedding at The Quarter was beautifully intimate with natural elements and a classic color palette.

This pair met while stationed overseas at one of their favorite spots, the gym, and almost immediately hit it off.

After a quick trip home from deployment, Stephanie dropped Ben off at the airport. As she was walking away he said, "this is stupid, we should get married," immediately Stephanie replied, "yes!"

Finding the perfect venue for their moody wedding vibe was easy when it came to The Quarter! "It was a quaint size in a great location, and it was so beautiful. We fell in love with the brick walls and the windows overlooking the river," Stephanie told us.

They kicked off their day with an emotional first look that had us all reaching for a tissue, followed by a dreamy ceremony with candle light.

Once guests enjoyed a sip or two during cocktail hour, they found their seats to dine on savory selections from our friends at Dream Events & Catering.

For their first dance, Stephanie and Ben chose "On My Way to You" by Cody Johnson. "For the last two years Ben has been gone due to military deployments and an unaccompanied overseas tour," Stephanie told us. "Whenever he comes back it’s a tradition for him to say 'I’m on my way to you.'" Anyone else having bad allergies?

Looking back, Stephanie and Ben found "how beautiful everything looked and the location," to be what made their special day so memorable. Not to mention how many times their guests told them how fun their wedding was!

Megan and Taylor's rustic romantic wedding at The Bell Tower was southern-inspired with a relaxed, casual feel.

The pair met at William & Mary college where sparks flew. Their relationship went the distance when Megan lived in New York and Taylor in D.C. Shortly after, they moved to Nashville on a whim to begin their lives together.

Even though the pair now live in Philadelphia, they knew they wanted to make it official right here in Nashville where their lives finally merged.

They envisioned a fun, stress-free destination wedding with a glowing cocktail hour feel carried throughout the day. With that in mind, we couldn't wait to get started!

The day began with an intimate first look at Up, A Rooftop Lounge, which had the couple grinning from ear to ear. Megan radiated joy in a gorgeous off-the-shoulder lace dress as she carried an overflowing bouquet. And we loved Taylor's elegant navy tuxedo jacket too. Kudos to our ultra-stylish grooms!

After pictures with the rest of the wedding party, everyone made their way to The Bell Tower to kick off the festivities where rustic romance was the name of the game.

A custom greenery ring by Forget Me Not Designs was suspended from the beams to mark the altar. Sprigs of greenery lined the aisle as Megan made her grand entrance escorted by her mom.

Following, guests made their way to the patio for cocktail hour as the main space was transformed for the reception. The couple chose two signature cocktails to honor their precious pups and we're obsessed! Guests sipped Tom the Dog Collins' and Palmer's Punch made with vodka, St. Germain, lime juice, lemonade, a splash of champagne and a pink rock candy garnish.

And since we don't ever suggest drinking on an empty stomach, plenty of light bites were served as well. Including southern favorites like fried green tomatoes, mini chicken and waffles, and mini pulled pork sliders.

The reception space was washed with soft ambient lighting by Infinity's production team with ivory linens and navy napkins from Music City Tents for a classic southern look.

After mixing and mingling, guests were seated for dinner, where balsamic grape and apple salads with cornbread and honey butter were already waiting. They perused the buffet for entrees including chef-carved slow roasted prime rib with grilled asparagus and and rosemary roasted fingerling potatoes. Buttermilk fried chicken and four cheese macaroni was also up for grabs.

For dessert, our donut lovers just couldn't help but include a custom wooden donut wall into their day. With an assortment of donuts by none other than Fox's Donut Den, of course!

When it was time to say goodnight, Megan and Taylor's nearest and dearest wished them well as they exited through a tunnel of sparklers.
