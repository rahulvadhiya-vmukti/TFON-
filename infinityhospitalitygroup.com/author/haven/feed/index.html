Sarah and Daniel said, "I do," at OZ Arts Nashville last year, and today we're celebrating their one year anniversary with a look back at their sweet spring wedding.

The evening began with a traditional Jewish ceremony outside just before sunset. A whimsical custom-built chuppah from PS Designs set the scene. While pastel pinks and blue hues screaming spring lead guests to their seats.

Afterward, cocktail hour kicked off on the patio. Light bites included chicken salad phyllo cups, tequila infused watermelon (an Infinity summer favorite), fried green tomatoes, and mini hot chicken and waffles.

Meanwhile, the indoor space was transformed into a luxe, elegant setting for the reception. White floral arrangements with bright pops of fuchsia stood tall in sparkling crystal holders, and paper lanterns were strung from the ceiling for ambiance.

Guests sat down to plated dinner beginning with focaccia and sun-dried tomato butter alongside spinach and strawberry salads. Entrees included honey balsamic chicken with parmesan risotto and rigatoni primavera.

Revisiting this sweet couple's wedding on their anniversary is just as sweet as a slice of wedding cake! Scroll on to see all the adorable details.

]]> AMY + TYLER’S PRECIOUS PUP-INSPIRED WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/amy-tyler-pup-inspired-wedding/ Mon, 06 Aug 2018 20:09:27 +0000 http://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=9422 A sentimental, fur baby-inspired wedding ceremony & reception at The Bridge Building.

Amy and Tyler met eight years ago at Vanderbilt. Amy fell in love not once, but twice with Tyler and his goldendoodle, Tigger. Soon after, she followed suit and got one of her own. And wait for it, it was Tigger's half-sister Kensie!

After graduation, the squad moved to North Carolina for Tyler's medical residency. But because Nashville was still in their hearts, they moved back when it ended.

Excited to be home, they were thrilled to tie the knot at The Bridge Building. And we were just as thrilled to help bring their precious pup-inspired wedding to life!

This adorable couple found so many ways to invite us into their lives on their special day. Vandy school spirit and their love for their pups were evident in the little touches.

Floral collars were even made to include Tigger and Kensie in a few photos before the ceremony!

Following a sunny rooftop ceremony, guests enjoyed passed hors d'oeuvres on the riverfront patio. They sipped on one of two signature cocktails, a Kens-a-rita or a Tig-uila Sunrise. Both appropriately named after their doodles!

For dinner, guests sat down to a plated dinner beginning with balsamic grape and apple salads. For the main course, they were served lemon and herbed baked chicken, grilled flat iron steak or bourbon glazed salmon.

Shortly after, a dance party on the rooftop ensued. Huge, custom-made cutouts of the bride and groom were passed around the dance floor causing more than a few laughs.

For dessert, guests indulged in a sweet smorgasbord of cakes covered in the palest pink buttercream by Dulce Desserts.

Later on, the couple departed across the Pedestrian Bridge through a tunnel of mini American flags. Guests were invited to join them on Broadway to continue the party, ending the evening in true Nashville style.

]]> AMANDA + HUNTER’S CHIC, CLASSIC NEUTRAL WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/amanda-hunter-chic-classic-neutral-wedding/ Tue, 23 Jan 2018 22:37:03 +0000 http://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=4272 A traditional yet on-trend wedding ceremony & reception at The Bell Tower.

Amanda and Hunter's chic, classic neutral wedding at The Bell Tower was traditional, yet incredibly on-trend.

The couple chose a subtle color palette of neutral tones, with just a hint of sparkle. Their selection was perfectly juxtaposed with The Bell Tower's historic wooden beams.

Their day began with a sweet and sentimental ceremony. The father of the groom officiated, which added a special personal touch to the ceremony. Rustic urns containing stunning abundant florals by Forget Me Not Designs framed the altar. Pews were even brought in from the father of the groom's church especially for the occasion! The large and lively wedding party kept the energy high from start to finish.

After a cocktail hour on the patio, guests retreated inside for a buffet-style dinner. Dinner selections included maple mustard glazed chicken, baseball cut sirloin, sun-dried tomato creamed spinach, roasted brussels sprouts, and rosemary roasted fingerling potatoes.

For dessert, two impressive cakes by Dulce Desserts were up for grabs. Guests chose from a simple but beautiful four-tiered white cake or a University of Tennessee Knoxville themed grooms cake - to pay homage to Hunter's college career.

A vibrant dance party followed, led by the couple and their exuberant wedding party. From the neutral colors to just the right amount of glamour, Amanda and Hunter's wedding created a beautiful atmosphere for all who attended.

It was a gorgeous spring day for a gorgeous couple. An southern vintage wedding where no detail was left to chance.

The Bridge Building was filled with vintage furniture from Southern Events to encapsulate their elegant theme. Hints of vintage lace and accents of gray, pink, and navy surrounded the reception and ceremony.

As guests entered, they were offered Erin & Seth's signature cocktail, the Blackberry Sparkler. It was a refreshing mix of blackberry liqueur and sparkling wine topped with a fresh blackberry. As a guest book, the couple used their favorite records. Guests wrote their sweet notes and congratulations in silver and gold on the record sleeves.

The couple's ceremony on the riverfront overlooking the gorgeous skyline of Nashville was an absolute stunner.

Following the ceremony, guests enjoyed cocktail hour and delighted in small bites including bacon wrapped scallops, maple bacon chicken skewers, and mini pulled pork biscuit sliders.

After mingling, family and friends sat down for a plated dinner. The meal started with a balsamic grape and apple salad. Guests indulged in peppercorn filet and prawn with grilled asparagus and garlic parmesan mashed potatoes as the main course.
