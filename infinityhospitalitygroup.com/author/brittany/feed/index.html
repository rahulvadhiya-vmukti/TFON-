We loved helping Jami and Rob tie the knot at The Bell Tower. Their gold winter wedding was modern, contemporary and chic, with just the right amount of glamour.

The couple decided on a regal color palette of white, green and gold, with a hint of sparkle here and there for added fun.

Following an intimate indoor ceremony, guests were treated to a variety of passed hors d'oeuvres including fried mac and cheese and country ham biscuits.

After an indulgent cocktail hour, guests took their seats for a plated dinner service. Guests selected either peppercorn filet with blistered campari tomatoes and buttermilk mashed potatoes, or chicken scallopini with lemon scented asparagus and rosemary roasted new potatoes.

With full bellies, guests made their way to The Tasting Room for a little after-dinner sipping and danced the night away. The room was full of love and laughter in celebration of Jami and Rob.

Katye and Russell's timeless summer wedding began with a ceremony at Scarritt Bennett followed by a reception at The Bell Tower.

Inspired by the wood beams and industrial iron railings at The Bell Tower, Katye and Russell envisioned a natural and organic reception. We accomplished this by utilizing wood accents with natural greenery and delicate white flowers. Black lantern centerpieces complemented both the rustic and industrial elements within the space, making for an enchanting setting.

With Katye and Russell's chosen color palette of ivory and steel blue, utilizing this shade of blue was simple yet effective as it reads more of a neutral. Using a muted shade allowed us to incorporate a color that isn't so bold, which created a subtle romantic ambience for the day.

Prior to the send-off, the happy couple requested everyone to clear the room for one final dance to take in the last few moments of their wedding day. Katye and Russell danced, shared private words, and had a chance to reflect. It was a perfect ending to a perfect day.

Katye, a University of Kentucky alum, and Russell, an Auburn alum, were sent off with pom-poms. As the couple made their way through a sea of blue, white and orange, they waved their own respective colleges pom poms, and left their wedding reception in true collegiate style.

Rachel and Jonathan's classic, contemporary wedding at The Bell Tower was inspired by a chic color palette of black and ivory.

It was important to them that simplicity and style be carried throughout the day. Their vision came to life with fresh white florals by Forget Me Not Designs, a candlelit aisle, and whimsical white paper lanterns.

The bride and groom chose to forgo a traditional timeline and its accompanying formalities. They opted for a mix and mingle environment for their guests instead. Although we don't always recommend this, it worked for Rachel and Jonathan, as it spoke to their easy-going personalities. Nashville Party Authority was the key ingredient here, making a single announcement to guests, encouraging them to eat, dance and be merry at their own leisure.

With tradition put aside, this refreshing and unique flow allowed guests to dance freely, explore and mingle in celebration of the happy couple.

Julia Ann and Brady's elegant ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building was inspired by 1940s old Hollywood glamour.

From dramatic draping to gold chiavari chairs to gatsby chandeliers, the industrial space radiated style and glamour. A classic palette of ivory and black was the perfect fit for this vintage-inspired day. Julia Ann designed and arranged her own flowers, which provided a pop of color on each table.

Following a beautiful riverfront ceremony, guests delighted in cocktail hour before taking their seats for a plated dinner. They chose from two delicious entrees, ginger soy salmon with grilled asparagus and sage & white cheddar mashed potatoes or chicken roulade with four cheese macaroni and sautéed haricots vert.

After their intimate evening of dinner and dancing, the couple took off in true old Hollywood style in a classic Packard sedan.

Gail and Jim celebrated their nuptials with a colorful spring wedding at The Bridge Building.

These two started the evening with a cocktail hour on the rooftop alongside their guests. The rooftop tent was fully draped in a crisp white by Nashville Audio Visual and bright pops of color lined the aisle. When the ceremony began, the soon-to-be newlyweds walked down the aisle together. Gail and Jim were accompanied by his youngest daughter, Grace.

After the ceremony, guests enjoyed soft, upbeat jazz as they drifted between the first floor and the fresh air of the riverfront. The main space was set with a mix of square and round tables draped in vibrant, living coral linens. Romantic floral centerpieces sat atop each table and swirling silver chandeliers added an airy, feminine feel to the space.

The night was complete with custom dessert stations, including a chef-manned bananas foster station with rum, caramelized brown sugar, bananas, and vanilla bean ice cream.
