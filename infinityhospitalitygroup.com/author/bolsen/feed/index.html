Swooning for days over Jordan and Jake's elegant neutral wedding at The Bridge Building!

This sweet pair were high school sweethearts, and their years of love, laughter and tears radiated through them.

Both Nashville natives, Jake proposed under a tree at her parent's house where they took prom pictures together nearly 8 years ago. Their wedding day wish was to enjoy time with family and friends, and they did just that.

The couple's wedding weekend began with an elegant rehearsal dinner at The Quarter, followed by a timeless ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building.

They utilized a neutral color scheme of white, ivory and beige while florals and greenery added a touch of color. Jordan’s mom brought in some really fun rugs to enhance the ceremony space and other personal touches to incorporate.

Jordan and Jake kept their wedding traditional with the inclusion of Forget Me Not Designs' natural wood cross and the exclusion of a first look. Following a beautiful rooftop ceremony, guests headed to the riverfront for drinks and light bites.

For dinner, guests enjoyed table-side wine service along with maple mustard glazed chicken or creole shrimp and grits. Family and friends were given custom koozies, light-up sunglasses, fun light-up tambourines, and personalized M&M’s as take-homes.

The evening ended with sparkler sendoff under the Pedestrian Bridge, and the pair drove off into the night in a luxury sedan from Grand Avenue.

We helped Chicago natives, Rachel and Kevin, travel to Nashville to tie the knot with a strikingly chic garden-inspired wedding at The Bell Tower.

For their big day, the couple desired a fun atmosphere illuminated by candlelight. They opted for a neutral color palette of champagne with blush, gold and silver accents.

Thanks to our friends at Quest, curtain-style draping added romance and provided a stunning ceremony backdrop. While our eye-catching crystal chandeliers added drama and ambiance.

"Secret Garden" style florals from Forget Me Not Designs were the icing on the cake. Among these included a single blush spray rose boutonniere for Kevin and a loose style bouquet with anemones, blush roses, eucalyptus, and greenery for Rachel. An impressive 10 foot curly willow arch interwoven with white stock flowers framed the altar. Loose greenery, black, white and metal lanterns, and candles in tall glass cylinders were scattered down the aisle.

Stylish signage and sweet table numbers were artfully calligraphed by White Ink Calligraphy.

Gorgeous gold chiavari chairs added the perfect amount of glamour to the ceremony, while beige linens and ivory napkins subtly set the tone for the reception. Thanks, Liberty Party Rental!

Rachel and Kevin affectionately call each other "bird." In honor of their sweet mutual nickname, Rachel gifted Kevin with custom lovebird cufflinks! It was the sweetest, most thoughtful detail that meant so much to them.

The couple's precious Pomeranian pup, Roxy, was the pre-ceremony star of the show, and they made sure to include her in their wedding photos.

For cocktail hour, guests enjoyed a variety of passed hors-d'oeuvres including cranberry bruschetta, coconut-crusted shrimp, miniature hot chicken and waffles, and Thai basil chili lamb lollipops.

A plated dinner followed with delicious southern biscuits and honey butter along with pre-set balsamic grape and apple salads to start. Entrees included red wine braised short ribs and bourbon-glazed salmon with lemon-scented asparagus, parmesan risotto, and southern-style green beans.

For dessert, bite-sized Bundtinis from Nothing Bundt Cakes were a crowd favorite!

As the evening came to a close, the couple decided to forgo a traditional send-off. Instead, they moved next door to Martin's Bar-B-Que Joint for a fun afterparty. It was the perfect way to cap off their magical evening and enjoy a late night bite!

]]> An ‘Alice in Wonderland’ Inspired Baby Shower https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/alice-in-wonderland-destination-baby-shower/ Fri, 12 May 2017 01:17:38 +0000 http://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=947 A Q&A with mom-to-be about her Alice in Wonderland inspired, destination baby shower.

The post An ‘Alice in Wonderland’ Inspired Baby Shower appeared first on Infinity Hospitality.

]]>

Centerpieces of classic books, teacups overflowing with flowers, and "painting the roses red" bouquets set the tone for the Shah's Alice in Wonderland inspired baby shower. An archway of lush greenery with vibrant flowers welcomed guests to go "down the rabbit hole." Lauren Marie Atkinson Designs really knocked it out of the park!

After ceremonial traditions, dances and speeches, lunch was served. Guests enjoyed fun Asian fusion stations with sushi, spring rolls, lettuce wraps, dumplings, red curry, rice, and more.

Congratulations to mom and father-to-be, Shamini and Mikul! A wonderful conclusion to their weekend in Nashville celebrating their baby girl.

Where did you get your inspiration and how did you bring it to life?

We currently live in Scottsdale, Arizona, but my husband’s family lives in Tennessee. We wanted to do something different than the other baby showers in the family and to show the out of town guests Nashville.

I have always been a fan of Lewis Carroll’s Alice in Wonderland and was excited to do a themed party.  I am someone who is very detailed oriented, and I knew exactly what I wanted.  When I sent the description and pictures to Brittany, she brought it to life in an inspiration board that was more than I could have hoped for and within the budget.

What were some of the highlights of your day?

I loved the table decorations and the entrance way. That wowed me and was perfect for an afternoon event.

I and my guests thoroughly enjoyed the photo booth, especially since it was self-managed and can be texted/emailed immediately to you.  It was in the perfect location...so no one missed out on fun pictures and backgrounds of their choosing.

Also, the food was delicious.  We were concerned about not being able to use outside catering, but we found there was no reason to when you have such a delicious selection.  We are vegetarian and some of our guests have strict requirements on taste and they were able to cater to all of our needs.  I was completely impressed by the service of the staff in making sure all the food stations were full.

How did you choose your venue?

We looked at about 30 venues in Nashville, and we were looking for something unique that kept with the theme. We also wanted something that represented or showed off the South.

It was a difficult feat, because we were looking at venues online with no idea how it was in person. When we had it narrowed down to 5, none of which were The Bridge Building, my cousin flew down to check out venues.

After rejecting all 5 choices for different reasons, she told me she was going to this venue to see how it looked. She did not go inside, but was able to take pictures and videos and we loved it.

It catered to all our specific needs and the event planner made things 1000 times easier. I’m someone who micro manages, and it was a delight to not have to worry.

What was your experience like planning the shower?

Not being from Nashville and wanting to show family and friends who flew in from different parts of the world, I was very impressed by the people who helped me plan the actual shower.

I believe we showed off Nashville at its finest and had amazing food and drinks. We did a tasting with the chef that put us more at ease and they were able to work with us for all our needs.

Do you have any tips for others planning an event?

Trust the planner. We dissected the bill, questioned everything and in the end, everything that was suggested by the staff was an excellent choice and what we needed.

If you get to caught up in the minutia, it can ruin the reason you are having this event, to have fun!  Let the experts do what they do best so that way you can enjoy your event!
