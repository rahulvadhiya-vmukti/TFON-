Here’s a look back at Mary and Joe’s classic indoor garden wedding ceremony and reception with a neutral color palette this past January at The Bell Tower.

Mary and Joe, both high school AP English teachers, met teaching at the same school. While working together, they became great friends and were dating other people when they first met, but when they both became single, they took their opportunity to be together! 

The couple was engaged in the beginning of quarantine while renting a romantic cabin in Georgia. They went on a hike one day and Joe let Mary walk ahead for a little ways before he surprised her with the proposal. Mary said she knew it was coming  but didn't know exactly when and she was so surprised in the moment that she forgot to say “Yes!”

When it came to the vision for their day, Mary said she wanted a formal, black tie optional wedding and to feel like a high-end cocktail party with movie premier vibes. 

Mary wore an elegant lace gown with a deep V neckline, a classic waist-length veil, and held a stunning, cascading bouquet full of wild greenery and bright white blooms. Her bridesmaids wore mix-and-match black gowns with smaller versions of her bouquet. Joe and his groomsmen kept the elegant, black tie look going in sharp tuxedos with white boutonnieres. 

The I dos were held in the main space of The Bell Tower in a beautiful ceremony. The aisle was bordered with white rose petals and warm pillar candles, with large standing floral arrangements of greenery and white roses at the entrance and altar.

After Mary and Joe were pronounced as husband and wife,  the guests moved into the covered  patio space for cocktail hour. It was decorated with white paper lanterns, string lights, modern white lounge furniture, trees and plants, and seagrass rugs, giving the space a luxurious, natural feel that tied into their ceremony perfectly. 

While guests mixed and mingled outside, Infinity’s event team flipped the main space for the reception.  The room was set with bright white linens and black chiavari chairs that created the elegant look and feel Mary wanted. Tall floral arrangements stood at the center of each table, full of mixed greenery and white flowers, with smaller arrangements and candles below. Our grand staircase and original wood columns were wrapped in Jackson vine to bring the indoor garden look to life, and the room was lit from above with iron chandeliers from Nashville Lighting & Production. 

For dinner, guests chose from several food stations, including gourmet french fries, sliders, surf & turf, and risotto. And then the dance floor was open to keep the celebration going!

Samantha and Reece made it official with a cheery spring wedding on a sunny afternoon at The Bell Tower.

This sweet couple met when they were set up by mutual friends. "We were living in different states and met in Nashville one weekend with our friends," Samantha told us.

They knew it was meant to be, and Reece later proposed at the Opryland Hotel after a romantic dinner while the two were staying there for a Valentine's Day/pre-deployment celebration. With Nashville near and dear to their hearts, they decided it was only right to tie the knot here as well.

After visiting a few venues, they chose the The Bell Tower as they loved the character of the building, its convenient downtown location, and general ambiance.

For the big day, a rustic vibe paired with a sunny modern palette of navy blue and sunflower yellow created a cheerful atmosphere.

After a sweet ceremony between the windows, chic lounge furniture from Mayker Creative spruced up the patio for cocktail hour. While honeycomb lucite escort cards led guests to their seats.

Since great food was high on the priority list, a variety of Infinity favorites were up for grabs out on the patio. These included candied bacon, mini tomato bisque and grilled cheese shooters, key west crab cakes, hot chicken sliders, ahi tuna baskets, and coconut crusted shrimp. And we haven't even gotten to the dinner menu yet!

For dinner, balsamic grape and apple salads were waiting as guests took their seats. Entrees served included chef-carved slow roasted prime rib with sun-dried tomato creamed spinach and cheddar cheese and chive smashed potatoes. As well as bourbon-smoked bruleed scallops with balsamic grilled vegetables and lemon roasted fingerling potatoes. We'll take one of everything, please!

Later on, the couple danced to "Our Love Is Here To Stay" as guests looked on from the mezzanine with full bellies and hearts.

Music City Rhythm Revue kicked off an epic dance party where the couple had a blast dancing the Hora surrounded by loved ones in what was definitely one of our favorite moments of the day!

]]> DANIELLE + JORDAN’S NATURAL BLUE WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/danielle-jordan-natural-blue-wedding/ Thu, 28 Mar 2019 17:35:21 +0000 https://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=14317 A bright, natural blue wedding ceremony & reception at The Bridge Building.

Danielle and Jordan made it official on a bright winter afternoon with a natural blue wedding at The Bridge Building.

An icy palette of cool blue hues with ivory, gold accents and plenty of natural eucalypti by Forget Me Not Designs resulted in a winter wonderland with a modern southern twist.

After a breathtaking first look on the riverfront, Danielle's most memorable moment of the day, they exchanged vows framed by a rectangular greenery arch on the rooftop.

Following, guests lounged comfortably on velvet lounge furniture by 12th Table and enjoyed light bites during cocktail hour.

Fun calligraphed signage by White Ink Calligraphy and shimmering, patterned linens by BBJ Linen were fun modern touches.

Additionally, Details Nashville totally hooked it up with an awesome photo booth! Guests had a blast taking fun snapshots throughout the night.

Passed hors d'oeuvres included Nashville favorites hot chicken sliders, fried mac and cheese, bacon-wrapped shrimp, and BBQ chicken flatbread. We'll take one of each, please!

Afterward, guests made their way to the buffet for dinner. Entrees included baseball cut sirloin, seared ahi tuna, and four cheese macaroni with southern style green beans and cheddar cheese and chive smashed potatoes.

Later, they danced to "Just Breathe" by Pearl Jam for their first dance. "We both love Pearl Jam and wanted to choose a song that was non-traditional," Danielle told us. "We wanted our guests to get a little taste of what we like."

For dessert, our chocolate lovin' couple treated their guests to a mouthwatering brownie sundae station with all the fixings. Because all you need is love and chocolate, right?

To wrap up the evening, they made a stylish getaway into what is quite possibly our favorite "something borrowed" to date!

As guests waved fun, personalized rally towels to honor the couples' love of sports, they sped off in Danielle's grandfather's Rolls Royce, her most favorite car.
