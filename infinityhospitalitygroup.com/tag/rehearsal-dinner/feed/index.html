BEFORE THEY WED

PLANNING THE PERFECT REHEARSAL DINNER

You've spent months planning the big day, but don't overlook the details of the days leading up to it. Set the tone of your wedding weekend with a stylish rehearsal dinner and welcome reception.

Held the night before the wedding, this is the perfect opportunity for family and friends to gather in a not-so-hectic setting. It's typically a more relaxed evening full of excitement and anticipation.

Traditionally planned by the groom's family, it's becoming more common for either both sets of parents to share the responsibility or for couples to step in themselves.

Follow our five tips when planning the perfect rehearsal dinner!

1. Pick a unique venue.

Don't feel obligated to go the restaurant route. Especially if you're going traditional with your wedding the following day, the rehearsal dinner is a great opportunity to get creative. We love to impress out-of-town guests and ensure they have a true Nashville experience. Picking a venue that showcases the city and its unique history is a great way to introduce your guests to Nashville.

Betsy + David spoiled their guests at their elegant rehearsal dinner with an unparalleled view of Music City at The Bridge Building.

Photos by Melanie Grady & SheHeWe

2. Keep it casual.

You don't want your rehearsal dinner to outshine the wedding. Incorporate your style, but keep it casual. From the attire to the speeches, a more casual rehearsal dinner will encourage mixing and mingling and ensure everyone is comfortable, especially those who may be meeting for the first time.

Photo by Eden Ingle Photo

3. Choose a fun theme.

Whether it's inspired by a location, a color palette or one or your passions, incorporating a theme is a fun way to kick off the wedding weekend.

This sweet couple moved to Italy after their nuptials, so they began their wedding weekend with an Italian-inspired rehearsal dinner at The Quarter. Printed linens, Italian sodas, and fun centerpieces brought this theme to life.

Photos by Sara Bill Photography

4. Think about the food.

Let the food shine! It can either complement your wedding day menu or it can be completely different, so get creative with it! If you're opting for a formal plated dinner on the wedding day, give your guests a taste of Nashville for the rehearsal with a fun build-your-own BBQ station.

Lindsay + Brian's Southern-inspired menu included baby back ribs and buttermilk fried chicken, plus hot chicken sliders as a late night bite.

Photos by Sara Bill Photography

5. Plan some icebreaker activities.

The rehearsal dinner is a great time to introduce close family and friends to each other who may not have met yet. Whether it's board games, casino games, or cornhole, there's nothing like a game night to keep spirits high and ensure everyone feels part of the group.

Photos by SheHeWe & Caley Newberry

Lindsay and Brian kicked off their wedding weekend in style with an alfresco-inspired rehearsal dinner and welcome party at The Bell Tower.

With Lindsay being a Nashville native and Brian from Connecticut, they really wanted to introduce his family to the city. It was most important to them that they express their love and appreciation to their guests for traveling, and to give them an authentic Nashville experience with plenty of southern hospitality!

The couple hoped to create a truly alfresco experience within the space. So, we brought the outdoors in!

The overall design was evident from the get-go, with a beautiful invitation suite by Momental Designs depicting a sketch of The Bell Tower to set the tone.

The evening also included live trees from Green Resources, Infinity wooden farm tables and rustic arrangements by LMA Designs, which really put guests in an outdoor mindset.

Since a true southern experience was the goal, that also meant a southern-inspired menu!

During cocktail hour, guests noshed on refreshing summer bites like cranberry bruschetta and tequila-infused watermelon. Heartier selections included candied bacon, mint raspberry lamb lollipops and fried oysters.

Afterward, guests enjoyed table-side wine service as they sat down to pre-set watermelon salads before a plated dinner. Entrees included house-smoked baby back ribs and buttermilk fried chicken breast.

And it doesn't stop there! More guests joined afterward for the welcome party and were treated to late night bites of brisket tacos and hot chicken sliders.

For any guests with particularly sweet teeth, an Infinity dessert station opened with s'mores, tiramisu and strawberry shortcake shooters.

They capped off the night with specialty whiskey and bourbon tastings in the Tasting Room.

Betsy and David's wedding weekend began with an elegant rehearsal dinner at The Bridge Building.

David's parents hosted their family and close friends for an intimate dinner in honor of the soon-to-be newlyweds. Afterward, they were joined by even more guests to celebrate the kick-off of their big weekend.

David's mother Beth found a beautiful invitation that consisted of beige, ivory and gold which consequently became the theme and overall look for the evening.

As a result, we created a space that was elegant yet simple, utilizing warm beige linens, crisp white napkins and glamorous gold Chiavari chairs.

The couple's main goal was to treat everyone to a wonderful meal with a fantastic view. It was important that everyone was taken care of and that no detail was missed. We definitely made that happen!

The evening began with passed hors d'oeuvres including candied bacon, pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, margherita flatbread, antipasto skewers, artichoke beignets and coconut crusted shrimp. Guests sipped on the couple's signature cocktail from Nashville Event Bartending, as well as some of their favorite local brews.

For dinner, guests were served chef-carved herb roasted beef tenderloin with grilled asparagus and garlic parmesan mashed potatoes or grilled halibut with sautéed haricots vert, and roasted corn and charred tomato risotto.

After dinner, family and friends of the couple took a trip down memory lane, reminiscing about old memories. Everyone took turns to congratulate the pair and wish them well. It was a fun way to incorporate the whole room and you could feel how excited everyone was for them.
