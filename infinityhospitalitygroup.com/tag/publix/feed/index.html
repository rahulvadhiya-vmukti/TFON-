Amanda and Channing had the most elegant August wedding at The Bell Tower, complete with gorgeous draping and a string-lit ambiance!

This sweet couple met in 2017 and had their first date in Central Park in NYC. Four years later they were engaged in Cabo and married on August 13, 2022.  We are so happy to have played a part in their forever!

Amanda's portraits outside The Bell Tower were truly a moment. She held a large bouquet of greenery with soft pink florals and wore a beaded off-the-shoulder gown with lace detailing from Savvy Bridal. Her bridesmaids wore elegant matching off-the-shoulder black dresses and held smaller versions of Amanda’s bouquet. 

Their ceremony was complete with beautiful floor-to-ceiling white draping behind the altar, romantic candle aisle markers, and white flower petals down the aisle.

After they said the ceremony, guests moved to our beautiful patio for a cocktail reception. The space was complete with black and white decor with greenery-filled flower arrangements that elegantly tied into the rest of the day.

Then the party began! Amanda and Channing's reception was complete with simple yet elegant round tables with white linens that tied into the draping from their ceremony, which they also used as a backdrop for their intimate sweetheart table between our grand windows.

The couple shared a sweet moment on our mezzanine at the end of the evening overlooking their friends and family celebrating.

Kristen and Vincent had a classic ceremony and reception filled with modern boho florals and neutral, white, and gold details.

After meeting at UT Knoxville as freshmen in college, these two later moved to Charlotte, NC. While driving back to visit family and friends in Nashville, they planned a full night in Knoxville to break up the drive. Little did Kristen know, Vincent had plans of his own. He proposed at a beautiful rooftop hotel, surrounded by roses with their sweet pup Henry by their side. Once they arrived in Nashville the next morning, Kristen was surprised with an engagement party full of their closest friends and family. So sweet!

When it came to choosing their venue, Kristen and Vincent loved the traditional wedding feel The Bell Tower has, as well as how close it is to Broadway.

On her big day, Kristen stunned in a modern, long sleeve mermaid gown with a deep V-neckline. Her hair was styled in a classic low bun with curls to frame her face, and she held a beautiful freeform style bouquet of white florals and greenery. And to top it off, she wore a chapel-length veil adorned with pearls.

Kristen's bridesmaids wore custom matching gowns while carrying posy versions of her bridal bouquet, while Vincent and his dapper groomsmen sported matching black tuxedos with white boutonnieres.

When it came to the design, Kristen wanted a mix of classic, modern, and boho. We have to say, we think we nailed it with the help of The Florista. The ceremony was set with mahogany Chiavari chairs and large floral arrangements lining the aisle, full of white blooms, pampas grass, and abundant greenery. A beautiful hanging floral installation was suspended above the altar, creating a stunning focal point, while The Bell Tower’s staircase was dressed in the dreamiest greenery to tie the room together.

On the patio, Kristen and Vincent achieved the perfect mix of classic, modern, and boho by setting the space with a classic white bar, a large floral arrangement, a custom neon sign, and a modern lounge area with an Insta-worthy white floral backdrop. 

The reception was filled with neutral, white, and gold details. The king's table was lined with greenery garlands, dreamy taper candles in gold candlesticks, and centerpieces that repurposed the bridesmaids’ bouquets to add more depth and texture. The rest of the room was filled with round tables dressed in white linens that had a mix of classic floral centerpieces in varying heights, some with modern gold stands, and pillar candles at the center. They also chose a beautiful white facade and abundant white floral arrangements to decorate the live band’s stage.

For their first dance, Kristen and Justin chose “All Yours” by Tyler Childers. “We have both always loved Tyler and would listen to all his vinyls on our record players,” Kristen told us.

They also chose to share a final dance at the end of the night, giving them a moment to take in the venue and “look all around and reflect on the amazing night that just happened with all our favorite people.”

Here’s a look back at Mary and Joe’s classic indoor garden wedding ceremony and reception with a neutral color palette this past January at The Bell Tower.

Mary and Joe, both high school AP English teachers, met teaching at the same school. While working together, they became great friends and were dating other people when they first met, but when they both became single, they took their opportunity to be together! 

The couple was engaged in the beginning of quarantine while renting a romantic cabin in Georgia. They went on a hike one day and Joe let Mary walk ahead for a little ways before he surprised her with the proposal. Mary said she knew it was coming  but didn't know exactly when and she was so surprised in the moment that she forgot to say “Yes!”

When it came to the vision for their day, Mary said she wanted a formal, black tie optional wedding and to feel like a high-end cocktail party with movie premier vibes. 

Mary wore an elegant lace gown with a deep V neckline, a classic waist-length veil, and held a stunning, cascading bouquet full of wild greenery and bright white blooms. Her bridesmaids wore mix-and-match black gowns with smaller versions of her bouquet. Joe and his groomsmen kept the elegant, black tie look going in sharp tuxedos with white boutonnieres. 

The I dos were held in the main space of The Bell Tower in a beautiful ceremony. The aisle was bordered with white rose petals and warm pillar candles, with large standing floral arrangements of greenery and white roses at the entrance and altar.

After Mary and Joe were pronounced as husband and wife,  the guests moved into the covered  patio space for cocktail hour. It was decorated with white paper lanterns, string lights, modern white lounge furniture, trees and plants, and seagrass rugs, giving the space a luxurious, natural feel that tied into their ceremony perfectly. 

While guests mixed and mingled outside, Infinity’s event team flipped the main space for the reception.  The room was set with bright white linens and black chiavari chairs that created the elegant look and feel Mary wanted. Tall floral arrangements stood at the center of each table, full of mixed greenery and white flowers, with smaller arrangements and candles below. Our grand staircase and original wood columns were wrapped in Jackson vine to bring the indoor garden look to life, and the room was lit from above with iron chandeliers from Nashville Lighting & Production. 

For dinner, guests chose from several food stations, including gourmet french fries, sliders, surf & turf, and risotto. And then the dance floor was open to keep the celebration going!
