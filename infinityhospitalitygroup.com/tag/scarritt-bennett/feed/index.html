Dive into Whitney and Carson’s love story with a winter wedding that seamlessly blends modern trends with timeless charm. It was a stylish black and white affair, complemented by fresh greenery.

Their special day started at The Scarritt Bennett Center, the perfect backdrop for Whitney and Carson to say their “I dos.” The ceremony was full of timeless elegance with historic charm in the Wighman Chapel.

“Walking down the aisle and seeing Carson for the first time that day, and all the people we love in the same room … it was a feeling I could never imagine,” Whitney shared with us.

She stunned in a full lace gown alongside her best gals in matching beige dresses. As for the groom, Carson and his groomsmen rocked the traditional suit and tie look.  

After sealing the deal with a kiss, the celebration continued at The Bell Tower in Downtown Nashville. If you’ve never been, picture this: arched windows, historic charm, and wide open spaces. Greenery arrangements sat atop each table and adorned the large chandeliers, giving the main space a forest feel. Tables were dressed in black linens that made the greenery and white floral arrangements pop, with matching black drapery framing the grand windows. It was the perfect blend of modern trends and timeless style.

When guests arrived, they were met with signature cocktails and a large grazing table covered in delicious treats. After guests found their seats, they were served a plated dinner service with selections of bourbon glazed salmon with grilled asparagus and parmesan risotto, maple mustard glazed chicken with brown sugar glazed carrots and roasted garlic mashed potato, and smoked brisket with grilled corn and black eyed pea salad. 

The newlyweds opened the dance floor with a first dance to “Can You Feel the Love Tonight” and “Brown Eyed Girl” before guests joined in the all-night celebration!

Whitney and Carson were later sent off with white and orange pom poms in honor of their alma mater, the University of Tennessee.

It's safe to say we're seeing stars over Hannah and Hayden's stylish, neutral southern wedding.

This sweet couple is currently in medical school in Johnson City, Tenn., and met there too. After being in the same friend group for years, they took the next step and started dating.

Later, Hayden decided to make it official. "Hayden proposed with the mountains as a backdrop on my birthday with big balloons spelling out “marry me” in one of our favorite spots," Hannah told us. Swoon!

The next order of business was finding the perfect place to tie the knot, and they quickly decided on Nashville as it was central for everyone.

"I’m from middle Tennessee, Hayden's from west Tennessee, and we're in school in east Tennessee," Hannah told us. "I wanted a beautiful church for the ceremony and something fun and more 'Nashville' for the reception." Their wish was our command!

Starting at Scarritt Bennett, the girls got ready in matching pajama sets with mimosas in hand. The couple wrote letters to each other before their first look and took a moment for themselves before they tied the knot.

"Having a few moments alone with Hayden after our first look knowing we were about to become husband and wife was the most memorable moment of the day," Hannah said.

Following their traditional ceremony, everyone made their way to The Bridge Building for a fun-filled cocktail reception.

Upon arrival, guests were greeted with passed wine while local brews were up for grabs behind the bar. They noshed on a variety of delectable selections including pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, mini tomato bisque shooters and grilled cheese sandwiches, prosciutto-wrapped asparagus, beef and vegetable kabobs, hot chicken sliders, and coconut-crusted shrimp. We'll take one of each, please!

Since it's not always just about the bride, Hayden was in charge of the music. For the first dance, he chose "Speechless" by Dan and Shay. It was so sweet watching them twirl in the heart of the space!

To wrap up, the couple grinned and fist-pumped their way through a tunnel of sparklers and smiles under the Pedestrian Bridge.

Hannah and Taylor tied the knot with a classic champagne wedding beginning on a perfect fall afternoon at Scarritt Bennett and followed by an elegant reception at The Bell Tower.

This sweet pair met here in Nashville during the summer of 2016. The rest is history and Taylor later asked Hannah to be his wife at her family's lake house.

Above all, this couple envisioned an elegant, classic evening filled with family, friends and a good time.

The day began with a stunning ceremony at Scarritt Bennett. Guests entered through a breathtaking custom floral archway by Forget Me Not Designs, while white hydrangeas marked the end of each pew.

After Hannah and Taylor said "I do," guests made their way to The Bell Tower. We transformed the space into a romantic oasis from top to bottom. String lights draped from the beams and a stand-out chandelier hung in the middle of the space. Champagne-hued linens paired perfectly with black Chivari chairs from Music City Tents while creamy florals sat on each table.

During cocktail hour, guests relaxed with a drink or two in a chic lounge area courtesy of Please Be Seated.

Following, guests sat down to an elegant plated dinner and toasted to the happy couple with a glass of champagne. Entrees served included chef-carved slow-roasted prime rib with garlic smashed new potatoes and honey balsamic chicken with parmesan risotto.

And we never forget the kiddos! Since quite a few children were in attendance, we made sure to keep the crispy chicken tenders and fries comin' to keep their little tummies happy.

After dinner, guests looked on from the mezzanine as the couple danced as husband and wife for the first time.

"That was one of my favorite parts of our whole day," Hannah told us. "Bryce and Bethany sang the best stripped-down version of LANY'S 'ILYSB' which is one of our favorite songs. Taylor suggested it and when we listened to it, we both got tears in our eyes!"

An epic dance party then ensued and boy was it one for the books! "No one left the dance floor until they had to organize the send-off!" Hannah told us.

To wrap up, the couple left through a tunnel of white maracas in one of our most memorable send-offs!

"Maracas have been a big part of celebrations in my family ever since I was in kindergarten, so it was perfect," Hannah said. "Especially because I was too scared to run through sparklers!"

Katye and Russell's timeless summer wedding began with a ceremony at Scarritt Bennett followed by a reception at The Bell Tower.

Inspired by the wood beams and industrial iron railings at The Bell Tower, Katye and Russell envisioned a natural and organic reception. We accomplished this by utilizing wood accents with natural greenery and delicate white flowers. Black lantern centerpieces complemented both the rustic and industrial elements within the space, making for an enchanting setting.

With Katye and Russell's chosen color palette of ivory and steel blue, utilizing this shade of blue was simple yet effective as it reads more of a neutral. Using a muted shade allowed us to incorporate a color that isn't so bold, which created a subtle romantic ambience for the day.

Prior to the send-off, the happy couple requested everyone to clear the room for one final dance to take in the last few moments of their wedding day. Katye and Russell danced, shared private words, and had a chance to reflect. It was a perfect ending to a perfect day.

Katye, a University of Kentucky alum, and Russell, an Auburn alum, were sent off with pom-poms. As the couple made their way through a sea of blue, white and orange, they waved their own respective colleges pom poms, and left their wedding reception in true collegiate style.
