Alexa and Conor had the most romantic and modern wedding with neutral decor and bright white flower arrangements at The Bell Tower.

This sweet couple met in high school and then went on to college together at Auburn University. After graduating and a bit of long distance, Conor asked Alexa to marry him.

When it came to their wedding day, Alexa wanted a mix of romantic, modern, and natural design elements. Teaming up with Old Louisville Flower Studio and Infinity Hospitality Group, they nailed it.

For the ceremony, our main space was set with mahogany Chiavari chairs and a hexagonal wooden arch at the altar. It was dressed in bright white and ivory floral arrangements mixed with whimsical greenery and airy white draping that created a romantic focal point. Up above hong three beautiful Gatsby-style chandeliers.

After saying “I do," and as event staff flipped the main space for the reception, guests gathered on the covered patio for a cocktail hour. The decor and setup encompassed the modern feel Alexa was looking for, set with neutral lounge groupings, a custom neon sign, and gold details that provided a beautiful space for guests to mingle and socialize ahead of dinner.

Alexa and Conor’s design style was cohesive throughout the day, with the reception perfectly matching the ceremony and cocktail hour. The space was filled with neutral, white, and gold details. The head table was lined with an airy white runner, elevated floral arrangements with more whimsical greenery, and centerpieces that repurposed the bridesmaids’ bouquet to add depth and texture. The rest of the tables featured matching floral arrangements and champagne gold linens to finish off the modern look.

For their traditional reception cake, the newlyweds went with a classic three-tier white cake with gold leaf details to match their design, while the groom's cake was elaborately decorated with a mahi mahi fish and buttercream waves.

Alexa and Conor shared their first dance to "Into the Mystic" by Van Morrison and made their exit as guests waved navy blue and burnt orange shakers for their alma mater, Auburn!

Last October, Ashley and Kelly said “I do” surrounded by close family and friends during their intimate rustic wedding ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building. 

This pair met online and quickly realized they were a perfect match through their shared love of great films, East Nashville, and their pets. After some time together as a couple, Ashley and Kelly went on a long and relaxing beach vacation and got engaged on the shore! 

When it came time to start wedding planning, they wanted to stay out of the spotlight and share time with their friends and family, but their easygoing personalities and sweet romance couldn’t help but shine through.

Before both Ashley and Kelly’s moms walked each of them down an aisle, which was lined with blush and ivory rose petals, they spent some time getting ready—their attire was perfectly buttoned-up but down-to-earth—and then exchanged heartfelt letters they wrote each other expressing their forever love. 

After a sweet and intimate daytime ceremony where the almost-newlyweds exchanged vows in front of a stunning view of downtown Nashville, our main space was flipped for a reception that featured plenty of string lights, a small dance floor, and a collection of round guest tables adorned with smoke-colored linens and romantic floral arrangements in gold vases. They were made with pink peonies, thistles, blush and ivory roses, lush greenery, and succulents.

Guests then enjoyed a dinner of balsamic grape and apple salad, buttermilk fried chicken, sautéed vegetables, four cheese macaroni, and white cheddar mashed potatoes. Delish! 

As the easygoing day of fun, love, and laughter came to a close, Ashley and Kelly opened one of the biggest wedding gifts we’ve ever seen. While 2020 may have been a challenging time to hold a wedding, Ashley and Kelly’s guests made everyone laugh with a giant Uhaul box filled with the pandemic essentials: toilet paper and cleaning products!
