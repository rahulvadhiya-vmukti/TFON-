Meg and Tommy had a classic wedding ceremony at St. George’s Episcopal Church, followed by a fun-filled reception at The Bell Tower.

This lovely pair met when they were 18 at the University of Tennessee and have been together ever since. Both originally from Tennessee, Meg and Tommy decided to make a big move after graduation—  to Chicago!

Tommy decided to pop the question when they were back in Knoxville visiting family. Before a planned dinner at Calhoun’s, they took a walk along the river and Tommy got down on one knee. He later surprised Meg with a big party downtown. 

Knowing they would need enough room for all of their family and friends close to home, The Bell Tower was the perfect spot for their reception. 

On April 10th, their wedding day finally arrived. The day began with the sweetest ceremony at St. George’s Episcopal Church. Meg wore a gorgeous strapless gown by Anne Barge, paired with a long veil with lace trim and a romantic rose bouquet. Tommy wore a classic black tux with a white rose boutonniere.

Once the ceremony was complete, guests made their way to The Bell Tower for a night full of fun, food, and dancing. When they arrived, pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, bacon wrapped dates, and hot chicken sliders were up for grabs.

“After the long year we had with not seeing loved ones due to COVID, it was overwhelmingly beautiful seeing everyone as we walked in. We will always remember this moment,” Meg explained to us. 

Following cocktail hour, guests feasted on delectable dinner stations and then took to the dance floor. Meg and Tommy shared their first dance to “She’s in Love With the Boy” by Trisha Yearwood. Meg told us, “We are not the slow dance type. We are always dancing to upbeat fun songs, so we thought it was perfect!” And we can’t disagree!

Nicole and Andre celebrated their marriage with a spring wedding full of bold florals and navy blue details at The Bridge Building.

This fun-loving pair met while they were in high school working at the same movie theater, and the rest was history! On their 8th anniversary, Andre set up a private tour at the Huntsville space center and finally popped the question.

It was important to Nicole and Andre that their wedding day was low stress and in the heart of their city. 

They started their big day with a sweet first look before their rooftop ceremony. The altar was set with LMA Designs’ wooden arch adorned in rich-colored blooms and lots of greenery. 

While Nicole and Andre said their vows, we added the final touches for their modern yet eclectic wedding reception in the Cumberland space. The room was set with white and navy linens, navy blue taper candles, industrial lighting, sweet flowers in bud vases, and loads of greenery.

As guests made way for cocktail hour, they enjoyed selections of beer and wine in addition to an assortment of cured and smoked meats, many cheeses, spiced and candied nuts, breads, and honey before enjoying a savory plated dinner by The Bridge Building chefs.

After an upbeat wedding party entry and a traditional cake cutting, Nicole and Andre shared a first dance to “The Way You Look Tonight” by none other than Michael Bublé. 

And before guests sent the couple through a tunnel of rose petals and lavender, they made sure to hit the dance floor!

Lauren and Grant celebrated their love with a classic summer wedding full of soft blue hues and dainty details.

This sweet couple met in true Nashville style on a Sunday afternoon at the well known Dawg House Saloon during the NHL playoffs. Once they locked eyes, Grant made the first move— and just a year later, he found himself down on one knee asking Lauren to be his wife!

"We always knew we wanted the ceremony to be at Lauren's childhood church, Christ Church Cathedral. Pretty quickly, we knew The Bell Tower was what we wanted for the reception," Grant told us. "We only looked at four places total but couldn't forget the beauty of The Bell Tower."

They started their big day with a sweet first look before their ceremony.

"We both felt like we needed those few minutes of alone time together before we started the celebration," Lauren told us. "It helped ease the nerves for the rest of the day!"

While Lauren and Grant said their vows, we set up The Bell Tower for their classic summer wedding reception. The room was decorated with white and powder blue linens, acrylic details, whimsical floral arrangements, and loads of greenery on each table.

As guests arrived, they enjoyed a premium bar of beer and wine on the patio, in addition to a handful of savory bites. Selections included pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, fried green tomatoes, bleu cheese stuffed bacon-wrapped dates, brisket tacos, and miniature chicken and waffles. But the food didn't stop there!

Before Lauren and Grant shared their first dance to "All I'll Ever Need" by Anderson East, guests filled their plates from the dinner buffet. Fun fact: they chose their first dance song while on a road trip together just six weeks into their relationship!

It was important to Lauren and Grant that all of their guests had a good time and danced. Looking back, it seems that they did just that! With memories to share for a lifetime, guests sent-off the newlyweds through a tunnel of sparklers.

Danielle and Jordan made it official on a bright winter afternoon with a natural blue wedding at The Bridge Building.

An icy palette of cool blue hues with ivory, gold accents and plenty of natural eucalypti by Forget Me Not Designs resulted in a winter wonderland with a modern southern twist.

After a breathtaking first look on the riverfront, Danielle's most memorable moment of the day, they exchanged vows framed by a rectangular greenery arch on the rooftop.

Following, guests lounged comfortably on velvet lounge furniture by 12th Table and enjoyed light bites during cocktail hour.

Fun calligraphed signage by White Ink Calligraphy and shimmering, patterned linens by BBJ Linen were fun modern touches.

Additionally, Details Nashville totally hooked it up with an awesome photo booth! Guests had a blast taking fun snapshots throughout the night.

Passed hors d'oeuvres included Nashville favorites hot chicken sliders, fried mac and cheese, bacon-wrapped shrimp, and BBQ chicken flatbread. We'll take one of each, please!

Afterward, guests made their way to the buffet for dinner. Entrees included baseball cut sirloin, seared ahi tuna, and four cheese macaroni with southern style green beans and cheddar cheese and chive smashed potatoes.

Later, they danced to "Just Breathe" by Pearl Jam for their first dance. "We both love Pearl Jam and wanted to choose a song that was non-traditional," Danielle told us. "We wanted our guests to get a little taste of what we like."

For dessert, our chocolate lovin' couple treated their guests to a mouthwatering brownie sundae station with all the fixings. Because all you need is love and chocolate, right?

To wrap up the evening, they made a stylish getaway into what is quite possibly our favorite "something borrowed" to date!

As guests waved fun, personalized rally towels to honor the couples' love of sports, they sped off in Danielle's grandfather's Rolls Royce, her most favorite car.

Friends first, sparks flew when Colin asked Alyssa to be his date to – wait for it – another wedding! He instantly knew she was the one and proposed at Silo on his birthday.

The couple envisioned a casual and intimate affair full of fun music, dancing, good food and good beer. Their summer wedding at The Bell Tower ticked all the boxes and was a total blast for all who attended.

Pops of blue were incorporated everywhere from the lush florals by Branching Out to navy napkins from Liberty Party Rental to Alyssa's bridesmaids' dresses and her "something blue" Converse kicks!

The day began with a sweet first look outside of The Bell Tower. Alyssa chose to include a first look with each of her parents too. It was the sweetest thing to see!

Afterward, Alyssa and Colin tied the knot beneath the beams framed by a sweet greenery arch. The couple has a huge family and we loved how they were all included in the ceremony.

Following their nuptials, guests made their way to the patio for cornhole. Guests sipped on a selection of local beers from Nashville Event Bartending's Local Brews package and noshed on light bites. Passed hors d'oeuvres included prosciutto-wrapped asparagus, brisket tacos, fried green tomatoes, and fried mac and cheese.

For dinner, guests helped themselves to maple mustard glazed chicken or bourbon glazed salmon from a buffet spread. And don't forget the biscuits with honey butter!

For dessert, a towering donut wall sat proudly on display with an assortment of donuts from Fox's Donut Den. It was such a fun way to incorporate the couples favorite treat, and it was awesome to see guests' reactions when they saw it!

Guests then ventured to The Tasting Room for an after dinner drink or two and grabbed a Goo Goo cluster to take home.

To wrap up the evening, Alyssa and Colin's family and friends sent them off through a tunnel of glowsticks down Korean Veterans Boulevard and into their waiting getaway Mercedes by Grand Avenue.
