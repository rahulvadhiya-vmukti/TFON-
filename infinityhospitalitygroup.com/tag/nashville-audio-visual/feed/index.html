We're wishing Tali and Josh a happy five-year anniversary! Their elegant and easygoing celebration at The Bell Tower was what spring wedding dreams are made of.

As they were planning their wedding, they knew they wanted a unique spring look with an emphasis on fun.

On the big day, Tali looked stunning in her sweetheart neckline dress with a floral lace pattern, and she wore her sister-in-law Rachel’s veil as a sweet way to incorporate family traditions. The structured bridal bouquet featured ivory peonies, blush pink roses, and a few lilac accents.

Josh kept it classic in his blue tux with monogrammed sleeves and gold fox cufflinks that showed off his fun personality and sense of style.

The bride and groom enjoyed a quiet first look at Vanderbilt before heading to the wedding venue to sign their beautifully personalized ketubah—a Jewish marriage contract.

As the ceremony began, they walked down an aisle lined with lanterns and ivory rose petals in a criss-cross pattern to stand together under a romantic chuppah featuring a soft, organic structure made up of small trees, delicate white blooms, and gold drapery from Nashville Audio Visual. It was a showstopper!

After the ceremony, guests made their way to the side patio and mezzanine to enjoy a cocktail hour with passed hors d'oeuvres while Infinity staff transformed the main space for the reception.

As the newlyweds were announced, they headed straight to the dance floor to share their first dance to Ed Sheeran's "Thinking Out Loud." Then it was time for emotional father-daughter and mother-son dances before the big cake cutting and blessings before the meal.

Long wooden tables for the wedding parties were filled with gorgeous floral arrangements—both tall glass arrangements and smaller clusters of roses and lilies in square glass containers—green pillar candles, and silver and gold satin napkins.

After dinner, it was time for the joyful hora, which kicked off a night of dancing. The couple refueled their guests with a late-night grilled cheese station to keep the party going, and the celebration ended with a flower petal send-off.

Congratulations to these two on their five years of wedded bliss! Keep scrolling for some elegant spring wedding inspiration filled with ivory, gold, and silver details.

Anne and Michael's lush spring wedding at The Bridge Building Event Spaces was reminiscent of a garden party.

Hues of pink, taupe, coral, and ivory brought the industrial space to life. While fresh spring florals from Enchanted Florist brought the outside in.

The day began with a breezy rooftop ceremony followed by cocktail hour on the riverfront. Guests delighted in key west crab cakes, bacon wrapped shrimp, fried mac and cheese and more.

After mixing and mingling, dinner was served. Entrees included bourbon glazed salmon and filet with red wine demi-glace, grilled asparagus and rosemary roasted new potatoes.

With satisfied stomachs, guests headed back to the rooftop for dancing.

As their parting gift, family and friends were encouraged to take a cookie or two in celebration of Anne and Michael.

Julia Ann and Brady's elegant ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building was inspired by 1940s old Hollywood glamour.

From dramatic draping to gold chiavari chairs to gatsby chandeliers, the industrial space radiated style and glamour. A classic palette of ivory and black was the perfect fit for this vintage-inspired day. Julia Ann designed and arranged her own flowers, which provided a pop of color on each table.

Following a beautiful riverfront ceremony, guests delighted in cocktail hour before taking their seats for a plated dinner. They chose from two delicious entrees, ginger soy salmon with grilled asparagus and sage & white cheddar mashed potatoes or chicken roulade with four cheese macaroni and sautéed haricots vert.

After their intimate evening of dinner and dancing, the couple took off in true old Hollywood style in a classic Packard sedan.

We helped Ashley and Evan tie the knot at the gorgeous Church of the Assumption, and ended with a fun and elegant wedding reception at The Bridge Building. We love planning off-site as well!

Following the ceremony, guests were directed to the riverfront of The Bridge Building for cocktail hour. DJ AyDamn played some light tunes as the sun was just beginning to set behind the skyline. We couldn't have asked for better weather! Guests enjoyed passed hors d'oeuvres and wine at the open bar decorated with comfortable lounge furniture.

After their arrival, Ashley and Evan had a receiving line to thank each and every guest for attending their special day. Food stations were scattered throughout, and the cake was placed in the center, creating a magnificent focal point for the room. Can we talk about how gorgeous it was?! Thanks, Dulce!

Following dinner, the formal dances, and the cake cutting, guests made their way up to the rooftop patio to get the dance party started! Thanks to DJ AyDamn, not a soul left the dance floor for the rest of the night, unless it was to get a slice of the Groom's cake!

Ashley and Evan were sent off by a crowd of sparklers as they hopped into a vintage car provided by Matchless Transportation, with champagne & chocolate covered strawberries. It was the perfect ending to a fabulous night with such a fun group of people!
