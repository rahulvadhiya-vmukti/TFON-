Kelly and Fred made it official with a summer wedding at The Bridge Building with a little help from our friendors.

These two met through mutual friends before Fred reached out to ask Kelly on a date, to which she accepted. Little did Fred know, Kelly decided to invite one of her friends to third wheel on the date since she didn't know him very well. It was much to Fred's surprise when he saw his beautiful date and a trusty friend by her side!

Fast forward to New Year's Eve, Kelly and Fred spent the evening in Atlanta with some friends. Of course it was a packed night no matter where they went, but after an Uber (or five), a little rain, and some minor frustration, they went into the new year as an engaged couple.

Once the wedding planning began, Kelly and Fred knew they wanted a venue located in downtown Nashville, and The Bridge Building was at the top of their list.

"We fell in love with The Bridge Building’s breathtaking views of downtown and the Cumberland River the moment we saw it," Kelly told us. "This was the first venue we visited... and we immediately cancelled our other tours."

"The fact that it was a one-stop-shop was also a major deal breaker," she continued. "I love that you not only provided an amazing venue but that it came with event coordinators and access to vendors."

Now let's get these two married!

Following some of the cutest flower girls ever, Kelly's mom walked her down the aisle to meet her handsome groom and bridesmaids dressed in shades of lavender and soft pink.

Making sure the day was as easy going as possible was most important to the couple, but they could also hardly wait to get on the dance floor!

The bride and groom took the first twirl to “Best Part” by H.E.R. ft. Daniel Cesar, followed by a traditional hymn that Fred's fraternity brothers sang to them.

To wrap up the evening, guests enjoyed a sweet piece of Nashville Sweets cake and received complimentary mints from the bride and groom.

When we asked the couple what their most memorable part of the day was, they said it was when "the dance floor opened and we finally got to let loose and really celebrate with our friends and family!"
