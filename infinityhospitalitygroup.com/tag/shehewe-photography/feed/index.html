We're wishing Tali and Josh a happy five-year anniversary! Their elegant and easygoing celebration at The Bell Tower was what spring wedding dreams are made of.

As they were planning their wedding, they knew they wanted a unique spring look with an emphasis on fun.

On the big day, Tali looked stunning in her sweetheart neckline dress with a floral lace pattern, and she wore her sister-in-law Rachel’s veil as a sweet way to incorporate family traditions. The structured bridal bouquet featured ivory peonies, blush pink roses, and a few lilac accents.

Josh kept it classic in his blue tux with monogrammed sleeves and gold fox cufflinks that showed off his fun personality and sense of style.

The bride and groom enjoyed a quiet first look at Vanderbilt before heading to the wedding venue to sign their beautifully personalized ketubah—a Jewish marriage contract.

As the ceremony began, they walked down an aisle lined with lanterns and ivory rose petals in a criss-cross pattern to stand together under a romantic chuppah featuring a soft, organic structure made up of small trees, delicate white blooms, and gold drapery from Nashville Audio Visual. It was a showstopper!

After the ceremony, guests made their way to the side patio and mezzanine to enjoy a cocktail hour with passed hors d'oeuvres while Infinity staff transformed the main space for the reception.

As the newlyweds were announced, they headed straight to the dance floor to share their first dance to Ed Sheeran's "Thinking Out Loud." Then it was time for emotional father-daughter and mother-son dances before the big cake cutting and blessings before the meal.

Long wooden tables for the wedding parties were filled with gorgeous floral arrangements—both tall glass arrangements and smaller clusters of roses and lilies in square glass containers—green pillar candles, and silver and gold satin napkins.

After dinner, it was time for the joyful hora, which kicked off a night of dancing. The couple refueled their guests with a late-night grilled cheese station to keep the party going, and the celebration ended with a flower petal send-off.

Congratulations to these two on their five years of wedded bliss! Keep scrolling for some elegant spring wedding inspiration filled with ivory, gold, and silver details.

Georgia couple Erica and Bert tied the knot at The Bell Tower with a classic and modern wedding ceremony and reception.

Their story began while attending college together at Georgia Southern; they were inseparable after meeting at a student bar! Five years later, Bert proposed in the new home they bought together.

When planning their big day, the couple wanted to make sure their reception was the party of the century, so they went with a destination wedding in Nashville. Since the majority of their guests were coming from out of town, Erica and Bert wanted to make sure they got a truly Nashville experience, and The Bell Tower was the perfect venue for it.

For their decor, the pair kept things streamlined with a color palette of white, black, and green. They took advantage of the historic space’s beams and tall ceilings with delicate string lights and a lovely custom monogram designed by White Ink Calligraphy. It featured pecan branches as a nod to Bert’s work as a pecan farmer.

The nearly newlyweds exchanged vows during a chic ceremony decorated with white florals and petals, white pillar candles, and dramatic white draping that highlighted the room's dramatic windows. The guests then enjoyed a cocktail hour with beer and wine on the covered patio, which was staged with lounge furniture, while the newlyweds snuck off to enjoy the post-ceremony glow over a private meal. 

Erica and Bert took advantage of both the main space and The Bell Tower's mezzanine for the reception, as each was filled with round mahogany bistro tables decorated with crisp white tablecloths, black accents, and plenty of greenery. 

A wooden farm-style sweetheart table anchored the reception with an elegant white chiffon table runner, eucalyptus and other delicate greenery, tea light candles, and white taper candles. 

After dinner, special guests led a champagne toast and speeches before the first dance kicked off.

Later in the evening, the newlyweds cut a classic white cake adorned with a waterfall arrangement of white orchids. And although their beloved dog couldn’t be in attendance, the furry friend did make an appearance as a part of the groom's cake topper and as an illustration on their cocktail napkins!

At the end of the night, the newlyweds made their way out of the party in a fun glow stick send-off.

BEFORE THEY WED

PLANNING THE PERFECT REHEARSAL DINNER

You've spent months planning the big day, but don't overlook the details of the days leading up to it. Set the tone of your wedding weekend with a stylish rehearsal dinner and welcome reception.

Held the night before the wedding, this is the perfect opportunity for family and friends to gather in a not-so-hectic setting. It's typically a more relaxed evening full of excitement and anticipation.

Traditionally planned by the groom's family, it's becoming more common for either both sets of parents to share the responsibility or for couples to step in themselves.

Follow our five tips when planning the perfect rehearsal dinner!

1. Pick a unique venue.

Don't feel obligated to go the restaurant route. Especially if you're going traditional with your wedding the following day, the rehearsal dinner is a great opportunity to get creative. We love to impress out-of-town guests and ensure they have a true Nashville experience. Picking a venue that showcases the city and its unique history is a great way to introduce your guests to Nashville.

Betsy + David spoiled their guests at their elegant rehearsal dinner with an unparalleled view of Music City at The Bridge Building.

Photos by Melanie Grady & SheHeWe

2. Keep it casual.

You don't want your rehearsal dinner to outshine the wedding. Incorporate your style, but keep it casual. From the attire to the speeches, a more casual rehearsal dinner will encourage mixing and mingling and ensure everyone is comfortable, especially those who may be meeting for the first time.

Photo by Eden Ingle Photo

3. Choose a fun theme.

Whether it's inspired by a location, a color palette or one or your passions, incorporating a theme is a fun way to kick off the wedding weekend.

This sweet couple moved to Italy after their nuptials, so they began their wedding weekend with an Italian-inspired rehearsal dinner at The Quarter. Printed linens, Italian sodas, and fun centerpieces brought this theme to life.

Photos by Sara Bill Photography

4. Think about the food.

Let the food shine! It can either complement your wedding day menu or it can be completely different, so get creative with it! If you're opting for a formal plated dinner on the wedding day, give your guests a taste of Nashville for the rehearsal with a fun build-your-own BBQ station.

Lindsay + Brian's Southern-inspired menu included baby back ribs and buttermilk fried chicken, plus hot chicken sliders as a late night bite.

Photos by Sara Bill Photography

5. Plan some icebreaker activities.

The rehearsal dinner is a great time to introduce close family and friends to each other who may not have met yet. Whether it's board games, casino games, or cornhole, there's nothing like a game night to keep spirits high and ensure everyone feels part of the group.

Photos by SheHeWe & Caley Newberry

GO GREEN

HOW TO HAVE A MORE SUSTAINABLE WEDDING

Sustainability is a huge trend this year and hopefully for many years to come. Couples are looking at what impact they're making on the environment and taking steps to reduce their wedding footprint.

Feeling overwhelmed on where to start? Every effort, no matter how small, makes an impact, so it's important to focus on what's most meaningful to you as a couple. That's why we've rounded up a few of our favorite ideas on how to have a more sustainable wedding.

Stay tuned every month for what wedding trends we're currently crushing, and scroll on for Pinterest-worthy sustainable inspiration from our real weddings!

1. Say Buh-Bye to Paper

Perhaps the easiest way to have a more sustainable wedding is to opt out of paper goods and come up with unique alternatives for table numbers, ceremony programs, escort cards, and more.

While we still support sending a beautiful invitation suite via snail mail, consider using recyclable or plantable paper and reducing the number of inserts in your suite.

Rachel + Kevin and Tammy + Jeff both opted for reusable table numbers, while Leslie + Kyle chose a calligraphed mirror in lieu of individual menus at each place setting.

Photos by Rachel Moore & SheHeWe

2. Find Like-Minded Vendors

It's important to work with vendors who also take strides to reduce waste. Look for florists who use locally-grown flowers and don't use foam blocks. You could even consider using potted arrangements that can be replanted instead of cut flowers.

Work with caterers who source their food locally and organically, as well as those who have a plan for the leftovers. If combatting the agricultural industry is your biggest priority, you'll want to find a caterer that is both vegan and vegetarian friendly.

Photos by Blue Vinyl Creative & Sara Bill Photography

3. Make a Charitable Donation

Instead of personalized party favors like plastic cups or koozies, opt to make charitable donations in honor of your guests. Margot + Josh donated to a cause near and dear to their hearts, the Herren Project, while Jonna + Brian donated to St. Jude.

Photo by BlueGenes Photography

4. Reuse Your Decor

Reusing your decor is not only more sustainable, but it also cuts costs. Floral arrangements can be reused throughout the day from the altar to the reception, candles can make their way from the ceremony aisle to the centerpieces, and an arch can frame the ceremony and the sweetheart table.

Another great way to extend the life of your florals is by donating them to a hospital or retirement home after your event is over!

Photo by John Myers

5. Eliminate Plasticware

Renting your china, silverware, and glassware is not only the more sustainable choice, but it will contribute to your overall look, too. We're also talking about the bar here. Plastic cups and straws need to go.

If you're set on disposables, opt for compostables instead.

Photos by Details Nashville

LET THERE BE LIGHT

OUR FAVORITE UNIQUE LIGHTING IDEAS

Inspired by our latest feature in StyleBlueprint's 9 Wedding Trends We're Loving, we wanted to dive a little deeper into one of our current crushes — unique lighting.

From neon installations to basket chandeliers, lighting is an essential part of any event. It is crucial to setting the mood and adding a little or a whole lotta personality.

Stay tuned every month for what wedding trends we're currently crushing on. Scroll on for Pinterest-worthy illumination inspiration from our real weddings!

1. Geometric Pendant Lights

We love a statement piece, and geometric details never fail to amp up any wedding with a little modern glamour.

In order to create a stunning focal point over the head table, Fallyn + Aaron worked together with Lauren Marie Atkinson and Infinity's production team to create this chic, custom installation for their reception.

Photos by Davello Photography

2. Paper Lanterns

Paper lanterns are a stylish way to illuminate any space. With endless color options and a ton of placement versatility, we love how they add dimension and visual interest to any room.

Pro tip: Add string lights for added romance and ambiance!

Photos by SheHeWe & Katelyn Nicole Photography

3. Edison Lights

Known for effortlessly amping up the cool-factor, Edison lights are a surefire way to add twinkling style with a little industrial edge.

Intertwined with greenery or simply strung on their own, we love how they add a warm glow to any space.

4. Bohemian Brass Pendants

For an enchanting and free-spirited effect, we're loving these unique brass pendants.

Pair them with earthy elements, vintage rugs, and bright pops of color to add the perfect golden glow to any boho celebration.

Photo by John Myers

5. Marquee Signs

Retro signage is popping up everywhere.

Seen in home decor, weddings and other events, marquee signs are not only a great way to light up your space, but they come in super handy for stylishly labeling certain areas or activities too.

Photos by Rachel Moore, Celladora Photography, & Nyk + Cali

6. Projection Mapping

Undeniably bold and unique, projection mapping makes use of the latest innovative technology to transform any space into, well, just about anything.

How does it work, you ask? High-resolution imagery or video is seamlessly projected onto the walls and sometimes even the ceiling to transport guests to another season, city, or planet, without ever leaving the room.

Need a visual? Peep Lauren + Stephen's fall reception below where they projected golden Aspen trees onto the walls to bring their favorite season inside.

Photos by Rachel Moore

7. Custom Gobos

Whether it's on the floor, on the wall, or on the side of the Pedestrian Bridge, gobos are one of our all-time favorite ways to personalize and commemorate any event.

With endless options including company logos, custom monograms, and even hand-drawn imagery, let your imagination run wild for a truly unforgettable way to illuminate your event that will really wow your guests.

Photos by SheHeWe & Erin McCall
