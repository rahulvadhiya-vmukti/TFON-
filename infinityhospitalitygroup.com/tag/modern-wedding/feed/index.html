Catie and Brendan’s wedding celebration at The Bell Tower was filled with fresh blue accents, gold details, and a mix of classic and modern decor. 

The couple met at the University of Kentucky in Lexington through mutual friends at a football tailgate, bonding over their love for Kentucky basketball. Catie agreed to a group date with their friends— and the rest is history. 

Brendan proposed in Frankfurt at the Lovers Leap winery, surrounded by family and friends. To celebrate, the couple shared their first bottle of wine together! 

When it came to the big day, Catie and Brendan knew they wanted to incorporate their alma mater’s colors of blue and white. We think it turned out beautifully! 

The wedding began with a beautiful ceremony in the main space, which was decorated with varying gold geometric pendant lights and greenery. The ceremony arch was a modern gold rectangle with greenery and a touch of white flowers.

After they were pronounced husband and wife, the celebration continued to our patio for cocktail hour. The space was decorated with light blue linens accented with white that tied into a green boxwood photo wall decorated with a white balloon arch and custom neon sign. 

While guests mingled over cocktails and hors d'oeuvres, Infinity staff flipped the main space and mezzanine for the reception. Tables were set  with crisp white linens, beautiful blue floral napkins, candles, and gold-framed table numbers. Some featured arrangements of greenery and white flowers in tall gold stands, while others were set with greenery surrounding the candles.

At the end of the night, they played “Dixieland Delight” as the couple went around to say goodbye to the crowd. It was a very fun and memorable evening for all.

Looking for a little alternative wedding inspo? Nicole and Johnny's modern fall wedding at The Bell Tower was filled with unique and ultra cool design details.

Nicole, a Nashville native, and Johnny, from Atlanta, met during their college years at a 4th of July party at Ole Miss and instantly bonded over their shared passions of going to concerts, hiking, and travelling. 

This adventurous couple eventually moved to Nashville together to be closer to Nicole’s family. Later, Johnny proposed in one of the most scenic locations we’ve heard of so far—on a hike to the top of Peru's wondrous Rainbow Mountain! 

When they started wedding planning, they knew they wanted a fun and stress-free day with their friends and family above all else. This stylish couple definitely knows how to throw a party, and their big day at The Bell Tower was filled with modern, alternative, and elegant design details.

For the ceremony, he rafters were draped with soft string lights, and the aisles were lined with romantic bouquets filled with greenery and delicate ivory blooms. And true to the couple, they broke with tradition and decided to set up their altar on the far side of our venue in front of a sliding wood door, flanked by two modern concrete pillars that held ornate iron vases filled with large arrangements.

Nicole wore a stunning semi-sheer lace wedding dress and veil, and her bridesmaids wore sleek black gowns. Johnny and his groomsmen sported light grey suits with black ties and matching pocket squares.

After the two exchanged heartfelt vows, it was time for their guests to break for a relaxed cocktail hour and enjoy a generous grazing table filled with specialty cheeses, crackers, and fresh fruit while the main space was flipped.

As the couple made their way back, guests took their seats at tables decorated with simple black linens, pillar candles, small bouquets of ivory roses and anemones, and grey napkins. For dinner, everyone enjoyed delicious options like honey balsamic chicken, Southwest salad, balsamic marinated grilled vegetables, four cheese macaroni, and rosemary roasted fingerling potatoes before hearing some sweet speeches and hitting the dance floor.

Nicole changed into a casual look with dark jeans, a band tee, and blue suede boots, and we loved her commitment to being true to her own style! During the high-energy reception, the newlyweds' shared love of music was obvious, and Nicole even joined the band on stage for a song or two! Guests took time to sign the vinyl record guestbook before Nicole and Johnny cut their creative wedding cake that was painted to look like a geode with sparkling crystal formations inside.

At the end of the night, Nicole threw on a killer fringed leather jacket, and the newlyweds made their way through a sparkler exit and hopped into a vintage red convertible like the rock stars they are.
