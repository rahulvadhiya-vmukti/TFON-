CURRENTLY CRUSHING

OUR FAVORITE ILLUSTRATION IDEAS

This month, we're diving a little deeper into another one of our current crushes — custom illustrations.

From save-the-dates to bar signage to party favors, custom illustrations are a unique way to have a little fun and incorporate personalized details into your big day. Whether it's a portrait of you and your spouse-to-be, your furry friends or even your venue, we're seeing illustrations pop up everywhere in weddings as a guaranteed way to wow your guests.

Stay tuned every month for what wedding trends we're currently crushing on. Scroll on for Pinterest-worthy inspiration from our real weddings!

1. Bar Signage

Our pet's are our children, so it's only natural for us to want them to be a part of our special day. A fun way to incorporate our fluffy friends is with custom illustrations and signature cocktails.

Photo by John Myers. Paper Goods by Dear Addie.

Photo by Julie Paisley. Paper Goods by The Dotted Line.

2. Invitations

Whether it's an illustration of the Nashville skyline or your wedding venue, we love it when invitation suites get personal.

A custom illustration of the beautiful Bell Tower graced Lindsay and Brian's rehearsal dinner invitations, not only making it personal but also giving any anxious guests a good visual of what to look for.

Photo by Details Nashville. Paper Goods by Momental Designs.

3. Guest Book Alternative

We've always been a fan of nixing the traditional guest book and opting for an alternative that won't gather dust on a shelf. Natalee and Marc chose to have guests sign an illustrated series of the Nashville skyline. They later framed them to hang in their home.

Photos by Sarah Swain Photography. Prints by Art Prints Vicky.

4. Temporary Tattoos

Becca and Mike had custom illustrations of themselves made into temporary tattoos for their guests, and it's safe to say, we're obsessed.

Photo by SheHeWe

5. Cakes

A custom cake topper is another fun way to incorporate an illustration into your wedding. Find you a bakery that not only designs cakes, but are artists too. Fallyn + Aaron commissioned the one and only Dulce Desserts to illustrate their feline friend for the groom's cake.

Photo by Davello Photography

Amy and Tyler met eight years ago at Vanderbilt. Amy fell in love not once, but twice with Tyler and his goldendoodle, Tigger. Soon after, she followed suit and got one of her own. And wait for it, it was Tigger's half-sister Kensie!

After graduation, the squad moved to North Carolina for Tyler's medical residency. But because Nashville was still in their hearts, they moved back when it ended.

Excited to be home, they were thrilled to tie the knot at The Bridge Building. And we were just as thrilled to help bring their precious pup-inspired wedding to life!

This adorable couple found so many ways to invite us into their lives on their special day. Vandy school spirit and their love for their pups were evident in the little touches.

Floral collars were even made to include Tigger and Kensie in a few photos before the ceremony!

Following a sunny rooftop ceremony, guests enjoyed passed hors d'oeuvres on the riverfront patio. They sipped on one of two signature cocktails, a Kens-a-rita or a Tig-uila Sunrise. Both appropriately named after their doodles!

For dinner, guests sat down to a plated dinner beginning with balsamic grape and apple salads. For the main course, they were served lemon and herbed baked chicken, grilled flat iron steak or bourbon glazed salmon.

Shortly after, a dance party on the rooftop ensued. Huge, custom-made cutouts of the bride and groom were passed around the dance floor causing more than a few laughs.

For dessert, guests indulged in a sweet smorgasbord of cakes covered in the palest pink buttercream by Dulce Desserts.

Later on, the couple departed across the Pedestrian Bridge through a tunnel of mini American flags. Guests were invited to join them on Broadway to continue the party, ending the evening in true Nashville style.
