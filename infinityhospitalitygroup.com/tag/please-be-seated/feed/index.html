The Estate at Cherokee Dock was the perfect spot for a beautiful union of love and cultural fusion for Megan and Jorge last September for their modern Mexican wedding.

Megan and Jorge went to the same high school together but didn’t know each other until a few mutual friends introduced them years later. Little did they know that six years and one romantic lodge proposal later, they would walk this aisle together as husband and wife! 

They exchanged vows in front of the Estate with the serene lakeside views in the background and the willow oak canopy to their side. Wood crossback chairs sat in the lawn alongside the main drive making the perfect runway to the altar for Megan, escorted by her mom. 

Following the ceremony, Jorge’s cultural heritage was intricately woven in with mariachi melodies leading guests to a cocktail hour on the Lakeview Event Center patio, which was dressed with fairy lights. Here, modern flair met traditional Mexican charm as fun velvet lounge seating arrangements invited guests to relax and mingle with a glass of their signature passion fruit margarita. Vibrant colors of pink, orange, and more were incorporated into the decor, adding pops of color against the elegant backdrop of the estate. 

Megan and Jorge also set up an area for photos with a pink velvet couch underneath a wooden arch adorned with vibrant floral arrangements by LMA Designs. It was finished with a chili pepper garland and custom neon sign that said, “Te amo.”  

As the sun began to set, guests transitioned into the event center for the reception. The ambiance was one of refined elegance. Champagne-colored satin linens adorned the tables, creating a luxurious setting, and white floral centerpieces exuded sophistication while paying homage to Megan’s classic taste. Each table was set with a papel picado-inspired table number and custom white maracas for every guest.

During the reception, guests savored a plated dinner experience that combined gourmet delights of smoked brisket with grilled corn and a black eye pea salad, maple mustard glazed chicken with brown sugar carrots and roasted garlic mashed potatoes, and a rigatoni primavera. 

As the night came to a close, Megan and Jorge danced to “Ride With Me,” by Cody Johnson under the shimmering installation of disco balls and chandeliers for the first time as newlyweds.  

Their wedding at Cherokee Dock was not only a testament to their love but also a beautiful blend of cultures, creating memories to cherish for a lifetime.

Alexa and Conor had the most romantic and modern wedding with neutral decor and bright white flower arrangements at The Bell Tower.

This sweet couple met in high school and then went on to college together at Auburn University. After graduating and a bit of long distance, Conor asked Alexa to marry him.

When it came to their wedding day, Alexa wanted a mix of romantic, modern, and natural design elements. Teaming up with Old Louisville Flower Studio and Infinity Hospitality Group, they nailed it.

For the ceremony, our main space was set with mahogany Chiavari chairs and a hexagonal wooden arch at the altar. It was dressed in bright white and ivory floral arrangements mixed with whimsical greenery and airy white draping that created a romantic focal point. Up above hong three beautiful Gatsby-style chandeliers.

After saying “I do," and as event staff flipped the main space for the reception, guests gathered on the covered patio for a cocktail hour. The decor and setup encompassed the modern feel Alexa was looking for, set with neutral lounge groupings, a custom neon sign, and gold details that provided a beautiful space for guests to mingle and socialize ahead of dinner.

Alexa and Conor’s design style was cohesive throughout the day, with the reception perfectly matching the ceremony and cocktail hour. The space was filled with neutral, white, and gold details. The head table was lined with an airy white runner, elevated floral arrangements with more whimsical greenery, and centerpieces that repurposed the bridesmaids’ bouquet to add depth and texture. The rest of the tables featured matching floral arrangements and champagne gold linens to finish off the modern look.

For their traditional reception cake, the newlyweds went with a classic three-tier white cake with gold leaf details to match their design, while the groom's cake was elaborately decorated with a mahi mahi fish and buttercream waves.

Alexa and Conor shared their first dance to "Into the Mystic" by Van Morrison and made their exit as guests waved navy blue and burnt orange shakers for their alma mater, Auburn!

Plated dinner began with biscuits and honey butter and roasted pear and fennel salads. Nashville favorites served included house hickory smoked pulled pork and buttermilk fried chicken breast served with caramelized onion potatoes and southern style green beans.

Proving that all you really need is love and donuts, the couple cut into a simple naked cake and shared a donut from our custom donut wall with donuts from Fox's Donut Den for dessert.
