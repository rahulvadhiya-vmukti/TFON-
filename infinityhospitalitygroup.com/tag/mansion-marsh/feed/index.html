FALLING IN LOVE

ALL OVER AGAIN WITH THESE FALL WEDDING ALTARS & AISLES

From understated glamor to whimsical greenery, we've rounded up our top five favorite wedding ceremony altars and aisles for fall. Scroll through for inspiration from real weddings at The Bell Tower and The Bridge Building.

Meanwhile, we'll be here, sipping pumpkin spice everything, patiently waiting for cooler temps.

1. Whimsical.

Anna and Zach's whimsical fall wedding at The Bell Tower was vibrant and playful. Wild branches and florals from Enchanted Florist wrapped around a rustic arbor while petals and painted pumpkins lined the aisle.

Photos by James Anderson

2. Bohemian.

Meghan and Kevin's modern burgundy wedding ceremony at The Bridge Building was equal parts organic and romantic. A wispy greenery backdrop paired with matte black lanterns and greenery filled urns from our friendor PS Designs set the tone for this fall fête.

Photos by CK Photo

3. Understated Glamor.

Jessica and Josh opted for a single chandelier to illuminate their glamorous, Gatsby-inspired ceremony with semi-circular seating at The Bell Tower. Proving that there truly is beauty in simplicity!

Love this look? We have an extensive lighting collection with various chandeliers in all shapes and sizes to choose from so you can keep calm and sparkle on.

Photos by Chelsea Somohano

4. Greenery.

Natalee and Marc incorporated their cool and eclectic style into their boho-inspired fall wedding at The Bridge Building. with modern, yet whimsical greenery.

Organic arrangements of magnolia leaves and wild greenery from Mansion & Marsh flanked the altar while sprigs of greenery marked the aisle.

Want more fall floral inspo from Natalee + Marc? Head to their blog post for her swoon-worthy bouquet.

Photos by Sarah Swain Photography

5. Amber Ambiance.

Sometimes all it takes is a little lighting to set the mood. Natalie and Tyler transformed The Bell Tower with a string light backdrop, warm amber uplighting, and candlelight for their elegant fall wedding.

Photos by Krista Lee Photography

For Natalee and Marc, it was essential their wedding was both fun for family and friends and absolutely beautiful. The couple's boho-inspired fall wedding ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building was just that.

To incorporate the bride's cool and eclectic boho style, we used a color palette of gold, gray, and marsala red with lots of wild greenery. Her stunning bouquet from Mansion & Marsh consisted of different types of seasonal greenery with pops of blush, and the deepest of burgundies.

With the skyline bright in the background, Natalee and Marc's evening ceremony in November was dark and romantic.

Following the ceremony, guests were treated to a laid-back cocktail-style reception which fit the two of them perfectly. Guests were offered a selection of hors-d'oeuvres to snack on including tomato bisque and miniature grilled cheese, mini pulled pork biscuit sliders, miniature chicken and waffles with rosemary sweet tea syrup, and candied bacon deviled eggs.

When the evening came to a close, the newlyweds said goodnight through a tunnel of sparklers. With Marc being a tour manager, guests sent them off on their very own tour bus! So fun and unique to the couple. Congratulations to Natalee and Marc!
