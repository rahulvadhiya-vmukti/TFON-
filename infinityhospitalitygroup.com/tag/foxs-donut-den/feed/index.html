Madeline and Jackson celebrated their marriage with a beautiful fall wedding day designed with a mix of modern elements, boho chic details, and classic style at The Bridge Building. 

This sweet pair met when they were only 12 years old through their church. In January 2021, Jackson got down on one knee at the Gentry Farm and surprised Madeline with a sweet proposal and family to celebrate. 

When it came to planning, the couple wanted to prioritize the intimacy of their ceremony and the fun of their reception. For them, the venue choice was easy. Madeline later told us, “We always knew we wanted The Bridge Building. We booked way ahead of time to ensure we could secure our date! We love the atmosphere there and the unbelievable view of the Nashville skyline.”

The soon-to-be newlyweds began their fall wedding day with a first look on the Cumberland River greenway. It was one of their most memorable moments from the day and completely calmed any wedding day jitters. Madeline was dressed in a high-neck gown with ruching, while Jackson was in a classic black tux. 

For the ceremony, the Cumberland space was set with a hanging greenery installation with white pampas, pillar candles lining the aisle for a warm glow, striking pampas arrangements at the altar, and a beautiful nighttime city view. 

After Madeline and Jackson said “I do,” guests enjoyed a few delectable appetizers including fried green tomatoes, hot chicken biscuits, Italian meatballs, and pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta on the riverfront patio while the Infinity staff flipped the space for the reception. Tables were set with greenery runners, mauve cheesecloth gauze, lavender sprigs, and a mix of modern gold centerpieces filled with a mix of candles and white florals.

When guests found their seats, they enjoyed a stationed dinner prepared by the in-house chef of BYO sliders, gourmet mac and cheese, smoked gouda bowtie pasta, white cheddar with penne pasta, and a mashed potato station. Afterwards, Madeline and Jackson shared a first dance to “Best Part of Me” by Ed Sheran. 

The night continued with a party on the dance floor and guests cheered on the newlyweds with their alumni school flags and wedding bells.

Tara and Jeffrey made it official with a chic romantic wedding ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building.

This happy couple met on a Sunday funday in March of 2017. Tara was standing near the dance floor with some of her girls and just so happened to catch Jeffrey's eye! A cheesy one-liner and a good laugh later, their hearts were set.

After that, Jeffrey proposed in their home right before heading off to a Kenny Chesney concert.

When it came to their wedding, Tara and Jeffery were a little unsure of where they wanted to say "I do," but that changed once they visited The Bridge Building.

"The minute we walked in, we knew it was our venue. The views of downtown were exactly what we envisioned for our big day," Tara told us.

On the day of their wedding, the rooftop was adorned with bright white drapery and classic white ceremony chairs with hanging arrangements of greenery along the aisle.

Tara's bridesmaids dressed in chic wine-colored gowns with colorful bouquets of ivory roses, sprigs of greenery, and burgundy dahlias. And with an elegant dress by Justin Alexander and glitzy new shoes, Tara was more than ready to meet her new husband at the altar.

Once vows were complete, guests headed downstairs to the riverfront to enjoy some delicious hors d'oeuvres during cocktail hour. Tara and Jeffrey decided on pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, crispy risotto balls, ahi tuna, hot chicken sliders, bacon-wrapped scallops, and Key West crab cakes for guests to snack on.

After moving inside for a plated dinner during the reception, it was time for dessert! The newlyweds chose a sweet and simple cake from Publix and a wall of donuts from Infinity favorite Fox's Donut Den.

After a night of dancing and celebration, everyone sent Tara and Jeffrey on their way through a sparkler tunnel on the Pedestrian bridge.

With a little help from our friendors, Demetria and John made it official with a moody modern wedding on a perfect summer day at The Bridge Building.

This sweet couple met through their athletic endeavors while attending the University of Georgia. And the proposal was just as strong!

"[John] took me hiking at Talulah falls, one of my favorite active things to do, and proposed next to a waterfall!" Demetria told us. Groom goals!

When the time came to find a venue for the big day, the process was smooth as silk. "We both love the city of Nashville and knew we wanted a fun wedding destination," Demetria told us. "We came to Nashville once, looked at The Bridge Building and immediately knew we wanted to get married there."

And we're so happy they did! Starting with an intimate first look on the riverfront, the sun was shining and emotions ran high as Demetria just couldn't wait to see her beau.

Following, guests took their seats for a heartfelt ceremony on The Bridge Building's rooftop. Modern and deliciously moody, a minimal design was carried out through every moment of the day, starting with LMA Designs' "Archie" — a stylish wood geometric arch adorned with greenery — placed at the altar. Demetria was radiant and glowing as she walked down the aisle with her mom by her side — her most memorable moment of the day.

"I wanted to enjoy every moment and be stress-free," Demetria told us. "Having Sarah guide my guests and wedding party all day and take the stress of that off my shoulders allowed me to relax and enjoy my wedding day."

Afterward, cocktail hour began downstairs in the main patio tent, with the newlyweds also in attendance! As they greeted their guests, glasses of pinot noir and champagne were passed and clinked. The couple also opted for two fun signature cocktails, a Mexican Mule made with tequila for the bride and a Kentucky Mule made with bourbon for the groom. Yum!

More than ready for food, guests then made their way inside the Cumberland space for a plated dinner where arugula salads were already waiting. Paired with chic black seating, white linens and black napkins created a classic elegant vibe. Towering black stands filled with greenery and subtle white blooms lined the head table for whimsical drama.

Later, the couple danced to "How Would You Feel" by Ed Sheeran — a song that they feel best describes their life together. To wrap up, guests enjoyed a donut or two from a donut tower with custom to-go bags and took home bags of Hershey's Kisses from the Mr. and Mrs.

Megan and Taylor's rustic romantic wedding at The Bell Tower was southern-inspired with a relaxed, casual feel.

The pair met at William & Mary college where sparks flew. Their relationship went the distance when Megan lived in New York and Taylor in D.C. Shortly after, they moved to Nashville on a whim to begin their lives together.

Even though the pair now live in Philadelphia, they knew they wanted to make it official right here in Nashville where their lives finally merged.

They envisioned a fun, stress-free destination wedding with a glowing cocktail hour feel carried throughout the day. With that in mind, we couldn't wait to get started!

The day began with an intimate first look at Up, A Rooftop Lounge, which had the couple grinning from ear to ear. Megan radiated joy in a gorgeous off-the-shoulder lace dress as she carried an overflowing bouquet. And we loved Taylor's elegant navy tuxedo jacket too. Kudos to our ultra-stylish grooms!

After pictures with the rest of the wedding party, everyone made their way to The Bell Tower to kick off the festivities where rustic romance was the name of the game.

A custom greenery ring by Forget Me Not Designs was suspended from the beams to mark the altar. Sprigs of greenery lined the aisle as Megan made her grand entrance escorted by her mom.

Following, guests made their way to the patio for cocktail hour as the main space was transformed for the reception. The couple chose two signature cocktails to honor their precious pups and we're obsessed! Guests sipped Tom the Dog Collins' and Palmer's Punch made with vodka, St. Germain, lime juice, lemonade, a splash of champagne and a pink rock candy garnish.

And since we don't ever suggest drinking on an empty stomach, plenty of light bites were served as well. Including southern favorites like fried green tomatoes, mini chicken and waffles, and mini pulled pork sliders.

The reception space was washed with soft ambient lighting by Infinity's production team with ivory linens and navy napkins from Music City Tents for a classic southern look.

After mixing and mingling, guests were seated for dinner, where balsamic grape and apple salads with cornbread and honey butter were already waiting. They perused the buffet for entrees including chef-carved slow roasted prime rib with grilled asparagus and and rosemary roasted fingerling potatoes. Buttermilk fried chicken and four cheese macaroni was also up for grabs.

For dessert, our donut lovers just couldn't help but include a custom wooden donut wall into their day. With an assortment of donuts by none other than Fox's Donut Den, of course!

When it was time to say goodnight, Megan and Taylor's nearest and dearest wished them well as they exited through a tunnel of sparklers.

]]> KASEY + GEORGE’S CLASSIC NATURAL WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/kasey-george-classic-natural-wedding/ Mon, 01 Jul 2019 15:35:07 +0000 https://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=15390 A classic, floral-filled ceremony & reception with an epic donut wall at The Bell Tower.

Kasey and George imagined a classic natural wedding at The Bell Tower where everyone could have a great time and enjoy the moment.

This sweet pair met through a mutual friend in college while Kasey was in undergrad and George in law school. "We were friends for about a year before we began dating," Kasey told us. "Over nine years later, we finally got married!"

As for the proposal, George had a hard time surprising Kasey, but it was just as sweet as if he had. "George wanted to surprise me, but after all the years it was proving to be quite difficult, along with picking out the right ring," Kasey said.

"He proposed with a paperclip ring just the two of us," she continued. "The next day, we picked out a ring together and the planning began."

Their first task? Finding the perfect location and venue. "Nashville has always been quite special to us and we knew we wanted somewhere different from where we live in Illinois and from where I grew up in Texas," Kasey told us.

"We visited a few places in Nashville and actually ended up canceling the rest of the venue tours after we saw The Bell Tower. We knew it was perfect for us!"

And we're so glad they did! Since the couple envisioned a classic natural vibe in shades of ivory and gray, we brought in Forget Me Not Designs to work her magic.

She covered the space in soft, white florals and bundles of eucalyptus, which popped beautifully against dove gray linens from Music City Tents.

For their ceremony, a rectangular arch adorned with sweet spray roses and greenery framed the altar.

And for Kasey's most memorable moment of the day? "The moment we realized all the planning and details came together to one fantastic event," Kasey told us. "Everyone seemed to really enjoy it and that was pretty amazing!"

After cocktail hour on the patio, the reception kicked off in the main space. The same arch used to frame the ceremony altar was used to frame the sweetheart table with a stylish linen loveseat from Please Be Seated to stay cozy.

Plated dinner began with biscuits and honey butter and roasted pear and fennel salads. Nashville favorites served included house hickory smoked pulled pork and buttermilk fried chicken breast served with caramelized onion potatoes and southern style green beans.

Proving that all you really need is love and donuts, the couple cut into a simple naked cake and shared a donut from our custom donut wall with donuts from Fox's Donut Den for dessert.
