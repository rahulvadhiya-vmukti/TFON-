University of Tennessee sweethearts Jenna and Ben envisioned a natural atmosphere for their romantic summer wedding at The Bell Tower. They traveled to Nashville from their current home in New Orleans to make that dream a reality!

This couple was so much fun to work with. They truly worked to make their day an experience for their friends and family to remember. It was most important for them to truly enjoy their day and have fun!

Jenna had her slate blue bridesmaids picked out when we started our planning process, so we built an overall look that coordinated with those.

The overall look included a neutral palette with light and airy florals. They really wanted to create a comfortable space where all of their guests could be together to enjoy the celebration. 

The couple decided to stick with tradition and waited until the ceremony to see each other. When we pulled back the draping and Ben saw Jenna for the first time, it was truly magical.

Ben was absolutely enamored and Jenna had an enormous smile on her face as she walked down the aisle. You could feel the love the two of them have for each other and at that moment nothing else mattered.

Since Jenna and Ben met at UTK, they knew they had to incorporate school colors at their wedding. To send them off, guests lined the street with collegiate colored orange and white pompoms. It was such a festive exit! They even treated their guests to "Power T" cookies as parting gifts.

Congratulations Jenna and Ben!

Swooning for days over Jordan and Jake's elegant neutral wedding at The Bridge Building!

This sweet pair were high school sweethearts, and their years of love, laughter and tears radiated through them.

Both Nashville natives, Jake proposed under a tree at her parent's house where they took prom pictures together nearly 8 years ago. Their wedding day wish was to enjoy time with family and friends, and they did just that.

The couple's wedding weekend began with an elegant rehearsal dinner at The Quarter, followed by a timeless ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building.

They utilized a neutral color scheme of white, ivory and beige while florals and greenery added a touch of color. Jordan’s mom brought in some really fun rugs to enhance the ceremony space and other personal touches to incorporate.

Jordan and Jake kept their wedding traditional with the inclusion of Forget Me Not Designs' natural wood cross and the exclusion of a first look. Following a beautiful rooftop ceremony, guests headed to the riverfront for drinks and light bites.

For dinner, guests enjoyed table-side wine service along with maple mustard glazed chicken or creole shrimp and grits. Family and friends were given custom koozies, light-up sunglasses, fun light-up tambourines, and personalized M&M’s as take-homes.

The evening ended with sparkler sendoff under the Pedestrian Bridge, and the pair drove off into the night in a luxury sedan from Grand Avenue.

As the evening came to a close, Lauren and Oscar's nearest and dearest sent them off through a tunnel of sparklers. They shared one last kiss and hopped into a waiting classic black and silver Bentley, provided by Matchless Transportation, ending the evening in undeniable style.

Tammy and Jeff's enchanting garden style wedding at The Bridge Building was inspired by an original love song. Tammy, a songwriter, wrote Jeff a song titled, "Lucky Me." The pair chose to incorporate this phrase throughout their wedding day.

Also inspired by the illustrious wedding featured in the hit Blockbuster film, Twilight, the couple utilized a subdued romantic color palette of pale green, ivory and gold. Alex King of Front Porch Farms and AK Events & Design brought their vision to life with enchanting white florals and mixed greenery. While Music City Tents & Events provided white wash farm tables and chairs, making the space feel fresh and natural.

Following an idyllic rooftop ceremony, guests made their way downstairs for the reception. A custom seating chart by White Ink Calligraphy incorporating records and guitar picks was a fun twist on the traditional escort card. Chocolate guitars by Nashville Chocolate & Nut Company sat at each place setting alongside a CD takeaway of "Lucky Me."  We love using these kinds of unique details to bring out the fun personalities of our couples.

After dinner, guests returned to the 4th floor for dancing and mingling. We transformed the space into a cozy oasis with white soft seating and fireplaces to ensure guests felt right at home. With a custom GOBO reading "Lucky Me" beautifully illuminating The Pedestrian Bridge, guests enjoyed the expansive evening views of the city and celebrated into the night with Tammy and Jeff.
