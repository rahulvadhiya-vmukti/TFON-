CURRENTLY CRUSHING

OUR FAVORITE WEDDING CEREMONY ALTAR IDEAS

The ceremony, no matter how short, is the most important part of the day. From floral-filled arches to artfully draped chuppahs, your ceremony backdrop will set the tone for the rest of the day.

So this month, we're crushing hard on custom altars and all the ways our couples have made their ceremony their own. Scroll on for Pinterest-worthy inspiration from our real weddings!

1. Circular

We love a good circular installation. Echoes of Eden Florals took things up a notch for Caitlin + Robinson's wedding at The Bridge Building by adding a floral ring suspended over the altar.

Photos by Ashton Brooke Photography

2. Whimsical & Wild

We're all for symmetry, but this whimsical installation from Forget Me Not Designs for Alyssa + Jayson's wedding at The Bell Tower has us seriously reconsidering.

Photo by Teale Photography

3. Geometric & Modern

Effortlessly cool, geometric shapes have made their way from table decor to front and center modern statement pieces. One of our personal favorites? LMA Designs' "Archie." See how Megan + Bravein and Fallyn + Aaron made this focal piece their own.

Photos by Blue Vinyl Creative, John Myers, & Taylor N. Photography

4. A Twist on Tradition

A symbol of the home a couple will build together, the chuppah is a perfect opportunity to personalize the altar. This whimsical custom-built chuppah by PS Designs stole the show at Sarah + Daniel's sweet spring wedding at OZ Arts Nashville.

Photos by Frozen Exposure

5. Floor to Ceiling

Make a dramatic impact with a backdrop that reaches the ceiling, like Lizzy + Clayton's bright romantic altar at The Bell Tower.  J. Kent Freeman Floral Design teamed up with Nashville Lighting & Production to create this delicate focal piece.

Photos by John Myers

Sarah and Daniel said, "I do," at OZ Arts Nashville last year, and today we're celebrating their one year anniversary with a look back at their sweet spring wedding.

The evening began with a traditional Jewish ceremony outside just before sunset. A whimsical custom-built chuppah from PS Designs set the scene. While pastel pinks and blue hues screaming spring lead guests to their seats.

Afterward, cocktail hour kicked off on the patio. Light bites included chicken salad phyllo cups, tequila infused watermelon (an Infinity summer favorite), fried green tomatoes, and mini hot chicken and waffles.

Meanwhile, the indoor space was transformed into a luxe, elegant setting for the reception. White floral arrangements with bright pops of fuchsia stood tall in sparkling crystal holders, and paper lanterns were strung from the ceiling for ambiance.

Guests sat down to plated dinner beginning with focaccia and sun-dried tomato butter alongside spinach and strawberry salads. Entrees included honey balsamic chicken with parmesan risotto and rigatoni primavera.
