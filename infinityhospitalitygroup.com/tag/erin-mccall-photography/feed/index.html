Julie Kate and Brooks' whimsical wedding at The Bell Tower was complete with lush floral arrangements and boho-inspired decor.

These two studied at the University of Tennessee and met during their senior year. After graduating, Brooks moved to Nashville to start his career while Julie Kate stayed at UT to complete her masters.

A long-distance relationship wasn't ideal for the pair, but to Brooks it was practically love at first sight, and he was not going to let that go! After a sweet proposal in downtown Nashville, Julie Kate and Brooks were ready to start planning.

"We both wanted something big and beautiful. I love how romantic and spacious The Bell Tower is," she told us.

For the ceremony, a floral ring of blush roses and dainty amaranthus by Murfreesboro Flower Shop hung above the altar. While white whimsical lanterns and string lights filled the soaring ceiling above.

When vows were complete, guests headed to the patio to snack on our favorite candied bacon as well as cranberry bruschetta and southern chicken sliders. Following cocktail hour, dinner was served. Stations included a trio of salads, baked potatoes, and BBQ sliders. Yum!

Afterward, the couple shared a first dance to a recording of Julie Kate's grandfather playing "Somewhere Over The Rainbow" on his piano. With teary eyes and warm hearts, guests later joined Julie Kate and Brooks on the dance floor before sending them off in a sparkler tunnel.

LET THERE BE LIGHT

OUR FAVORITE UNIQUE LIGHTING IDEAS

Inspired by our latest feature in StyleBlueprint's 9 Wedding Trends We're Loving, we wanted to dive a little deeper into one of our current crushes — unique lighting.

From neon installations to basket chandeliers, lighting is an essential part of any event. It is crucial to setting the mood and adding a little or a whole lotta personality.

Stay tuned every month for what wedding trends we're currently crushing on. Scroll on for Pinterest-worthy illumination inspiration from our real weddings!

1. Geometric Pendant Lights

We love a statement piece, and geometric details never fail to amp up any wedding with a little modern glamour.

In order to create a stunning focal point over the head table, Fallyn + Aaron worked together with Lauren Marie Atkinson and Infinity's production team to create this chic, custom installation for their reception.

Photos by Davello Photography

2. Paper Lanterns

Paper lanterns are a stylish way to illuminate any space. With endless color options and a ton of placement versatility, we love how they add dimension and visual interest to any room.

Pro tip: Add string lights for added romance and ambiance!

Photos by SheHeWe & Katelyn Nicole Photography

3. Edison Lights

Known for effortlessly amping up the cool-factor, Edison lights are a surefire way to add twinkling style with a little industrial edge.

Intertwined with greenery or simply strung on their own, we love how they add a warm glow to any space.

4. Bohemian Brass Pendants

For an enchanting and free-spirited effect, we're loving these unique brass pendants.

Pair them with earthy elements, vintage rugs, and bright pops of color to add the perfect golden glow to any boho celebration.

Photo by John Myers

5. Marquee Signs

Retro signage is popping up everywhere.

Seen in home decor, weddings and other events, marquee signs are not only a great way to light up your space, but they come in super handy for stylishly labeling certain areas or activities too.

Photos by Rachel Moore, Celladora Photography, & Nyk + Cali

6. Projection Mapping

Undeniably bold and unique, projection mapping makes use of the latest innovative technology to transform any space into, well, just about anything.

How does it work, you ask? High-resolution imagery or video is seamlessly projected onto the walls and sometimes even the ceiling to transport guests to another season, city, or planet, without ever leaving the room.

Need a visual? Peep Lauren + Stephen's fall reception below where they projected golden Aspen trees onto the walls to bring their favorite season inside.

Photos by Rachel Moore

7. Custom Gobos

Whether it's on the floor, on the wall, or on the side of the Pedestrian Bridge, gobos are one of our all-time favorite ways to personalize and commemorate any event.

With endless options including company logos, custom monograms, and even hand-drawn imagery, let your imagination run wild for a truly unforgettable way to illuminate your event that will really wow your guests.

Photos by SheHeWe & Erin McCall

Allison and Jack's biggest priority was for their guests have plenty of fun at their wedding. Their simple and elegant southern wedding reception at The Bell Tower was certainly one big celebration for all.

After a ceremony at St. Georges Episcopal Church, guests made their way to The Bell Tower to continue the celebration.

We loved that there was a fun, informal vibe to the day. Mixed seating and dinner stations ensured that guests were able to freely move throughout the space at their leisure.

Upon arrival, guests made their way to the patio for cocktail hour. Light bites included fried oysters, tequila-infused watermelon and goat cheese and duxelles stuffed mushrooms.

Between mixing and mingling, guests signed Nashville cookbooks in honor of the couple. Personalized koozies, matchbooks and CDs with the couples favorite songs on them were given to guests as take homes.

For dinner, stations included Infinity's southern comforts, gourmet sliders, mashed potatoes and more. There was something for everyone!

The Tasting Room opened after dinner so guests could enjoy samples from its impressive whiskey and bourbon spread. But before letting guests in, the groom, groomsmen, and fathers all went in together. It was great to see them sharing a moment together to celebrate the occasion.

After an epic dance party, courtesy of For A Good Time Call, Allison and Jack's family and closest friends sent them off under a shower of pink rose petals and into their waiting getaway car from Grand Avenue.
