Madding and Brooks celebrated their marriage with an elegant wedding day starting at the First Presbyterian Church in Nashville and ending at The Bell Tower, which was filled with refined style, lots of florals, and New Year’s celebrations!

Both Madding and Brooks went to the University of Auburn but didn’t meet until attending a mutual friend's wedding after graduation in 2017. Although they ultimately had a connection, they didn’t start dating until that following fall when they ran into each other again at an Auburn football game. When you know, you know!

Brooks later surprised Madding while she was working a photoshoot of her pastor. He got down on one knee and asked her to marry him! 

When it came to planning the big day, Madding knew she wanted a refined classic look with lots of flowers, and we think, with the help of our team, they hit it on the nail! She also said it was most important to find just the right venue, have a great band, and capture the day perfectly. 

They started the weekend with a rehearsal dinner at the Bridge Building. The space was set with a head table in navy linens with three round tables in white, all with gold chiavari chairs and tall centerpieces full of greenery, white flowers, and pops of red blooms. Our in-house chefs prepared a custom tapas stations with a lamb loin with mushroom risotto and broccolini, smoked gouda grits with shrimp, and beef tenderloin with potato gratin and brussel sprouts. Guests then celebrated with a cake by Dessert Designs.

On her wedding day, Madding stunned in a classic off-the-shoulder ball gown with a cascading white bouquet of orchids and peonies. Her bridesmaids had matching green velvet dresses and bouquets of greenery with champagne ribbons tied around the stems. As for Brooks and his groomsmen, they rocked classic black tuxedos with white boutonnieres.

After saying “I do” at First Presbyterian Church, the wedding party took a trolley over to The Bell Tower and enjoyed a pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta bite and a gimlet named after the couple’s dog Teddy before finding their seats in the main space. 

The main space was transformed into an elegant and romantic dream, full of cascading drapery, glamorous chandeliers above, and abundant candles on every table to create a warm ambiance. The grand floral arrangements filled the room with whimsical greenery and white blooms with pops of pastel blue, purple, and pink. The flowers served as the table centerpieces but also lined the white stage facade, decorated the grand staircase, and draped across their white six-tiered wedding cake. 

While Madding and Brooks shared a private meal together, their guests dined on a stationed dinner with different kinds of risotto, salad, roasted vegetables, and mashed potatoes, and later had a late night snack of gourmet french fries and quesadillas.

Before the clock struck midnight, Madding and Brooks cut their cake, shared a sweet first dance, and passed out their party hats! Guests counted down to the new year and everyone celebrated with silver streamers, beads, and all the fun as a live band played to celebrate the newlyweds.

Rachel and Camron celebrated their New Year’s Eve wedding with an intimate winter ceremony on the rooftop and a moody, boho reception in the main space of the Bridge Building. 

Rachel, from St. Louis, and Camron, from Philadelphia, met while working at a summer job at Six Flags and knew their connection was more than just a summer fling! Later on, Camron got down on one knee at the Forest Park in St Louis. 

During their time dating long distance, these lovebirds would spend a lot of time in Nashville, so they knew this was the perfect place to tie the knot. With the Bridge Building being a short walk from Broadway and having an unbeatable view of downtown, the venue was an easy choice! 

When wedding day arrived, Rachel and Camron shared a sweet first look before the ceremony on the riverfront patio. Rachel stunned in a romantic lace ball gown with exaggerated off the shoulder sleeves and a boho bouquet full of dried florals, pampas grass, proteas, and earth-toned pastel roses. As for Camron, he went with a blue suit and ivory tie to match the neutral style of Rachel’s bridesmaids, who wore mismatched beige and champagne-colored dresses. 

The ceremony was set with a nighttime view of downtown Nashville, glowing Christmas trees as the backdrop, and crisp winter air for an intimate setting to say “I do.” Rachel and Camron also had their family and friends participate in a candle lighting ceremony to symbolize their unity. 

Afterwards, guests took to the main space to grab a glass of champagne with their table number, and got ready for a plated dinner of herbed chicken with broccolini and roasted yukon potatoes and blackberry pork loin or bourbon glazed salmon with grilled asparagus and parmesan risotto.

The Cumberland space was decorated with a mix of farmhouse and round tables, black silk napkins against crisp white linens, moody bulb string lights and candle centerpieces surrounded with greenery. The head table, decorated with roses and a greenery garland, was dark wood with a modern black backdrop that was adorned with striking, bold boho floral arrangements that matched Rachel’s bouquet and a custom white LED sign featuring the couple’s newly-shared last name. On display was their classic, white three-tier wedding cake adorned with dried flowers and pampas. And for their guestbook, Rachel and Camron used a map of Nashville as an alternative for family and friends to sign to remember this very special day. The guestbook table was decorated with sweet photos of the couple and a modern black sign with gold lettering. 

Before the night came to an end, Rachel and Camron shared a first dance and then enjoyed Nashville’s annual firework show on the rooftop as they headed into the new year with their friends and family as newlyweds!

Rachel and Trey celebrated New Year's Eve with a wedding ceremony and reception at The Bell Tower in downtown Nashville.

Although they are both Nashville natives, these two actually met in Uganda while working with Heal Ministries. When Trey popped the question, they were overlooking the Nile River on a hill by their home, which was similar to how they spent their first-ever date together.

Once they returned to Nashville, the wedding festivities quickly began.

Since Rachel and Trey were both away for a year, it was important for them to have an incredible time with their friends and family. And they did just that!

The wedding day began with a sweet first look outside of The Bell Tower. Rachel stunned in her gown with ivory roses in her hair.

After a meaningful ceremony, guests took to the patio for signature cocktails and passed hors d'oeuvres while our team flipped the main space for dinner.

Once the reception was set with black linens and candle light, guests found their seats. The whimsical arch which set the scene for the ceremony was used again to frame the sweetheart table.

For the wedding toasts, our team passed glasses of champagne— because what else would you sip on to celebrate newlyweds on New Year Eve's? Afterward, Rachel and Trey made their way to the dance floor to "L-O-V-E" by Nat King Cole, later ending the night with "Dance With Somebody" by Whitney Houston. Safe to say it was a fun night of dancing!

Before sending these two off through a tunnel of sparklers, guests grabbed a pottery mug created by the women from Heal Ministries in Uganda. What a unique and meaningful gift!

Today we're jumping back to Jonna and Brian's New Year's Eve wedding! Their black and gold style at The Bridge Building was the perfect way to head into 2020.

A little over six years ago, Jonna was introduced to Brian by her best friend, who at the time was dating Brian's cousin. Once Brian was sure he had found the one, he got down on one knee and popped the question. The rest is history!

When asked why she chose The Bridge Building, Jonna told us they used to talk their dog for a walk along downtown Nashville's Riverfront Park and down the pedestrian bridge, which is attached the venue.

"We walked the stairs of our wedding venue at least 100 times before we toured it. We actually took our very first picture together in Nashville along the bridge. When we found out we could get married somewhere so special to us, the decision was very easy!" she explained.

For the big day, The Bridge Building was transformed with glamorous touches of black and gold. In the Cumberland space hung an array of string lights with Nashville Lighting & Production's Maria chandelier glowing center stage.

Following Jonna and Brian's sweet ceremony, our team quickly flipped the main space for dinner while guests enjoyed the couple's signature cocktail, a Moscow mule, and some savory bites.

Once the reception was set, dinner service began. Guests filled their plates with slow-roasted prime rib, grilled cheese, street tacos, gourmet french fries, and more. Yum!

In lieu of wedding favors, Jonna and Brian chose to make a donation to St. Jude Children's Hospital in honor of their guests—a thoughtful and sustainable choice.

And what better way to end a New Year's Eve wedding? Sparklers along the river after the fireworks, of course!
