New York City dwellers Nicole and Chris envisioned a romantic glam wedding at The Bell Tower. With gold touches, soft blush florals, and flickering candlelight, we're happy to say that the end result was all that and then some!

After get off work late one night, Nicole arrived home to flowers on the table and a pizza on the stove. "I put on my PJs and was pretty far into a pepperoni slice when Chris walked into the room, put on 'I Wanna Grow Old With You' from The Wedding Singer, and got down on one knee," Nicole told us. "I never wanted a big proposal. He knows my favorite place is at home and New York pizza is the best! It was perfect for us and a wonderful memory."

As planning began, they knew they wanted a destination wedding to make travel easier for their families. "Once we decided on Nashville, we started researching wedding venues," Nicole said. "We toured The Bridge Building and The Bell Tower, but once we walked into The Bell Tower, our minds were made up. It's gorgeous, open and everything we wanted in a space."

Starting with a bright ceremony between the windows on a perfect spring afternoon, there wasn't a dry eye in the house as Nicole began her walk down the aisle with her dad by her side.

"It was really important to include my father into our day as much as possible," Nicole told us. "He is eight years into his diagnosis with Alzheimer’s, and at the rehearsal, I just wasn’t sure he’d be able to stick it out. But all the stars aligned and at the wedding, my father walked me down the aisle, danced with me and was involved in everything he could. My heart was just so full and happy. It made the day complete and so full of love." Can someone please pass us a Costco sized box of Kleenex, stat?

Afterward, cocktail hour kicked off on the patio while the couple snuck off to take a few photos. Guests enjoyed candied bacon and southern favorites like jumbo shrimp cocktail shooters, mini chicken and waffles, and southern BLTs.

Meanwhile back inside, the space was transformed for an unapologetically romantic reception filled with florals.

A long wooden farm table created a statement-making focal point beneath the windows. While petal pink linens paired perfectly with metallic silver linens from BBJ Linen. Fluffy florals and geometric terrarium centerpieces by LMA Designs sat atop each table.

As guests took their seats for dinner, balsamic grape and apple salads began the meal. Entrees served included sweet tea marinated chicken, peppercorn filets with prawns and house-smoked brisket. Yum!

For dessert, Nicole surprised Chris with a jaw-dropping, colorful confectionary creation by Monica Petrone. Which included a custom topper and even a Van Halen fondant cassette tape!

"Monica also made 200 cupcakes in five different flavors that were boxed with stickers on them for our guests to take home," Nicole told us. "They were beautiful and insanely delicious!"

]]> REBEKAH + RALPH’S GLAMOROUS NEW YEAR’S EVE WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/rebekah-ralph-glamorous-new-years-eve-wedding/ Tue, 09 Apr 2019 20:15:12 +0000 https://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=14410 A glamorous New Year's Eve wedding ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building.

Rebekah and Ralph rang in their first year as husband and wife in timeless style with a glamorous New Year's Eve wedding at The Bridge Building.

"I was bartending at Morton's Steakhouse when he and his friend came in before a Mardi Gras party," Rebekah told us. "We just really hit it off, so they skipped their party when my friend and I got off work and hung out with us the rest of the night."

Knowing it was love, Ralph proposed later down the line on the grassy hill where Rebekah accidentally got her car stuck on their first date!

Since their vow exchange topped the priority list for the big day, a rooftop ceremony covered in soft white drapery by Quest Events was just the ticket. And Infinity's dramatic crystal chandeliers were the perfect choice for an icy winter evening.

"We love fireworks, New Year's Eve, and urban Nashville, so we picked the venue based on where we thought we'd see the fireworks best to enjoy the best visual of [the city]," Rebekah told us. "We love the industrial look of The Bridge Building and adore the Nashville skyline view from it."

Guests kept warm during cocktail hour with a drink in hand and noshed on tomato bisque shooters and miniature grilled cheese sandwiches.

For a family-style dinner, entrees included grilled halibut with sun-dried tomato creamed spinach and roasted root vegetable hash, and filet with truffle cheddar crusted brussels sprouts and roasted corn and charred tomato risotto.

Later, the couple danced to "At Last" by Etta James. "It's timeless and gorgeous, and we thought it was played at my parents' wedding," Rebekah told us. "Turns out that was wrong, but oh well!"

For dessert, this sweet pair skipped a traditional wedding cake in favor of a taste of Tennessee. Our Jack Daniel's bread pudding with extra whipped cream!

To wrap up, guests toasted with a glass of champagne ten minutes before the new year and sent the couple off through a tunnel of sparklers as the clock struck twelve. Definitely a Cinderella ending!

Cindy and Steven were married on a crisp, autumn afternoon at the Cathedral of the Incarnation.

Immediately following, they celebrated with friends and family at an undeniably romantic reception with a glamorous twist at The Bridge Building.

Modern string lighting and a striking chandelier set a romantic scene. While slate gray linens paired perfectly with sweet, blush napkins adding dimension and glamour.

Tall, tapered ivory candles sat atop each table. Silver Chiavari chairs provided stylish seating and table numbers calligraphed in silver ink complemented an elegant, mirrored seating chart.

As guests arrived, they enjoyed cocktail hour on the sun-soaked patio with unparalleled views of the Nashville skyline.

They noshed on light bites including ahi tuna baskets, candied bacon deviled eggs, beef and vegetable kabobs, brisket tacos and hot chicken sliders, of course!

After a drink or two and ample sunshine, guests made their way into the Cumberland space for dinner.

Spinach and strawberry salads began the meal and entrees included bourbon glazed salmon with roasted acorn squash and bleu cheese gnocchi gratin, and maple mustard glazed chicken with cheddar cheese and chive mashed potatoes.

And for dessert? Dulce Desserts brought their A game once again with a stunning four-tiered, silver foil-flecked confectionary creation in the palest of hues. We just couldn't take our eyes off of those sugar flowers!

Congratulations, Cindy and Steven!

]]> STEPHANIE + DREW’S MODERN GLAM WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/stephanie-drew-modern-glam-wedding/ Wed, 14 Nov 2018 19:26:38 +0000 https://blog.infinityeventsandcatering.com/?p=11632 A classic wedding ceremony & reception at The Bridge Building with a modern, glamorous twist.

Good food and drinks, and a fun, laid-back experience for guests were Tennessee natives Stephanie and Drew's biggest wishes for their modern glam wedding at The Bridge Building.

Utilizing a color palette of black, white, gold and silver, the overall vibe was simple and classic with a modern twist.

Stephanie and Drew chose to begin the day with a first look on the Pedestrian Bridge. And their precious chocolate labradoodle pup, Pepper, was also in tow!

A sweet ceremony followed, where Pepper was the flower girl! She totally stole our hearts and was dressed for the occasion in a gorgeous floral collar by LMA Designs.

Soaking up the southern sun, cocktail hour followed on the riverfront where guests sipped the couple's signature cocktails. Stephanie opted for The Sinclair, made with vodka, lavender simple syrup and lemon juice served in a collins glass with a lavender sprig. Drew chose The Buck Stops Here, a mule made with your choice of base spirit, ginger beer and a lime wedge served in a collins glass.

Along with their drinks, guests noshed on an array of light bites like ahi tuna baskets, fried green tomatoes, hot chicken sliders and prosciutto-wrapped melon.

After mixing and mingling, the bride and groom made their first entrance as husband and wife. They danced to "You Are The Best Thing" by Ray LaMontagne. Dinner stations were opened shortly after. Guests enjoyed a trio of salads, mashed potatoes and assorted grilled flatbreads stations.

And in honor of fall and its upcoming festivities, the couple opted for a chef-carved roasted turkey breast station served with a cranberry praline glaze. Oh, how we love fall!

For guests that left room for dessert, a golf-themed cake and matching cake pops were up for grabs in honor of Drew's second love.

This was such a fun group of people! So laid back and everyone had so much fun. We even had a few arcade games for guests to play like Pacman and Big Buck Hunter.

To wrap up, the couple was sent off through a sparkler tunnel onto The Honky Tonk Party Express bus with their entire wedding party. Then they drove around Broadway, complete with "NASH" and "VILLE" hats!

Congratulations to Stephanie and Drew!

As seen on The Pink Bride and Zola, Tinsel and Derek's romantic glam wedding day at The Bridge Building was complete with lush florals and gold touches.

After dinner, guests made their way back upstairs for dancing on the rooftop and indulged in a variety of desserts in addition to their two-tiered cake from Dulce Desserts. Cake pops and tartlets were just a few of the other sweet treats up for grabs.
