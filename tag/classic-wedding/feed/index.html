Tala and Vagif said their “I dos” at the Estate at Cherokee Dock with a stunning outdoor ceremony in the Serenity Gardens followed by a beautiful seated dinner in the Lakeview Event Center. 

These two first met during their college years and eventually fell in love. Vagif proposed at Nashville’s Love Circle, a romantic spot that overlooks the entire city. After surprising Tala with a sweet picnic, she said yes!

When it came to planning their wedding, it was important for the couple that their day flowed flawlessly so their only focus could be on sitting back, relaxing, and taking it all in. Accomplishment achieved, if we say so ourselves! Their day went off without a hitch.

Tala stunned in a sweetheart mermaid gown with draped off-the-shoulder sleeves and embroidered lace details. She accessorized with a jeweled hair piece, dainty tennis necklace, and a waltz length veil. Her bridesmaids donned champagne floor-length gowns in different styles, and all the ladies carried matching long-stem bouquets of classic white florals and greenery. Vagif and his groomsmen looked dapper in classic black tuxedos with coordinating white floral boutonnieres.

Following intimate vows in the Serenity Gardens, guests gathered on the patio of the Lakeview Event Center for a cocktail hour featuring an array of hors d’oeuvres prepared by the in-house chefs. Dishes included  ahi tuna in wonton baskets, avocado toast, butternut goat cheese stuffed mushrooms, classic tomato bruschetta, mini beef wellington, and braised short ribs. 

When the reception began, Tala and Vagif were introduced with a special cultural performance by the Al Fursan Dabka and Zaffah Group as guests prepared for a plated dinner service of chicken piccata with grilled asparagus and parmesan risotto and herb salmon with broccolini. 

The space was set with the most beautiful classic design featuring a mix of neutral white and champagne-colored linens, round and rectangle tables, and light wood crossback chairs. The tables were decorated with beautiful floral arrangements full of white blooms and greenery, white taper candles in gold candlesticks, and some elevated flowers to create dimension across the room. String lights were also hung above, cascading to the center of the room to create a warm glow. 

After a sweet first dance to Elvis Presley’s “I Can’t Stop Falling In Love,” Tala and Vagif cut their classic white three-tier cake and opened up the dance floor!

Here’s a look back at Mary and Joe’s classic indoor garden wedding ceremony and reception with a neutral color palette this past January at The Bell Tower.

Mary and Joe, both high school AP English teachers, met teaching at the same school. While working together, they became great friends and were dating other people when they first met, but when they both became single, they took their opportunity to be together! 

The couple was engaged in the beginning of quarantine while renting a romantic cabin in Georgia. They went on a hike one day and Joe let Mary walk ahead for a little ways before he surprised her with the proposal. Mary said she knew it was coming  but didn't know exactly when and she was so surprised in the moment that she forgot to say “Yes!”

When it came to the vision for their day, Mary said she wanted a formal, black tie optional wedding and to feel like a high-end cocktail party with movie premier vibes. 

Mary wore an elegant lace gown with a deep V neckline, a classic waist-length veil, and held a stunning, cascading bouquet full of wild greenery and bright white blooms. Her bridesmaids wore mix-and-match black gowns with smaller versions of her bouquet. Joe and his groomsmen kept the elegant, black tie look going in sharp tuxedos with white boutonnieres. 

The I dos were held in the main space of The Bell Tower in a beautiful ceremony. The aisle was bordered with white rose petals and warm pillar candles, with large standing floral arrangements of greenery and white roses at the entrance and altar.

After Mary and Joe were pronounced as husband and wife,  the guests moved into the covered  patio space for cocktail hour. It was decorated with white paper lanterns, string lights, modern white lounge furniture, trees and plants, and seagrass rugs, giving the space a luxurious, natural feel that tied into their ceremony perfectly. 

While guests mixed and mingled outside, Infinity’s event team flipped the main space for the reception.  The room was set with bright white linens and black chiavari chairs that created the elegant look and feel Mary wanted. Tall floral arrangements stood at the center of each table, full of mixed greenery and white flowers, with smaller arrangements and candles below. Our grand staircase and original wood columns were wrapped in Jackson vine to bring the indoor garden look to life, and the room was lit from above with iron chandeliers from Nashville Lighting & Production. 

For dinner, guests chose from several food stations, including gourmet french fries, sliders, surf & turf, and risotto. And then the dance floor was open to keep the celebration going!

Catie and Brendan’s wedding celebration at The Bell Tower was filled with fresh blue accents, gold details, and a mix of classic and modern decor. 

The couple met at the University of Kentucky in Lexington through mutual friends at a football tailgate, bonding over their love for Kentucky basketball. Catie agreed to a group date with their friends— and the rest is history. 

Brendan proposed in Frankfurt at the Lovers Leap winery, surrounded by family and friends. To celebrate, the couple shared their first bottle of wine together! 

When it came to the big day, Catie and Brendan knew they wanted to incorporate their alma mater’s colors of blue and white. We think it turned out beautifully! 

The wedding began with a beautiful ceremony in the main space, which was decorated with varying gold geometric pendant lights and greenery. The ceremony arch was a modern gold rectangle with greenery and a touch of white flowers.

After they were pronounced husband and wife, the celebration continued to our patio for cocktail hour. The space was decorated with light blue linens accented with white that tied into a green boxwood photo wall decorated with a white balloon arch and custom neon sign. 

While guests mingled over cocktails and hors d'oeuvres, Infinity staff flipped the main space and mezzanine for the reception. Tables were set  with crisp white linens, beautiful blue floral napkins, candles, and gold-framed table numbers. Some featured arrangements of greenery and white flowers in tall gold stands, while others were set with greenery surrounding the candles.

At the end of the night, they played “Dixieland Delight” as the couple went around to say goodbye to the crowd. It was a very fun and memorable evening for all.

Late last summer, Julia + Ivan had a simple, classic wedding at The Bell Tower with sunset-inspired florals and a petal send-off.

Julia, a Nashville native, and Ivan, from Birmingham, began their relationship during their time at Samford University. Ivan later surprised Julia with a sweet proposal as they worked on home improvements one seemingly ordinary morning. Julia told us that she was counting tiles when she realized what was happening!

When it came to planning the big day, they  wanted a simple and classic wedding with room for dancing and spending time with their loved ones. 

The couple could hardly wait to say their “I do’s.” On September 26, Julia wore a lace capped-sleeve dress with a simple updo. She held a bouquet of peach and orange florals as she walked the aisle to her now husband, Ivan, who was dressed in a sleek navy suit with a gray tie. 

For the ceremony, which was officiated by Ivan’s uncle, Nashville Lighting & Production hung a canopy of string lights, and softly glowing candles framed the couple as they said their vows. 

After they were pronounced husband and wife, our team quickly flipped the main space for a dinner buffet of herb chicken, marinated flank steak, grilled squash and zucchini, rosemary roasted new potatoes, and herbed basmati rice. 

Julia and Ivan later shared a first dance to “This Too Shall Pass” by Anderson East before enjoying a toast by Julia’s maid of honor and brother. 

To end the night, family and friends joined together to sing “Sweet Caroline” by Neil Diamond before sending the newlyweds off through a flower petal exit.

Laura and John Robert’s modern classic wedding at Scarritt Bennett and The Bell Tower with icy blues and ivory touches will sure be one to remember.

In this modern-day love story, Laura and John Robert both “swiped right” on Bumble. "On our first date, we talked for hours, only leaving the coffee shop when its employees began to close for the night," Laura told us. "Wanting to continue our time together, we went for ice cream and walked around a popular Nashville neighborhood."

By the end of the night, both Laura and John Robert had the feeling they had finally found their forever person.

When John Robert later decided it was time to put a ring on it, he came to us. On August 24, 2018, he popped the question at The Bridge Building. The Pedestrian Bridge glowed with the words, "Laura, Will You Marry Me?" It just doesn't get sweeter than that!

They began their special day at Scarritt Bennett with lots of love and laughter. The bridal party popped a little bubbly before putting on their classic blue dresses, and the couple shared a touching moment during their first look before the ceremony began.

After the ceremony, guests made their way to The Bell Tower for a fun, dance-filled reception. Upon arrival, they indulged in some yummy hors d'oeuvres including candied bacon, pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, french fry shooters, beef satay, brisket tacos, and hot chicken sliders. Dinner followed with an open buffet featuring a large assortment of selections. Safe to say no one left hungry!

Starting off the evening celebrations, Laura and John Robert shared their first dance to "Perfect" by Ed Sheeran to reminisce on the start of their journey.

After a memorable evening, the couple continued to dance their way through a tunnel of happy guests holding colorful maracas. John Robert gave Laura a final dip before heading off in their vintage blue car.
