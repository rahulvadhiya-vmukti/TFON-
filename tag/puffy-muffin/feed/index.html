Tala and Vagif said their “I dos” at the Estate at Cherokee Dock with a stunning outdoor ceremony in the Serenity Gardens followed by a beautiful seated dinner in the Lakeview Event Center. 

These two first met during their college years and eventually fell in love. Vagif proposed at Nashville’s Love Circle, a romantic spot that overlooks the entire city. After surprising Tala with a sweet picnic, she said yes!

When it came to planning their wedding, it was important for the couple that their day flowed flawlessly so their only focus could be on sitting back, relaxing, and taking it all in. Accomplishment achieved, if we say so ourselves! Their day went off without a hitch.

Tala stunned in a sweetheart mermaid gown with draped off-the-shoulder sleeves and embroidered lace details. She accessorized with a jeweled hair piece, dainty tennis necklace, and a waltz length veil. Her bridesmaids donned champagne floor-length gowns in different styles, and all the ladies carried matching long-stem bouquets of classic white florals and greenery. Vagif and his groomsmen looked dapper in classic black tuxedos with coordinating white floral boutonnieres.

Following intimate vows in the Serenity Gardens, guests gathered on the patio of the Lakeview Event Center for a cocktail hour featuring an array of hors d’oeuvres prepared by the in-house chefs. Dishes included  ahi tuna in wonton baskets, avocado toast, butternut goat cheese stuffed mushrooms, classic tomato bruschetta, mini beef wellington, and braised short ribs. 

When the reception began, Tala and Vagif were introduced with a special cultural performance by the Al Fursan Dabka and Zaffah Group as guests prepared for a plated dinner service of chicken piccata with grilled asparagus and parmesan risotto and herb salmon with broccolini. 

The space was set with the most beautiful classic design featuring a mix of neutral white and champagne-colored linens, round and rectangle tables, and light wood crossback chairs. The tables were decorated with beautiful floral arrangements full of white blooms and greenery, white taper candles in gold candlesticks, and some elevated flowers to create dimension across the room. String lights were also hung above, cascading to the center of the room to create a warm glow. 

After a sweet first dance to Elvis Presley’s “I Can’t Stop Falling In Love,” Tala and Vagif cut their classic white three-tier cake and opened up the dance floor!

]]> Sarah + Joseph’s Dreamy Indoor Garden Wedding https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/sarah-josephs-dreamy-indoor-garden-wedding/ Wed, 04 Jan 2023 02:00:52 +0000 https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/?p=32848 A spring wedding ceremony and reception with indoor garden inspiration at the Bridge Building.

Sarah and Joseph celebrated their nuptials with a dreamy rooftop ceremony and reception full of whimsical florals at The Bridge Building. 

These Nashville natives officially met on the first day of their junior year of high school and, with the help of some mutual friends, they made it official over the holidays a few months later. Their first date was December 2, 2014, and they’ve been together ever since! 

When it came time to pop the question, Joseph had a big surprise planned. During a trip to Maui, Hawaii, to visit Sarah’s family, he set up a “family” photoshoot and got down on one knee on December 21, 2021. To no one’s surprise, Sarah said “yes” and the wedding planning began!

It was important to Sarah and Joseph to have a fun wedding day celebrating with their friends and family. “We fell in love with The Bridge Building as soon as we saw it. It's actually the only venue we toured!” Sarah shared with us. “We are from Nashville and it's where we fell in love, but most of our guests were from out of town, so we knew we wanted our wedding to feel like a big Nashville party!”

When her wedding day arrived, Sarah was dressed in an A-line gown with a deep V neckline and beaded details, classic white heels, and an elegant updo with a jeweled side comb. Her best gals were in silky matching dresses in blush pink and held bright pastel bouquets. 

Leading up to Joseph’s most memorable moment, which was their first kiss as Mr. and Mrs., this pair first locked eyes as Sarah walked down the aisle. They said their vows on the rooftop in a minimalist yet dreamy ceremony with bright pastel floral arrangements and a downtown view. Sarah and Joseph also held a unity ceremony where they boxed a bottle of wine along with letters from their parents and loved ones to be opened on their future anniversaries. 

Afterwards, guests made their way to the riverfront to snack on passed appetizers of bacon wrapped dates, fried mac and cheese, pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, and tequila infused watermelon cubes while Infinity staff finished setting the Cumberland space for the reception. 

The room looked bright and dreamy as it was set with classic white linens, colorful pastel florals hung individually from the industrial beams, and warm Edison bulb lighting. On the tables sat printed custom blue and white dinner menus, homemade lavender shortbread cookies topped with edible flowers, large whimsical centerpieces of roses and wisteria, and interactive table numbers so guests could leave sweet messages for the newlyweds.  

Once guests found their seats, a plated dinner service began followed by a traditional first dance to "Forever Like That" by Ben Rector, toasts from loved ones, and the cutting of the cake. The couple also included an anniversary dance to honor all the married couples in attendance! 

As the night came to an end, guests lined up under the bridge with the University of Alabama shakers in honor of the couple's alma mater while Sarah and Joseph shared a final secret dance on the rooftop.

Talor and AJ celebrated their marriage with a timeless wedding day at the historic Bell Tower filled with classic style, black decor, and white florals.

The two met when Talor took her little brother to baseball practice and AJ was the coach. They hit it off, and eventually AJ proposed in Talor’s backyard after a Titans game.

The couple started their wedding day with a ceremony at The Bell Tower with glamorous chandeliers, soft string lighting, dramatic black drapes, and white flower arrangements with greenery, creating a timeless and classic wedding ceremony design with moody vibes.

Talor stunned in a beautiful strapless column gown with a chapel train and large bow in the back. She wore her hair in a sleek low bun with pearl earrings and tied it all together with elegant fingerless gloves and a classic white hand-tied bouquet. Talor's bridesmaids wore stunning, one-shoulder black gowns and held matching bouquets. While the guys wore sharp black tuxedos from Jos A Banks.

After Talor and AJ said "I do," our main space was flipped for the reception. The room was set with a stage in the corner and a traditional king's table with space to dance in the middle, surrounded by round tables dressed in black linens with black chiavari chairs and chandeliers above. There was also a beautiful white neon sign customized with their newly-shared last name, which was the perfect way to highlight the chic couple.

Talor and AJ shared a sweet first dance to “Another” by Adam Doleac, and as the evening went on, Talor had a wardrobe change into an above-the-knee reception dress with white cowboy boots to ensure she could dance the night away! T-Rac, the Tennessee Titans mascot, even made a guest appearance and hit the dance floor with Talor and AJ’s loved ones! Such a fun nod to their engagement!

Allie and Ben's whimsical fall wedding at The Bridge Building was nothing short of a romantic autumn dream.

With a high school beginning, a birthday proposal in a charming Texas cabin and an amazing couple, we could hardly wait to get their wedding started!

Allie and Ben's wedding day began with a sweet first look. To the couple, it was most important that their guests had a fun time and truly enjoyed a Nashville experience.

Their candlelit ceremony was held at sunset on The Bridge Building rooftop with a whimsical arch of greenery by Echoes of Eden Florals at the altar. Afterward, guests took to the riverfront for cocktail hour and enjoyed a libation or two before heading inside for dinner.

For the reception, the main space was set with white and beige linens, string lights and centerpieces of ivory florals and greenery. Dinner stations opened later in the evening, complete with a trio of salads, build your own BBQ sliders, a chicken and waffles station, and a mashed potato station.

Before the day was complete with an epic sparkler exit on the riverfront, Allie and Ben shared their most memorable moment of the day, a private last dance on the rooftop.
