The Bell Tower provided the perfect setting for Kristen and Guido’s romantic, flower-filled wedding reception. Decorated with delicate gold details, lush floral arrangements in hues of white and pink with verdant greenery, the soft glow of candles, and white draping from the beams, their celebration exuded elegance.

But before we dive into the splendor of their reception, let’s rewind to where it all began – the love story of Kristen and Guido. Childhood sweethearts, their bond stood the test of time and distance, only to be rekindled later in life.

Guido whisked Kristen away to his native country, Italy, where amid the breathtaking beauty of Lake Como, he asked her to be his forever with the help of his parents.

Fast forward to their wedding day, where the Bell Tower served as a symbol of their enduring love and the beginning of their new chapter together.

As guests entered the main space, they were greeted by cascading white drapery with a grand chandelier at the center, and rose-filled floral arrangements with the delicate scent of pink and white blooms mingling with the warm glow of candles.

During cocktail hour, guests sipped on signature cocktails— an Oaxaca Old Fashioned and Raspberry Moscow Mules —while eating an array of hors d’oeuvres made by the in-house culinary team. Selections included candied bacon, Key West crab cakes, and classic tomato bruschetta.

They then found their seats at tables set with elegant gold accents and a mix of smaller floral arrangements in bud vases, with textured pink napkins and custom menu cards at each place setting. They dined on a balsamic caprese salad and their choice of filet mignon, rigatoni primavera, or pan seared dayboat fish.

Amid laughter and heartfelt toasts, Kristen and Guido danced the night away, their love shining brighter than ever. Surrounded by family and friends, they celebrated not only their union but also the journey that brought them to this moment of joy and happiness.

As the evening drew to a close, the newlyweds left the Bell Tower hand in hand, ready to embark on the adventure of marriage together.

May Kristen and Guido’s love continue to bloom and flourish and their journey together be filled with countless moments of joy, laughter, and unwavering love!

Gage and Brian said “I do” last year with an indoor garden-inspired wedding full of light pink roses and sage green details at The Bridge Building. 

After first meeting online in 2014 and getting through the trials of long distance, Brian proposed to Gage in Toronto with a planned photoshoot while recreating one of their first ever dates at the zoo!

When it came to the big day, Brian and Gage wanted to feel like they were guests at their own wedding. With our team of professionals making sure that everything ran smoothly, we can proudly say we accomplished just that!  

The ceremony was held in the Cumberland space of The Bridge Building with Edison bulb lighting, a floral ring with lots of greenery and pink roses, and a view of Downtown Nashville. With Gage and Brian’s six groomsmaids standing at their sides in sage green dresses, and an escorted entrance with their moms, it was a ceremony like no other. 

Following the vows, guests made their way outside to the riverfront walkway to enjoy some fresh air along the river and a sip of cabernet as Infinity staff flipped the main space to ready it for the reception. 

The space transformed from an intimate ceremony to a romantic garden-inspired reception with tables of ivory and beige linens, each decorated with floral centerpieces of greenery and pink roses, and wood crossback chairs. The head table had a greenery runner with mixed candle lighting, and each setting had sage green place cards for the guests. 

When guests found their seats, a plated dinner was served consisting of honey balsamic chicken with roasted heirloom carrots and a citrus rice pilaf, herb roasted pork loin with roasted red pepper Brussel sprouts and smashed new potatoes, and smoked brisket with grilled corn and a black eyed pea salad.  

Once plates were clean and the cake was cut, the night ended with lots of fun and a sweet first dance to Blake Shelton’s “Nobody but You.” Gage and Brian were sent away under the pedestrian bridge, and the rest is history!

Meagan and Brian were married in style with a warm romantic wedding at The Bell Tower.

Since Brian is in the United States Air Force, this gorgeous couple met through mutual friends in Destin, Fl. while he was stationed there. He later proposed and had all of their friends and family flown in to celebrate. Too sweet!

For the big day, they opted for an overall warm feeling with gold tones, greenery, and candlelight covering almost every surface. While bright pops of fuschia brought the space to life and delicate white draping by our friends at Quest Events turned it into a heavenly dream scene.

Infinity's modern string light backdrop added a contemporary twist to their altar, and greenery crept up the wall behind it for added drama.

Meagan and Brian's first look took place here too! It was simply stunning and the perfect way to kick off the day and ease the nerves. When the time came to say "I do," Meagan descended The Bell Tower's towering staircase with her dad by her side.

After they made it official, guests made their way to the side patio for sipping and mingling. For dinner guests helped themselves to a variety of Infinity stations, including a trio of salads, baked potatoe, gourmet grilled cheese, and build-your-own BBQ stations. Yum!

Guests freely perused the Tasting Room and its impressive array of over 100 handpicked whiskeys and bourbons. This was definitely the groomsmen's favorite hangout spot of the night!

For dessert, family friend Angelino Cakes flew in specifically for the occasion. They created one heck of a groom's cake in the shape of a hyperrealistic armadillo à la Steel Magnolias, as well as a stylish tiered white cake.
