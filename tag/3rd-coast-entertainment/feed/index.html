Last spring, Jordan and Zach had an elegant rustic wedding at The Bell Tower with blue details and a feather send-off. 

Jordan, from Kentucky, and Zach, from Kansas, began their relationship with several double taps on their Instagram feed. After three months of Facetime and phone calls, Zach flew out to meet Jordan face to face, and the rest is history. 

Zach popped the question outside a family member’s home as the couple was heading for a night out. He said he forgot his wallet inside but grabbed the ring instead, and then he got down on one knee right as it began to snow. To him, this moment felt like the “perfect time.” 

“We actually toured The Bridge Building first and fell in love, but they were going to be closed for construction,” Jordan told us. “We loved everything about Infinity Hospitality, so then we found The Bell Tower. The old hardwood, the exposed beams, it was EXACTLY what we were looking for.”

It was important to the couple that their family and friends were able to enjoy the big day as one, and The Bell Tower helped them accomplish just that! The couple could hardly wait to say their “I dos.” 

On March 6th, Jordan wore a fitted lace gown with long, loose curls. She held a large bouquet of ivory and blue florals with a pin of her loved ones attached to the stems. Zach, on the other hand, rocked a deep blue suit with a duck feather bowtie.

For the ceremony, Jordan and Zach shared traditional vows and Jordan’s grandfather’s Bible that was used in wedding ceremonies before hers. 

After they were pronounced husband and wife, our team quickly flipped the main space for a dinner service full of Southern comforts, from smoked baby back ribs to grilled corn and whipped potatoes! 

Jordan and Zach later shared their first dance to “More of You” by Chris Stapleton before leading a traditional Grand March to get everyone up and dancing for the reception. 

Before the guests sent the newlyweds on their way, they grabbed a custom koozie and a Nothing Bundt Cake bundtlet.

Brennan and Kyle said "I do" surrounded by a small group of family and friends last fall at The Bridge Building. 

Brennan, a Nashvillian, met Mississippi native Kyle in Music City during a casual volleyball game. The pair started dating, and then Kyle popped the question in their home with a bundle of blooms and their beloved dogs. 

When the pair started planning their big day, they decided they wanted a relaxed micro wedding filled with good food, and, above all, they wanted everyone to have a good time. 

Their October wedding day began with a sweet rooftop ceremony under white drapery and glowing string lights. They wanted to keep it intimate, so they decided to forego wedding parties in order to focus on enjoying time with each of their guests. 

Florals were a very important part of their decor. The couple exchanged vows in front of a wooden arch adorned with a mix of greenery and pink and burgundy blooms that was perfectly coordinated to match Brennan’s bridal bouquet of dahlias, ranunculus, and champagne roses. 

After the ceremony, guests enjoyed a few hors d'oeuvres and relaxed during the cocktail hour until it was time for the seated dinner. The tables were set with white linens, and the tent was lit with soft purple hues. 

Guests filled their stomachs with Creole shrimp and grits, mustard-glazed chicken with carrots and whipped potatoes, and a yummy ribeye with grilled asparagus and Swiss potato gratin. 

Following dinner, the DJ  set the tone for the rest of the night, and guests danced the night away!

Today we're jumping back to Jonna and Brian's New Year's Eve wedding! Their black and gold style at The Bridge Building was the perfect way to head into 2020.

A little over six years ago, Jonna was introduced to Brian by her best friend, who at the time was dating Brian's cousin. Once Brian was sure he had found the one, he got down on one knee and popped the question. The rest is history!

When asked why she chose The Bridge Building, Jonna told us they used to talk their dog for a walk along downtown Nashville's Riverfront Park and down the pedestrian bridge, which is attached the venue.

"We walked the stairs of our wedding venue at least 100 times before we toured it. We actually took our very first picture together in Nashville along the bridge. When we found out we could get married somewhere so special to us, the decision was very easy!" she explained.

For the big day, The Bridge Building was transformed with glamorous touches of black and gold. In the Cumberland space hung an array of string lights with Nashville Lighting & Production's Maria chandelier glowing center stage.

Following Jonna and Brian's sweet ceremony, our team quickly flipped the main space for dinner while guests enjoyed the couple's signature cocktail, a Moscow mule, and some savory bites.

Once the reception was set, dinner service began. Guests filled their plates with slow-roasted prime rib, grilled cheese, street tacos, gourmet french fries, and more. Yum!

In lieu of wedding favors, Jonna and Brian chose to make a donation to St. Jude Children's Hospital in honor of their guests—a thoughtful and sustainable choice.

And what better way to end a New Year's Eve wedding? Sparklers along the river after the fireworks, of course!

New York City dwellers Nicole and Chris envisioned a romantic glam wedding at The Bell Tower. With gold touches, soft blush florals, and flickering candlelight, we're happy to say that the end result was all that and then some!

After get off work late one night, Nicole arrived home to flowers on the table and a pizza on the stove. "I put on my PJs and was pretty far into a pepperoni slice when Chris walked into the room, put on 'I Wanna Grow Old With You' from The Wedding Singer, and got down on one knee," Nicole told us. "I never wanted a big proposal. He knows my favorite place is at home and New York pizza is the best! It was perfect for us and a wonderful memory."

As planning began, they knew they wanted a destination wedding to make travel easier for their families. "Once we decided on Nashville, we started researching wedding venues," Nicole said. "We toured The Bridge Building and The Bell Tower, but once we walked into The Bell Tower, our minds were made up. It's gorgeous, open and everything we wanted in a space."

Starting with a bright ceremony between the windows on a perfect spring afternoon, there wasn't a dry eye in the house as Nicole began her walk down the aisle with her dad by her side.

"It was really important to include my father into our day as much as possible," Nicole told us. "He is eight years into his diagnosis with Alzheimer’s, and at the rehearsal, I just wasn’t sure he’d be able to stick it out. But all the stars aligned and at the wedding, my father walked me down the aisle, danced with me and was involved in everything he could. My heart was just so full and happy. It made the day complete and so full of love." Can someone please pass us a Costco sized box of Kleenex, stat?

Afterward, cocktail hour kicked off on the patio while the couple snuck off to take a few photos. Guests enjoyed candied bacon and southern favorites like jumbo shrimp cocktail shooters, mini chicken and waffles, and southern BLTs.

Meanwhile back inside, the space was transformed for an unapologetically romantic reception filled with florals.

A long wooden farm table created a statement-making focal point beneath the windows. While petal pink linens paired perfectly with metallic silver linens from BBJ Linen. Fluffy florals and geometric terrarium centerpieces by LMA Designs sat atop each table.

As guests took their seats for dinner, balsamic grape and apple salads began the meal. Entrees served included sweet tea marinated chicken, peppercorn filets with prawns and house-smoked brisket. Yum!

For dessert, Nicole surprised Chris with a jaw-dropping, colorful confectionary creation by Monica Petrone. Which included a custom topper and even a Van Halen fondant cassette tape!

"Monica also made 200 cupcakes in five different flavors that were boxed with stickers on them for our guests to take home," Nicole told us. "They were beautiful and insanely delicious!"
