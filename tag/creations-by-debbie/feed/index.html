Gage and Brian said “I do” last year with an indoor garden-inspired wedding full of light pink roses and sage green details at The Bridge Building. 

After first meeting online in 2014 and getting through the trials of long distance, Brian proposed to Gage in Toronto with a planned photoshoot while recreating one of their first ever dates at the zoo!

When it came to the big day, Brian and Gage wanted to feel like they were guests at their own wedding. With our team of professionals making sure that everything ran smoothly, we can proudly say we accomplished just that!  

The ceremony was held in the Cumberland space of The Bridge Building with Edison bulb lighting, a floral ring with lots of greenery and pink roses, and a view of Downtown Nashville. With Gage and Brian’s six groomsmaids standing at their sides in sage green dresses, and an escorted entrance with their moms, it was a ceremony like no other. 

Following the vows, guests made their way outside to the riverfront walkway to enjoy some fresh air along the river and a sip of cabernet as Infinity staff flipped the main space to ready it for the reception. 

The space transformed from an intimate ceremony to a romantic garden-inspired reception with tables of ivory and beige linens, each decorated with floral centerpieces of greenery and pink roses, and wood crossback chairs. The head table had a greenery runner with mixed candle lighting, and each setting had sage green place cards for the guests. 

When guests found their seats, a plated dinner was served consisting of honey balsamic chicken with roasted heirloom carrots and a citrus rice pilaf, herb roasted pork loin with roasted red pepper Brussel sprouts and smashed new potatoes, and smoked brisket with grilled corn and a black eyed pea salad.  

Once plates were clean and the cake was cut, the night ended with lots of fun and a sweet first dance to Blake Shelton’s “Nobody but You.” Gage and Brian were sent away under the pedestrian bridge, and the rest is history!

Here’s a look at Kendra and Dwight's romantic and rustic fall wedding day at The Bell Tower. A day filled with a lot of love and sweet, personalized details. 

This couple met in Nashville, working at Johnny Rockets in Opry Mills. After some persuasion, Dwight grew on Kendra and they started dating.

After four years, they got engaged in Cincinnati, Ohio, while on a skiing trip, and Dwight popped the question at a restaurant they went to!

When they started planning their wedding, they knew they wanted a fall, rustic-feeling wedding. This ultimately led them to The Bell Tower. It was our history and cozy, rustic vibe that sealed the deal for Kendra and Dwight. Added bonus? A select number of mahogany chiavari chairs, round tables, china, silverware, and glassware are included in our venue rental.

Kendra looked stunning in her sweetheart gown with lace and bead detailing from Eddy K Bridal. The bridesmaids wore burgundy dresses, while the bridesman and groomsmen wore coordinating black suits with burgundy ties. The groom stood out from his best men with an embossed suit jacket that had a satin lapel.

Kendra and Dwight held their ceremony between our tall, arched windows. The aisle led to  a beautiful hexagonal arch decorated with greenery and white, red, and soft pink flowers by Creations by Debbie. Cascading string lights were hung overhead from our original wood beams that created a warm glow against the exposed wood, the perfect touch for a romantic fall wedding. 

The space was then transformed for the reception, using their ceremony arch as the backdrop to their sweetheart table! The tables were decorated with white linens, burgundy napkins, and floral centerpieces of greenery with red roses.

The couple included several personalized touches throughout the day, such as a black and white photo wall to showcase their relationship, a framed quote used as a guestbook, and a signature cocktail—the Green Hawaiian.

Jacqui and Caleb's classic wine red wedding at The Bell Tower was full of thoughtful details and so much love.

Their story began after meeting at a bar in Franklin and continued with a pedestrian bridge proposal right in Downtown Nashville.

For the big day, Jacqui was dressed in a classic white gown with an open back and an all-white rose bouquet by Creations by Debbie. Her bridesmaids wore long wine red dresses with a refined up-do.

Jacqui and Caleb began their day with a sweet first look outside of The Bell Tower before saying their vows between the tall arched windows. At the altar, sat a wood arch adorned with roses and sprigs of greenery.

After the ceremony, guests sipped on cocktails on the patio while we transformed the main space into their perfect fall reception. The tables were set with white and wine red linens, gold lanterns, and a balsamic grape and apple salad at each place setting.

Once guests found their seats, a delectable plated dinner service began. Dinner included bourbon-glazed salmon with grilled asparagus and parmesan risotto, as well as roasted pork loin with steamed broccoli and cheddar cheese mashed potatoes, and chicken piccata with grilled vegetables and rosemary roasted potatoes.

Jacqui and Caleb completed their day with a traditional bouquet and garter toss from the mezzanine before busting a move on the dance floor. For an extra thoughtful touch and to keep the kiddos busy, there were clipboards for coloring, too.
