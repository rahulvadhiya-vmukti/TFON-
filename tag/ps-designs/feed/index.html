We're wishing Tali and Josh a happy five-year anniversary! Their elegant and easygoing celebration at The Bell Tower was what spring wedding dreams are made of.

As they were planning their wedding, they knew they wanted a unique spring look with an emphasis on fun.

On the big day, Tali looked stunning in her sweetheart neckline dress with a floral lace pattern, and she wore her sister-in-law Rachel’s veil as a sweet way to incorporate family traditions. The structured bridal bouquet featured ivory peonies, blush pink roses, and a few lilac accents.

Josh kept it classic in his blue tux with monogrammed sleeves and gold fox cufflinks that showed off his fun personality and sense of style.

The bride and groom enjoyed a quiet first look at Vanderbilt before heading to the wedding venue to sign their beautifully personalized ketubah—a Jewish marriage contract.

As the ceremony began, they walked down an aisle lined with lanterns and ivory rose petals in a criss-cross pattern to stand together under a romantic chuppah featuring a soft, organic structure made up of small trees, delicate white blooms, and gold drapery from Nashville Audio Visual. It was a showstopper!

After the ceremony, guests made their way to the side patio and mezzanine to enjoy a cocktail hour with passed hors d'oeuvres while Infinity staff transformed the main space for the reception.

As the newlyweds were announced, they headed straight to the dance floor to share their first dance to Ed Sheeran's "Thinking Out Loud." Then it was time for emotional father-daughter and mother-son dances before the big cake cutting and blessings before the meal.

Long wooden tables for the wedding parties were filled with gorgeous floral arrangements—both tall glass arrangements and smaller clusters of roses and lilies in square glass containers—green pillar candles, and silver and gold satin napkins.

After dinner, it was time for the joyful hora, which kicked off a night of dancing. The couple refueled their guests with a late-night grilled cheese station to keep the party going, and the celebration ended with a flower petal send-off.

Congratulations to these two on their five years of wedded bliss! Keep scrolling for some elegant spring wedding inspiration filled with ivory, gold, and silver details.

BLOOMING INTO SUMMER

OUR FAVORITE SUMMER WEDDING FLOWERS

As we jump into the new season, we wanted to kick it off with some of our favorite summer wedding flowers!

From bridal bouquets to reception centerpieces, florals are an essential part of any wedding. It is important to pay attention to which flowers are in season and which are not to get the freshest look and, not to mention, the biggest bang for your buck!

Keep scrolling for a round-up of our favorite summer wedding flowers from real weddings.

1. Sunflowers

First and foremost, we have the beloved sunflower at the top of the list!

This bright yellow floral brings light to any arrangement. Take a peak at how Marie and Samantha incorporated this fun pop of color into their wedding day.

Florals by Forget Me Not Designs and LMA Designs. Photos by Melanie Grady and Details Nashville.

2. Sea Holly

More commonly known as blue thistles, sea holly adds a touch of whimsy to any arrangement. With such a unique look, this bloom is a standout addition to any summer or fall wedding.

Forget Me Not Designs helped Kelley + Nate incorporate these eccentric perennials into their vintage-inspired Fourth of July wedding, while Rose Hill Flowers placed them in bouquets for Lauren + Grant's classic summer wedding.

Photos by Cody & Allison Photography, Amber Beckham, Jon Reindl Photography, SheHeWe Photography, and Details Nashville. Florals by Forget Me Not Designs, Rose Hill Flowers, Enchanted Florist, and LMA Designs.

3. Hydrangeas

Hydrangeas effortlessly add dimension and fill any arrangement with an extra soft touch. Whether entwined with greenery or on their own, we love how they bring elegance into any space.

Varying hues of blush and ivory hydrangeas set the scene for Ashley + Justin's ultra-romantic summer wedding at The Bridge Building, while all white arrangements for Amanda + Hunter's chic wedding at The Bell Tower created a classic, timeless atmosphere.

Photos by Nashville Wedding Collection and Janelle Elise Photography. Florals by PS Designs and Forget Me Not Designs.

4. Garden Roses

These many-petaled, cupped blooms are larger and lusher than standard roses, making them a good substitute for the out-of-season peony.

Bonus? Roses are said to thrive in hot, humid weather. They open up to the sun and can handle hot temperatures with grace.

Garden roses from LMA Designs added a romantic vibe to Maddie + Johnny's sweet summer wedding at The Bridge Building. They also help add dimension, as seen in Amy's show-stopping bouquet by Haute Flower Boutique for her pup-inspired wedding.

Photo by Rae Marshall and Julie Paisley. Florals by LMA Designs and Haute Flower Boutique.

5. Zinnias

Zinnias add a burst of color to any event. Varying in color and size, this floral is extremely versatile and can create stunning looks that stand out from traditional arrangements.

These buds flourish in the summer and are actually one of the easiest flowers to grow if you want to test your green thumb.

Fun fact: The zinnia was the first flower to grow aboard the International Space Station. That's right, zinnias can grow and blossom in microgravity!

Photos by Eden Ingle Photo.

MEET ME AT THE ALTAR

OUR FAVORITE CEREMONY DECOR IDEAS

Spring has finally sprung, and we couldn't possibly be happier!

With wedding season just around the corner, we're celebrating the return of warmer days by serving up some of our favorite ceremony looks that will inspire you to head down the aisle in style.

On the big day, a large majority of the magic happens down the aisle and at the altar. Unless we're talking open bar, the ceremony is where it all goes down and there's no turning back; that is unless you're Julia Roberts in a Garry Marshall movie.

Although couples often prefer to keep them short and sweet these days, the ceremony is the most important and most photographed part of the wedding day and should never be overlooked during the planning process.

Besides officially getting hitched and all that fun stuff, you also have a chance to wow your guests and define your wedding style in one fell swoop by making your ceremony space your own. And that's an impressive feat before cocktail hour!

From suspended floral rings to string light backdrops, our couples are always raising the bar just a little higher by elevating their altars and aisles with chic creative decor and accents as unique as their love.

Scroll on to get inspired by some of our favorite innovative ceremony ideas from real weddings. Try to pick just one, we dare ya!

1. Fluffy forals.

We know what you're thinking. Florals for spring? Groundbreaking. But hear us out!

Ashley and Justin incorporated a variety of one-of-a-kind floral decor elements by our friendor, PS Designs, that totally transformed their lavish, shabby chic ceremony at The Bridge Building into a lush, romantic oasis.

Overflowing florals in rustic urns with a custom floral arch framed the altar and rose petals were scattered down the aisle. A custom floral ring dripping with hydrangeas was suspended above for even more drama.

And the cherry on top? Airy, blush draping by Quest Nashville pulled it all together beautifully. We think Miranda Priestly would be proud.

Photos by Nashville Wedding Collection.

2. Riverfront magic.

If you're a minimalist, are looking for ways to trim your decor budget, or simply adore a certain spot in your city, do like Taylor and Baron and leave it to the scenery!

Their sun-drenched riverfront ceremony at The Bridge Building utilized the venue's unparalleled views of downtown Nashville as their backdrop.

Photos by Cannon Weddings.

3. Striking romance.

Rachel and Kevin tied the knot with a strikingly romantic spring wedding at The Bell Tower. Their luxe, garden-inspired ceremony set the tone for one epic evening.

Thanks to our friends at Quest, romantic curtain-style draping provided a stunning ceremony backdrop alongside a custom whimsical floral arch from Forget Me Not Designs. Varying styles of lanterns and pillar candles were scattered down the aisle while gold Chiavari chairs and chandeliers added a feeling of luxury.

And we aren't the only ones in love! Catch their feature on The Pink Bride here.

Photos by Rachel Moore.

4. Vintage-inspired.

Erin and Seth's vintage-inspired riverfront ceremony at The Bridge Building transformed the industrial space into chic, southern soirée.

Tall floral arrangements from Enchanted Florist flanked their ceremony altar. While delicate bouquets marked each white-washed Chiavari chair from Liberty Party Rental. All with that unforgettable backdrop, Nashville.

Photos by Details Nashville.

5. Rooftop secret garden.

Tammy and Jeff's idyllic rooftop ceremony at The Bridge Building was inspired by the illustrious wedding featured in the hit blockbuster film, Twilight, and makes us swoon every single time.

Alex Westerhouse of Front Porch Farms and Westerhouse Weddings brought the space to life with enchanting white florals lining the aisle and a mixed greenery arch. And all to match Tammy's dramatic gown with embroidered florals! Obsessed.

Photos by SheHeWe.

6. Tradition.

Sarah and Daniel incorporated tradition into their gorgeous outdoor ceremony at OZ Arts Nashville. A hand-stitched canopy hung over a custom built chuppah from PS Designs.

Photos by Frozen Exposure.

7. Whimsical romance.

Color us obsessed. Lizzy and Clayton's bright romantic wedding ceremony at The Bell Tower featured vibrant florals and whimsical greenery.

J. Kent Freeman Floral Design teamed up with our production team to elevate our classic string light backdrop, and what can we say, we're in love!

See their feature on StyleBlueprint here.
