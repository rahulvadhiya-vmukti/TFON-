Here’s a look at Kendra and Dwight's romantic and rustic fall wedding day at The Bell Tower. A day filled with a lot of love and sweet, personalized details. 

This couple met in Nashville, working at Johnny Rockets in Opry Mills. After some persuasion, Dwight grew on Kendra and they started dating.

After four years, they got engaged in Cincinnati, Ohio, while on a skiing trip, and Dwight popped the question at a restaurant they went to!

When they started planning their wedding, they knew they wanted a fall, rustic-feeling wedding. This ultimately led them to The Bell Tower. It was our history and cozy, rustic vibe that sealed the deal for Kendra and Dwight. Added bonus? A select number of mahogany chiavari chairs, round tables, china, silverware, and glassware are included in our venue rental.

Kendra looked stunning in her sweetheart gown with lace and bead detailing from Eddy K Bridal. The bridesmaids wore burgundy dresses, while the bridesman and groomsmen wore coordinating black suits with burgundy ties. The groom stood out from his best men with an embossed suit jacket that had a satin lapel.

Kendra and Dwight held their ceremony between our tall, arched windows. The aisle led to  a beautiful hexagonal arch decorated with greenery and white, red, and soft pink flowers by Creations by Debbie. Cascading string lights were hung overhead from our original wood beams that created a warm glow against the exposed wood, the perfect touch for a romantic fall wedding. 

The space was then transformed for the reception, using their ceremony arch as the backdrop to their sweetheart table! The tables were decorated with white linens, burgundy napkins, and floral centerpieces of greenery with red roses.

The couple included several personalized touches throughout the day, such as a black and white photo wall to showcase their relationship, a framed quote used as a guestbook, and a signature cocktail—the Green Hawaiian.

Today we're looking back on Maddie and Jordan's moody, modern fall wedding day at The Bridge Building, and trust us— it's what wedding dreams are made of.

From the moment we met this pair, we could see their love! Celebrating the next chapter of their lives with a fun mix-and-mingle atmosphere with lovely vibes was most important to them, and we couldn't wait to get started.

The big day began with a moving first look in downtown Nashville, and they could hardly wait to see each other in their wedding day attire. Jordan rocked a pair of Christian Louboutin shoes, in addition to a feathered bowtie. As for Maddie, she chose a star-embroidered gown by Rue De Seine. But the style didn't stop with these two! Instead of bouquets, Maddie's bridesmaids donned floral necklaces, and we're obsessed! What a unique alternative to the traditional bridesmaid bouquet.

Their heartfelt ceremony took place below a tropical greenery ring with a candlelit aisle and not a dry eye in sight.

After Maddie and Jordan said their "I dos," guests enjoyed beer and wine on the rooftop while the main space was being transformed for the reception. Black linens from Music City Tents were perfect for the moody atmosphere, along with a modern show-stopping cake by Baked in Nashville and a fun photo booth for added personality. 

Shortly after, food stations opened for dinner with a trio of salads to start. Guests then chose from selections of assorted grilled flatbreads, chicken and waffles, and more. When they'd had enough to eat, everyone hit the dance floor with the lovely newlyweds and made memories of a lifetime!

Lauren and Grant celebrated their love with a classic summer wedding full of soft blue hues and dainty details.

This sweet couple met in true Nashville style on a Sunday afternoon at the well known Dawg House Saloon during the NHL playoffs. Once they locked eyes, Grant made the first move— and just a year later, he found himself down on one knee asking Lauren to be his wife!

"We always knew we wanted the ceremony to be at Lauren's childhood church, Christ Church Cathedral. Pretty quickly, we knew The Bell Tower was what we wanted for the reception," Grant told us. "We only looked at four places total but couldn't forget the beauty of The Bell Tower."

They started their big day with a sweet first look before their ceremony.

"We both felt like we needed those few minutes of alone time together before we started the celebration," Lauren told us. "It helped ease the nerves for the rest of the day!"

While Lauren and Grant said their vows, we set up The Bell Tower for their classic summer wedding reception. The room was decorated with white and powder blue linens, acrylic details, whimsical floral arrangements, and loads of greenery on each table.

As guests arrived, they enjoyed a premium bar of beer and wine on the patio, in addition to a handful of savory bites. Selections included pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, fried green tomatoes, bleu cheese stuffed bacon-wrapped dates, brisket tacos, and miniature chicken and waffles. But the food didn't stop there!

Before Lauren and Grant shared their first dance to "All I'll Ever Need" by Anderson East, guests filled their plates from the dinner buffet. Fun fact: they chose their first dance song while on a road trip together just six weeks into their relationship!

It was important to Lauren and Grant that all of their guests had a good time and danced. Looking back, it seems that they did just that! With memories to share for a lifetime, guests sent-off the newlyweds through a tunnel of sparklers.

Allison and Ben had a classic plum wedding ceremony and reception at The Bell Tower full of all things fun!

This pair met in 2018 while Allison worked at Monroe Carell Jr. Children's Hospital in Nashville and Ben was in Huntsville, Ala. Allison wasn't too interested in trying a long-distance relationship, however, they decided to give it a shot. And thank goodness they did!

Their first date was supposed to be at Arrington Vineyards, but due to weather conditions, they settled on something different. Since this location was on their "bucket list," Ben thought it would be just the place for a sweet proposal. With a big surprise in store, Ben made sure to have a photographer ready to capture the moment. The couple enjoyed a wine tasting, a simple picnic, and then one life-changing question.

When Allison and Ben began looking for venues, they decided on the classic look of The Bell Tower. They also told us that they were "excited to be able to have the ceremony and reception all in one place." A bonus for sure!

While the Infinity team set up the main space with help from our friendors, Allison shared a first look with not only her now hubby, but with her dad and brother as well. Nothing gets you ready for the day like pure reactions from loved ones, especially if you're in your mom's wedding dress! Which Allison had redesigned by Karen Hendrix Couture.

Later during the ceremony, Allison and Ben shared unity sand pouring to symbolize everything that they have and will become in the future together. Afterward, the ceremony was transformed into a classic reception of ivory linens and plum florals while guests snacked on hors d'oeuvres on the patio. All of which included fried mac n' cheese, coconut crusted shrimp, candied bacon, pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, tomato bisque with mini grilled cheeses, and lastly, candied bacon deviled eggs.

Allison and Ben had a fun choreographed first dance by Nashville Wedding Dance to "Shut up and Dance" by Anchor and Bell. But, the dancing didn't stop there.

Once the DJ encouraged guests to get ready for the sparkler send-off, this fun-loving couple thoroughly enjoyed a secret last dance to Tina Turner's "The Best."

We loved helping Roku host a refined southern corporate dinner at The Bridge Building for their third annual client event.

Coupling a great menu with a chic neutral palette and pastel-hued florals by Forget Me Not Designs, the evening was a blast for all who attended.

As guests arrived, cocktail hour kicked off on the patio where the company logo was projected onto the Pedestrian Bridge. They sipped the signature cocktail of the evening, a refreshing Roku Rita, made with tequila, triple sec, agave, lime juice, with a salted rim and a lime wedge. Light bites made the rounds including pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, shrimp and grits bites, bacon-wrapped scallops, and more.

Meanwhile, inside the Cumberland space, ivory tables with light wooden chairs and soft florals created a stylish and inviting atmosphere.

As guests took their seats, Roku iced cookies in company colors by Baked in Nashville were waiting at each place setting as a take-home treat. Along with kale salads and artisan rolls with sun-dried tomato butter to start.

For dinner, guests perused the buffet with options for all and enjoyed table-side wine service. Selections included baseball cut sirloin with pearl couscous and rosemary roasted fingerling potatoes, and pine nut encrusted salmon with sautéed haricots verts.

For a sweet surprise, guests indulged in donuts from our cinnamon sugar donut station for dessert. With delectable toppings including bourbon salted caramel, chai whipped cream, and orange chocolate sauce. Yes, to all!
