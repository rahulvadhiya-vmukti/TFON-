LET THERE BE LIGHT

OUR FAVORITE UNIQUE LIGHTING IDEAS

Inspired by our latest feature in StyleBlueprint's 9 Wedding Trends We're Loving, we wanted to dive a little deeper into one of our current crushes — unique lighting.

From neon installations to basket chandeliers, lighting is an essential part of any event. It is crucial to setting the mood and adding a little or a whole lotta personality.

Stay tuned every month for what wedding trends we're currently crushing on. Scroll on for Pinterest-worthy illumination inspiration from our real weddings!

1. Geometric Pendant Lights

We love a statement piece, and geometric details never fail to amp up any wedding with a little modern glamour.

In order to create a stunning focal point over the head table, Fallyn + Aaron worked together with Lauren Marie Atkinson and Infinity's production team to create this chic, custom installation for their reception.

Photos by Davello Photography

2. Paper Lanterns

Paper lanterns are a stylish way to illuminate any space. With endless color options and a ton of placement versatility, we love how they add dimension and visual interest to any room.

Pro tip: Add string lights for added romance and ambiance!

Photos by SheHeWe & Katelyn Nicole Photography

3. Edison Lights

Known for effortlessly amping up the cool-factor, Edison lights are a surefire way to add twinkling style with a little industrial edge.

Intertwined with greenery or simply strung on their own, we love how they add a warm glow to any space.

4. Bohemian Brass Pendants

For an enchanting and free-spirited effect, we're loving these unique brass pendants.

Pair them with earthy elements, vintage rugs, and bright pops of color to add the perfect golden glow to any boho celebration.

Photo by John Myers

5. Marquee Signs

Retro signage is popping up everywhere.

Seen in home decor, weddings and other events, marquee signs are not only a great way to light up your space, but they come in super handy for stylishly labeling certain areas or activities too.

Photos by Rachel Moore, Celladora Photography, & Nyk + Cali

6. Projection Mapping

Undeniably bold and unique, projection mapping makes use of the latest innovative technology to transform any space into, well, just about anything.

How does it work, you ask? High-resolution imagery or video is seamlessly projected onto the walls and sometimes even the ceiling to transport guests to another season, city, or planet, without ever leaving the room.

Need a visual? Peep Lauren + Stephen's fall reception below where they projected golden Aspen trees onto the walls to bring their favorite season inside.

Photos by Rachel Moore

7. Custom Gobos

Whether it's on the floor, on the wall, or on the side of the Pedestrian Bridge, gobos are one of our all-time favorite ways to personalize and commemorate any event.

With endless options including company logos, custom monograms, and even hand-drawn imagery, let your imagination run wild for a truly unforgettable way to illuminate your event that will really wow your guests.

Photos by SheHeWe & Erin McCall

THE BELL TOWER

A SLICE OF HISTORICAL HEAVEN

Amidst bustling downtown Nashville, The Bell Tower sits pretty and proudly on the corner of 4th Avenue South and Korean Veterans Boulevard.

Built in 1874, The Bell Tower was one of the first churches built by freed slaves after the Civil War.

Following the abolition of slavery, many Americans reveled in newfound freedom; and this resulted in a massive missionary effort in the south.

Consequently, this effort financed new churches and schools, and new denominations were created. Including the African Methodist Episcopal (A.M.E).

In what is now SoBro and the home of The Bell Tower, was once called Black Bottom. The 5-block radius got its name from the black mud and flooding of filthy water caused in part by the Cumberland River and in part by Wilson Spring Branch, a creek that was later bricked over as a sewer.

Most of the surrounding neighborhood was destroyed after World War II, and we're proud to say that The Bell Tower is one of the last original structures of Black Bottom still standing in downtown Nashville.

On June 17, 1874, construction began on a brand new Saint Paul A.M.E. Church at the corner of what was then Cherry Street and Franklin Street.

At 3 p.m., around 1500 people gathered to witness the laying of the cornerstone. Many traveled from Murfreesboro and Shelbyville just to partake in the celebration.

A marching band joyfully marched from Cedar Street through Summer to Demonbreun and back again.

Hymns could be heard coming from the ceremonial service, and a prayer was held in the adjoining building where the church was to be built.

A few decades and more than a few new faces later, here we are!

The Bell Tower has since been transformed into a breathtaking, contemporary event venue for just about any kind of social gathering or celebration.

Formerly home to EOA Architects, the space is now full of natural southern light with a spacious open floor plan allowing for a multitude of possibilities.

The original exposed beams and downstairs stonework still remain, thus keeping the character and historic charm of the venue still intact. We even kept the original sub-flooring, which contains salvaged wood from homes destroyed during the war.

Meanwhile, a towering staircase leads to a spacious mezzanine. This bonus space is perfect for another bar or extra seating with a perfect bird's eye view of the happenings below.

Our awesome production squad is ready and able to set the tone of any event with all the latest gadgets and completely customizable lighting options. Some of our favorites include paper lanterns, string lights, a custom floral ring and our stunning collection of chandeliers.

So, envisioning cocktail hour on an air-conditioned patio in the middle of July? Done, and done. Maybe a personalized gobo to represent your brand? No sweat, we can project any monogram, logo or design onto the walls or even the floor! And the options don't stop there.

 

Pro tip: The mezzanine is the best spot in the house for photographers to perfectly capture first dances, speeches and other memorable moments from above!

And for bonus points? The Tasting Room is located within the actual bell tower and is available for hourly rental.

Impeccably designed, bottles line the walls and striking modern chandeliers hang from above. Guests are invited to sample local favorites and lesser-known distillery spirits from a selection of over 100 hand-picked bourbons, whiskey and scotch. Because when in Nashville, do as the Honky Tonkers do! That's the saying, right?

Above all, we love seeing our innovative clients and talented staff work together to create unforgettable experiences and endless memories at The Bell Tower.

From intimate rehearsal dinners to romantic weddings, to music showcases and not-so-average corporate events, no event is left behind. And we can't wait to see more of what the future holds!

Rachel and Jonathan's classic, contemporary wedding at The Bell Tower was inspired by a chic color palette of black and ivory.

It was important to them that simplicity and style be carried throughout the day. Their vision came to life with fresh white florals by Forget Me Not Designs, a candlelit aisle, and whimsical white paper lanterns.

The bride and groom chose to forgo a traditional timeline and its accompanying formalities. They opted for a mix and mingle environment for their guests instead. Although we don't always recommend this, it worked for Rachel and Jonathan, as it spoke to their easy-going personalities. Nashville Party Authority was the key ingredient here, making a single announcement to guests, encouraging them to eat, dance and be merry at their own leisure.

With tradition put aside, this refreshing and unique flow allowed guests to dance freely, explore and mingle in celebration of the happy couple.
