Kelsie and James had a chic blush wedding in late summer with romantic and playful touches at The Bell Tower.

The couple started their relationship after they met at a work conference in Cincinnati, Ohio. Once their love blossomed, James threw a surprise engagement at a brewery in Houston. To make it extra special, he made sure Kelsie’s family was there to celebrate with them right after she said yes!

As they started the planning process, they decided to celebrate with great food, an awesome bar, and a big party that their friends and family could enjoy.

Kelsie and James' ceremony featured plenty of romantic details like soft draping that accentuated a chandelier, a wooden archway adorned with white and dusty pink roses, and an aisle lined with white petals and pillar candles. Kelsie made a grand entrance using the staircase that was covered in greenery with white roses and hydrangeas dotted throughout.

After the two exchanged vows, guests enjoyed a cocktail hour with passed appetizers like fried mac and cheese and deviled eggs as the event staff flipped the main space for the reception.

For dinner, guests sat down to tables decorated in fun sequined linens with romantic elements, such as tall Monet candlesticks. They enjoyed a Southern-inspired plated dinner that featured dishes like sweet tea marinated chicken, grilled veggies, and goat cheese grits. 

We loved how all of the gilded mirror signs and table numbers with calligraphy tied the rest of their gold accent pieces together and added a touch of romance to the spaces. Plus, they went with a creative guest book idea: a gorgeous natural wood display.

And the big party that was at the top of their wish list? Kelsie and James were serious about having everyone dance the night away, so they gave their guests the chance to ditch the uncomfortable shoes and put on their ingenious party favors: flip flops!

And to add to the playful vibe, the couple had a gourmet cotton candy cart for their guests who wanted a freshly spun sweet treat.

Later in the night, the newlyweds headed over to The Bell Tower’s Tasting Room and served their guests a late-night snack of hot chicken biscuits.

Here's one dreamy summer wedding we haven't stopped blushing about.

In October of 2015, Natalie and Roydell met through a mutual friend at a local favorite, Tin Roof, and immediately hit it off. They talked and danced all night, which quickly led to a sweet first date at the movies. Roydell knew he had found his forever, so on their anniversary, he got down on one knee, and the rest was history.

This loving pair decided on The Bell Tower for their wedding ceremony and reception, decked in hues of ivory, blush, and sage. Above the main space hung a canopy of string lights with Nashville Lighting & Production's Maria chandelier glowing at center stage. At the altar, sat arrangements of soft pink roses with ivory hydrangeas by LMA Designs.

Following a sweet ceremony, our team quickly flipped the main space for dinner while guests sipped on pinot grigio alongside sweet and savory candied bacon on the patio.

As guests found their seats for the reception, it was time to introduce the bride and groom. Natalie and Roydell made a grand entrance followed by a first dance to “You Are the Best Thing” by Ray LaMontagne.

With happy hearts and teary eyes, dinner was ready to be served. Guests had the choice between bourbon-glazed salmon with grilled asparagus and rosemary roasted fingerling potatoes or a marinated steak with sautéed haricots verts and buttermilk whipped potatoes.

This dreamy blush wedding day was topped off with a long night of dancing and a send-off through a tunnel of sparklers!

Caitlin, from North Carolina, and Robinson, from Tennessee, met through a mutual friend in their current home of D.C. On their one year anniversary, Robinson decided to make it official.

While exploring in Iceland, the couple went off on a morning walk when Robinson got down on one knee. Caitlin told us there was a hidden photographer capturing every moment. Swoon!

Since Nashville was a common destination for them, they knew it was the perfect spot to tie the knot. "We weren’t sure where we wanted our venue to be," Caitlin said, "but after touring The Bridge Building, our hearts were set on the gorgeous rooftop setting and the skyline views of the city!"

For this couple, flowers and photography were of high importance to them on their wedding day. But most importantly, they wanted their guests to have a great time! They envisioned a classic wedding with light and bright shades of white, blush and neutrals.

The day began with an intimate first look. "Seeing each other for the first time before the ceremony was so special and something we’ll always remember!" Caitlin told us. "We were both so nervous until then but that made everything so much better."

On the rooftop, a dreamy circular arch and suspended floral ring from Echoes of Eden Florals set the tone for Caitlin and Robinson's romantic summer wedding.

Following the ceremony, guests headed downstairs for the reception. The evening began with delectable southern hors d’oeuvres including hot chicken sliders and shrimp and grits.

At the end of the day, Caitlin and Robinson were sent off to their getaway car through a tunnel of bright sparklers lighting up the Pedestrian Bridge.
