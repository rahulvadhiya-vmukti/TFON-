Here’s a look at Kendra and Dwight's romantic and rustic fall wedding day at The Bell Tower. A day filled with a lot of love and sweet, personalized details. 

This couple met in Nashville, working at Johnny Rockets in Opry Mills. After some persuasion, Dwight grew on Kendra and they started dating.

After four years, they got engaged in Cincinnati, Ohio, while on a skiing trip, and Dwight popped the question at a restaurant they went to!

When they started planning their wedding, they knew they wanted a fall, rustic-feeling wedding. This ultimately led them to The Bell Tower. It was our history and cozy, rustic vibe that sealed the deal for Kendra and Dwight. Added bonus? A select number of mahogany chiavari chairs, round tables, china, silverware, and glassware are included in our venue rental.

Kendra looked stunning in her sweetheart gown with lace and bead detailing from Eddy K Bridal. The bridesmaids wore burgundy dresses, while the bridesman and groomsmen wore coordinating black suits with burgundy ties. The groom stood out from his best men with an embossed suit jacket that had a satin lapel.

Kendra and Dwight held their ceremony between our tall, arched windows. The aisle led to  a beautiful hexagonal arch decorated with greenery and white, red, and soft pink flowers by Creations by Debbie. Cascading string lights were hung overhead from our original wood beams that created a warm glow against the exposed wood, the perfect touch for a romantic fall wedding. 

The space was then transformed for the reception, using their ceremony arch as the backdrop to their sweetheart table! The tables were decorated with white linens, burgundy napkins, and floral centerpieces of greenery with red roses.

The couple included several personalized touches throughout the day, such as a black and white photo wall to showcase their relationship, a framed quote used as a guestbook, and a signature cocktail—the Green Hawaiian.

Hilary and Zach celebrated their love with a purple fall wedding ceremony and reception at The Bell Tower.

These two lovebirds were introduced by mutual friends in college at the University of Pittsburgh. After moving to Nashville, they took a road trip back to where it all started, and in front of the Cathedral of Learning, Zach got down on one knee.

When it came time to choose a venue, Hilary and Zach told us, "We fell in love with the entire vibe— the high ceiling, brick, and wood" of The Bell Tower. Not to mention, Zach "really loved the whiskey room."

They wanted their special day to be full of good food and dancing with those they love most. And we think we accomplished just that!

Between the arched windows with a string light backdrop and romantic floral arrangements framing the altar, Hilary and Zach tied the knot. In addition to exchanging vows, these two incorporated a wine box ceremony. A bottle of wine is placed in a case that the couple sealed together to be opened at a later date. How fun!

After the ceremony, guests headed to the patio to enjoy some delectable hors d'oeuvres. Selections included pepper jelly pimento cheese bruschetta, fried green tomatoes, bleu cheese stuffed bacon-wrapped dates, and hot chicken sliders, as well as pear and feta hushpuppies.

Meanwhile, our team transformed the main space for dinner with gray linens, florals, and purple color washed walls.

Hilary and Zach decided on a buffet dinner service for their guests and, instead of having a traditional wedding cake, they opted for a Pittsburgh cookie table. With 10 different flavors, there was no way you could choose just one!

Music and dancing were a top priority for this pair, so, of course, they danced the rest of the night away. Scroll on for more details from this purple fall wedding!
