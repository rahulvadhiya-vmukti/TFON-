Lauren and Michael celebrated their marriage with an intimate boho wedding on The Bridge Building rooftop, full of dramatic floral arrangements and the perfect moody ambiance. 

This sweet pair met through mutual friends in Knoxville, Tennessee. Michael later proposed during a photoshoot while the couple vacationed in Cancun. How romantic!

Lauren and Michael wanted a special day that focused on the meaning of their vows with all the trendy wedding details. Safe to say I think we accomplished it!

When the day finally arrived, Lauren stunned in a long sleeve lace gown with a layered skirt and a loosely curled do. Her gals rocked mix-and-match neutral dresses and held bouquets of ivory and soft pink blooms. 

For the ceremony, our team of coordinators, Forget Me Not Designs, and Nashville Lighting & Production worked their magic to set the tone with moody lighting, candles lining the aisle, and pampas grass arrangements at the altar creating the most romantic, boho-inspired ambiance.  

After Lauren and Michael said their vows, guests sipped on pinot noir as our staff flipped the rooftop into an intimate reception with the same trendy boho decor and candle lighting and prepared the food stations for a delicious dinner, all prepared in-house. 

Once guests found their seats, Lauren and Michael shared their first dance to “You Are So Beautiful” by Joe Cocker— and the rest is history!

Brennan and Kyle said "I do" surrounded by a small group of family and friends last fall at The Bridge Building. 

Brennan, a Nashvillian, met Mississippi native Kyle in Music City during a casual volleyball game. The pair started dating, and then Kyle popped the question in their home with a bundle of blooms and their beloved dogs. 

When the pair started planning their big day, they decided they wanted a relaxed micro wedding filled with good food, and, above all, they wanted everyone to have a good time. 

Their October wedding day began with a sweet rooftop ceremony under white drapery and glowing string lights. They wanted to keep it intimate, so they decided to forego wedding parties in order to focus on enjoying time with each of their guests. 

Florals were a very important part of their decor. The couple exchanged vows in front of a wooden arch adorned with a mix of greenery and pink and burgundy blooms that was perfectly coordinated to match Brennan’s bridal bouquet of dahlias, ranunculus, and champagne roses. 

After the ceremony, guests enjoyed a few hors d'oeuvres and relaxed during the cocktail hour until it was time for the seated dinner. The tables were set with white linens, and the tent was lit with soft purple hues. 

Guests filled their stomachs with Creole shrimp and grits, mustard-glazed chicken with carrots and whipped potatoes, and a yummy ribeye with grilled asparagus and Swiss potato gratin. 

Following dinner, the DJ  set the tone for the rest of the night, and guests danced the night away!

Alyssa and Stewart celebrated their nuptials with a romantic blush wedding in July on the rooftop of The Bridge Building.

This sweet pair met through mutual friends. Later on, Stewart surprised Alyssa with the rose gold engagement ring of her dreams in the comfort of their own home accompanied by wine and sushi.

As a Nashville loving duo, Alyssa and Stewart knew they wanted a wedding ceremony with a great view of the city.

Alyssa told us, "When we visited The Bridge Building, we knew we’d found the perfect spot and couldn’t imagine getting married anywhere else."

The rooftop was decorated with soft blush rose petals and candles with a cascading crystal chandelier that took center stage. While Alyssa changed into her glamorous reception dress following an intimate ceremony, our team quickly turned the rooftop into a lovely reception.

Guests were greeted at their tables by a balsamic grape and apple salad followed by maple bourbon baked beans, cheddar cheese mashed potatoes, four cheese macaroni, and a choice of glazed pulled pork or chicken cordon bleu.

Afterward, it was time for Alyssa and Stewart to cut the cake, which was made by the same baker who did Alyssa's parents' wedding. That wasn't the only personal touch to her day, though, as Alyssa also carried with her a ring her father gifted her when he married her mother. She was two at the time and held onto the keepsake all these years! So sweet!

Before sending the couple off, guests got to take home white chocolate covered Oreos, rice crispy treats, boxed apple pie flavored caramel apples, and koozies featuring the Nashville skyline designed by The Vinyl Vixen-McEwen.

]]> ERIN + RYAN’S SIMPLE, ELEGANT WEDDING https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/erin-ryan-simple-elegant-wedding/ Mon, 16 Dec 2019 21:40:37 +0000 https://infinityhospitalitygroup.com/?p=23218 A swoon-worthy proposal and an elegant rooftop wedding at The Bridge Building.

Erin and Ryan celebrated their love for each other with a simple, elegant wedding on the rooftop of The Bridge Building.

This sweet couple met through their best friends. "Ryan was always invited along on their outings, but to not have him feel like a third wheel I was invited to tag along," Erin told us. "Took us over a year to figure out we were meant to be together, but here we are!"

Later on, Ryan popped the question lakeside with all of their family and friends in on it. The night began with a going away dinner between Erin and her dad. Erin and Ryan were moving to southwest Florida, and she wanted one last dinner with her dad. "After dinner, my dad had me walk to look at something by the lake," Erin said. "One of my closest friends was standing there with a love note from Ryan."

She was directed to walk around the lake, and every 50 yards or so was another friend or family member holding a love note. As Erin continued to walk around the lake, the group of loved ones behind her kept growing. Ryan even flew in some people from out of state.

Finally, about halfway around the lake, there was Ryan waiting to get down on one knee. Swoon!

While looking for a venue, Erin told us, "we wanted something with a view of the city since the majority of our guests were from out of town and had never been to Nashville."

"The Bridge Building was an easy choice," she continued. "With Infinity being all inclusive we didn’t have to worry about keeping up with a bunch of different vendors."

And a stress-free wedding day is exactly what they got!

Beginning with a simple, elegant wedding ceremony on the rooftop, Erin and Ryan stuck with tradition and opted out of a first look. Which led to their most memorable moment of the day, walking down the aisle.

After the two exchanged vows, guests made their way to the bar for a drink or two while our team quickly flipped the rooftop for dinner.

Following dinner and drinks, guests danced the night away with the Nashville skyline as their backdrop. Later on, Erin and Ryan closed out the night with one last private dance on the rooftop.
