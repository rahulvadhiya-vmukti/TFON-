Emily and Will celebrated their nuptials with a class fall wedding ceremony at Christ The King Catholic Church followed by a fun reception at The Bridge Building in downtown Nashville.

These two first met on the Emory & Henry College campus in southwest Virginia. They both played soccer for the college and met during the preseason their freshman year. A friendship quickly began, but after a few late-night talks and breakfast dates, they grew much closer. Three years later, Will got down on one knee and asked Emily to be his forever on the beloved Nashville Pedestrian Bridge.

Since Nashville was near and dear to their hearts, they knew it was the perfect place to continue this next chapter of their lives.

Their wedding day began with a beautiful and traditional ceremony at Christ The King Catholic Church, before heading downtown for the reception.

Emily had her gals dressed in deep burgundy gowns with bouquets full of ivory florals and sprigs of greenery. In addition, the groomsmen wore classic black suits, while Will went with a patterned gray coat and a velvet bowtie.

When guests arrived at The Bridge Building, white and black linens, gold chargers, and little ivory pumpkins dressed up the main space in subtle fall style. Passed hors d'oeuvres of candied bacon, fried green tomatoes, margarita flatbread, and tomato bruschetta greeted them upon arrival. 

Shortly after, guests took their seats to a plated dinner with the choice of sirloin steak or rosemary chicken. Entrees were dressed with delicious sides like truffle cheddar crusted brussel sprouts, garlic mashed potatoes and southern style green beans. 

"All of Me" by John Legend was Emily and Will's pick for their first dance. "When the song first came out Will told me that the song reminded him of us," Emily told us. "That memory stuck with us years later." Swoon!

Amid the excitement and rush of the evening, the couple stepped outside just in time to catch the sunset. "The sun was setting behind the city, and it was the most beautiful sunset," Emily said. "So many of our guests were sitting outside enjoying conversation and it was a beautiful, calm moment."

After a night of dancing and laughs, guests sent Emily and Will off to their 1950's Rolls Royce with bubbles.

FALLING IN LOVE

ALL OVER AGAIN WITH THESE FALL WEDDING ALTARS & AISLES

From understated glamor to whimsical greenery, we've rounded up our top five favorite wedding ceremony altars and aisles for fall. Scroll through for inspiration from real weddings at The Bell Tower and The Bridge Building.

Meanwhile, we'll be here, sipping pumpkin spice everything, patiently waiting for cooler temps.

1. Whimsical.

Anna and Zach's whimsical fall wedding at The Bell Tower was vibrant and playful. Wild branches and florals from Enchanted Florist wrapped around a rustic arbor while petals and painted pumpkins lined the aisle.

Photos by James Anderson

2. Bohemian.

Meghan and Kevin's modern burgundy wedding ceremony at The Bridge Building was equal parts organic and romantic. A wispy greenery backdrop paired with matte black lanterns and greenery filled urns from our friendor PS Designs set the tone for this fall fête.

Photos by CK Photo

3. Understated Glamor.

Jessica and Josh opted for a single chandelier to illuminate their glamorous, Gatsby-inspired ceremony with semi-circular seating at The Bell Tower. Proving that there truly is beauty in simplicity!

Love this look? We have an extensive lighting collection with various chandeliers in all shapes and sizes to choose from so you can keep calm and sparkle on.

Photos by Chelsea Somohano

4. Greenery.

Natalee and Marc incorporated their cool and eclectic style into their boho-inspired fall wedding at The Bridge Building. with modern, yet whimsical greenery.

Organic arrangements of magnolia leaves and wild greenery from Mansion & Marsh flanked the altar while sprigs of greenery marked the aisle.

Want more fall floral inspo from Natalee + Marc? Head to their blog post for her swoon-worthy bouquet.

Photos by Sarah Swain Photography

5. Amber Ambiance.

Sometimes all it takes is a little lighting to set the mood. Natalie and Tyler transformed The Bell Tower with a string light backdrop, warm amber uplighting, and candlelight for their elegant fall wedding.

Photos by Krista Lee Photography
