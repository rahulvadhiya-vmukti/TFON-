Emmy and Mike celebrated their marriage with a colorful, festival-inspired wedding at The Bridge Building full of eclectic retro details, touches of gold, and a sunset ambiance. 

The pair met at Vanderbilt University on the Crew Team and, after graduation, dated long distance while Mike attended med school in his home state of Florida and Emmy went to grad school for law and psychology in Nebraska. Power couple, if we do say so ourselves!

After 7 years of dating and lots of FaceTime calls, Emmy decided it was time to pop the question one July afternoon over another virtual date. 

The soon-to-be newlyweds began their fall wedding day getting ready in the lounge areas of the Bridge Building before their outdoor ceremony on the riverfront patio. Emmy was dressed in a beaded dress with a high ponytail, and a bouquet of bright florals consisting of pink, purple, orange, and more. 

Inspired by Emmy's love of music festivals, their Jewish ceremony was set facing the pedestrian bridge with oriental rugs and bohemian gold lanterns lining the aisle, and a simple copper chuppah with asymmetrical floral arrangements of orange and pink blooms at the altar. String lights hung above with colorful fringe tassels and the sun began to set over the Nashville skyline. 

Once the newlyweds said “I do,” guests enjoyed a crisp glass of wine, live music, and boho lounge seating areas before heading into the Cumberland space for a colorful reception. 

For the reception, the tables were set with blue, ivory, and dusty rose linens, gold vases, disco ball installs, and colorful taper candles inside hurricane vases, along with bright floral arrangements featuring shades of orange and pink and lots of greenery.

Guests enjoyed a stationed dinner of classic BYO sliders, Mexican street corn, a selection of salads, and a TN food truck with late night bites including gourmet french fries and a trio of quesadillas. 

Emmy and Mike shared a sweet first dance after cutting one of their three delicious and uniquely decorated cakes before everyone made their way to the rooftop and celebrated the night away!

Taylor and Baron's stunning wedding took place on a gorgeous spring day at The Bridge Building.

The couple desired a clean, classic, and minimal look with a great atmosphere and a no-stress policy.

A subtle yet effortlessly romantic color palette of dusty rose and gray set the tone. Silver chiavari chairs from our friends at Liberty Party Rental added timeless elegance and a hint of glam.

Photography was one of Taylor and Baron's main priorities and Cannon Weddings really delivered. The couple's first look on the riverfront with the perfect view of the Nashville skyline was beautifully captured.

Taylor and Baron exchanged vows on the riverfront. Witnessing all of their emotion once they were pronounced husband and wife was beautiful, touching, and my favorite moment of the day.

Following the ceremony, dinner was served in the main space. Guests enjoyed hot chicken and herb roasted pork loin with four cheese macaroni, cheddar cheese and chive mashed potatoes, and grilled veggies.

After one epic dance party on the rooftop, guests sent the couple off with a gorgeous sparkler exit on the Pedestrian Bridge.

Julia Ann and Brady's elegant ceremony and reception at The Bridge Building was inspired by 1940s old Hollywood glamour.

From dramatic draping to gold chiavari chairs to gatsby chandeliers, the industrial space radiated style and glamour. A classic palette of ivory and black was the perfect fit for this vintage-inspired day. Julia Ann designed and arranged her own flowers, which provided a pop of color on each table.

Following a beautiful riverfront ceremony, guests delighted in cocktail hour before taking their seats for a plated dinner. They chose from two delicious entrees, ginger soy salmon with grilled asparagus and sage & white cheddar mashed potatoes or chicken roulade with four cheese macaroni and sautéed haricots vert.

After their intimate evening of dinner and dancing, the couple took off in true old Hollywood style in a classic Packard sedan.

It was a gorgeous spring day for a gorgeous couple. An southern vintage wedding where no detail was left to chance.

The Bridge Building was filled with vintage furniture from Southern Events to encapsulate their elegant theme. Hints of vintage lace and accents of gray, pink, and navy surrounded the reception and ceremony.

As guests entered, they were offered Erin & Seth's signature cocktail, the Blackberry Sparkler. It was a refreshing mix of blackberry liqueur and sparkling wine topped with a fresh blackberry. As a guest book, the couple used their favorite records. Guests wrote their sweet notes and congratulations in silver and gold on the record sleeves.

The couple's ceremony on the riverfront overlooking the gorgeous skyline of Nashville was an absolute stunner.

Following the ceremony, guests enjoyed cocktail hour and delighted in small bites including bacon wrapped scallops, maple bacon chicken skewers, and mini pulled pork biscuit sliders.

After mingling, family and friends sat down for a plated dinner. The meal started with a balsamic grape and apple salad. Guests indulged in peppercorn filet and prawn with grilled asparagus and garlic parmesan mashed potatoes as the main course.
