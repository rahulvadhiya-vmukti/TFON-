The Bell Tower became the enchanting backdrop for Carley and Turner’s September wedding day. Combining timeless elegance with a few modern trends, their wedding was a stunning blend of bold and bright florals and classic black-and-white decor. 

The couple told us they “immediately fell in love with The Bell Tower when we first visited the venue. It was exactly what we had imagined and we loved that it can accommodate a large group while still feeling very intimate.”

These Nashville natives have known each other since they were kids. Carley and Turner started dating their sophomore year at the University of Tennessee where they continued to run in the same circles. Turner proposed at the Richland Country Club, where they later ended up having their rehearsal dinner! 

Carley and Turner’s vision for their wedding decor was a seamless fusion of classic and contemporary elements. The venue was adorned with striking black and white decor, creating a sophisticated and timeless ambiance. The florals added a burst of vibrant colors to the scene. Bold bouquets of roses, peonies, and dahlias in shades of red, pink, and orange brought warmth and energy to the venue.

Carley stunned in a strapless gown from Adorn Nashville with trendy full sleeve lace gloves, while her best gals dressed to impress in mismatched black bridesmaid dresses. Turner looked classically dapper in a black tuxedo from Street Tuxedo Nashville with groomsmen to match.

The couple exchanged vows before a beautifully adorned floral installation, soft white draping, and classic crystal chandeliers hanging above. 

After saying their “I dos,” Carley said one her favorite moments of the day was walking down the mezzanine staircase into their reception as a newly-married couple. Black and white linens adorned each table, providing a chic and sophisticated backdrop for the vibrant floral centerpieces. The dance floor was alive with energy as friends and family joined the newlyweds in celebrating their love. Good food, music, and being with all those they love was the focal point of importance to this couple when it came to planning their day— and we have to say they achieved just that!

As the couple danced the night away, surrounded by the love and joy of their closest friends and family, it was clear that their wedding day was not just an event but a beautiful beginning to a lifetime of happiness together!

Deysi and Michael celebrated their love with an enchanting garden wedding at the Estate at Cherokee Dock. With its picturesque landscapes, grand mansion, and lush gardens, the Estate provided the perfect backdrop for their magical day.

Deysi, originally from Brazil, and Michael, from New Jersey, first met at work when Deysi moved to New Jersey. Starting off as just friends, Michael showed Deysi the ropes when she made the big move. Their love grew slowly but surely, and after another move to Nashville, these two were ready to tie the knot.

With most of their guests coming from out of town, and many from Brazil, they wanted to make sure it was a smooth and enjoyable experience for all. It was important that the food, drinks, and atmosphere were top tier— and with us, that’s a promise! The Estate was the perfect backdrop for their guests to celebrate with its breathtaking views of Old Hickory Lake and sprawling lawns. A truly memorable and magical experience.

Deysi and Michael got ready with their own wedding parties in the mansion’s luxury bridal suite and nearby Stable Loft  before walking the aisle to say “I do” on the Lakeside Lawn. The lakeside ceremony was set with arrangements of vibrant blooms and lush greenery at the altar with a flower petal aisle.

Afterwards, guests sipped on glasses of frozé with a selection of passed apps during the outdoor cocktail hour in the Serenity Gardens, including Bloody Mary oysters, hot chicken biscuits, ahi tuna in a crispy wonton, arancini, polenta cakes with goat cheese, and Key West crab cakes. Lounge areas were also set to create a comfortable environment for guests to mingle with string lights for a fairytale ambiance. 

For their reception inside the Lakeview Event Center, Deysi and Michael blew their guests away with the most stunning and enchanting indoor garden design. The room was draped in forest green velvet with strings of fairy lights across the whole ceiling, while tables were set with soft white linens and acrylic ghost chairs. Centerpieces included antique candelabras wrapped in greenery and full-size faux trees with floating glass bulbs, individually hung white flowers, tea lights, and touches of moss. It was like a fairy tale brought to life!

For dinner, we helped Deysi and Michael curate a culinary experience that embraced Nashville’s renowned food scene. The menu featured a fusion of Southern flavors and personal favorites with a selection of four entrees—lemon almondine trout, grilled lamb chop with a tomato demi-glace, chicken cordon bleu, and rigatoni primavera.

During the reception, live musicians played celebratory tunes to kick off the dance floor  and encourage loved ones to join the couple in the fun. And after a late night dessert display of French toast bread pudding, mini cheesecake bites, cake cutting, and more, Deysi and Michael ended their night with a jump in the pool!

When it came to the big day, Brian and Gage wanted to feel like they were guests at their own wedding. With our team of professionals making sure that everything ran smoothly, we can proudly say we accomplished just that!  

The ceremony was held in the Cumberland space of The Bridge Building with Edison bulb lighting, a floral ring with lots of greenery and pink roses, and a view of Downtown Nashville. Once plates were clean and the cake was cut, the night ended with lots of fun and a sweet first dance to Blake Shelton’s “Nobody but You.” Gage and Brian were sent away under the pedestrian bridge, and the rest is history!
